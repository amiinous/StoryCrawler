The Love Questions - Episode 3

Sera is waiting outside the karaoke bar…

When some guys start hassling her.

They tease her about waiting all alone and goad her to go home with them.

Suddenly, Christian comes running out from inside the bar.

Christian

Hey. HEY! You guys think it’s okay to talk to a girl like that?

Sera

Christian?

Christian

You creeps better get OUT of here.

Sera

Christian! Wow, you look JUST like your profile.

Christian

Sera, are you okay?

Sera

I’m fine.

Sera

They’re just jerks.

Christian

I hate guys like that.

Christian

Guys who say dirty stuff to girls.

Sera

It’s part of being in the big city —

Sera

I’ve handled myself okay so far.

Christian

Some first meeting, huh?

Sera

Lucky you look like your profile picture.

Sera

Otherwise I might have thought you were some crazy weirdo.

Christian

You look like your picture too —

Christian

Totally beautiful.

Sera

Oh, stop.

Sera

I’m just your average science girl.

Christian

You really think so?

Christian

Then why is every guy on the street staring at you?

Sera

Because this dress is bright yellow.

Christian

Didn’t you get my message?

Sera

What message?

Christian

To meet me inside.

Christian

I got us a booth.

Sera

That message didn’t come through.

Sera

I assumed you were late.

Christian

I got here an hour early.

Sera

That’s sweet.

Christian

Yeah. Didn’t want to miss you.

Christian

You wanna come up?

Sera

Sure.

Christian

This way — it’s on the first floor.

Sera

Thank goodness! If you’d have made me walk five flights of stairs…

Sera

I’d have killed you.

Christian

After you, Sera.

Sera

Wow, you’re holding the door for me!

Sera

Such a gentleman.

Christian

Always have been.

Sera

Hey — this place is amazing.

Sera

The windows are HUGE.

Sera

I’ve never seen so much glass in one place.

Christian

All the better to see the view, right?

Sera

You can say that again.

Sera

Times Square looks fantastic from this height.

Christian

Here — I reserved us a table.

Sera

Nice booth.

Christian

Cool, huh?

Sera

Yeah, great view.

Sera

You must have been here REALLY early to get this one.

Christian

Yeah, I’ve been here a while.

Sera

Ooh, and the cocktails look FUN!

Christian

You want one?

Sera

Sure. Wait —

Sera

What’s the server wheeling over?

Christian

A karaoke screen.

Sera

With TWO microphones?

Christian

I’ll sing the first song.

Sera

Oh yes, you will.

Sera

You’ll sing ALL the songs.

Christian

So what kind of music do you like?

Sera

I’m not sure.

Christian

How can you be not sure?

Sera

I haven’t thought about music in a while.

Sera

I guess I like those old-fashioned love songs from the 1950s.

Sera

Elvis Presley.

Sera

Nat King Cole.

Sera

I really love Unchained Melody by the Righteous Brothers.

Christian

No kidding?!

But the excitement in Christian’s face disappears…

When he sees the sadness in Sera’s eyes

He puts his hand on her arm.

Christian

You okay?

Sera

Yes, just…

Sera

Thinking about that song is hard.

Sera

My dad loved it.

Sera

It makes me remember when we were all together.

Christian

You know, it makes me think of my brother.

Christian

He used to play it over and over.

A few minutes pass.

Sera

Look at us — lost in our memories.

Christian

It’s good to sit in silence sometimes.

Sera

I guess I’d have to agree with that.

Christian

You know what?

Christian

Maybe Unchained Melody should be OUR song instead.

Sera

Our song?

Christian

The words kind of fit with us, don’t you think?

Sera

What do you mean?

Christian

Wanting to be together.

Christian

Having to wait.

Christian

Time going so slowly.

Christian

That’s how I’ve felt this entire week.

Christian

Hey, don’t go all shy on me.

Sera

So are you going to sing me a song, rock star?

Christian

Sure.

Christian

Here comes the waiter.

Christian

Hey man? Can you hook up some Elvis Presley?

Christian

Let me just check this mic.

Christian

Sera, Sera, one, two three.

Sera

What’s the song?

Sera

I don’t recognize the intro.

Christian

Love Me Tender.

Christian

Okay — here come the lyrics.

Christian sings ‘Love Me Tender.’

For the first time, Sera lets the words overwhelm her —

She feels tears prickle in her eyes.

Sera

Wow, nice singing. You’re really good. Your voice is…

Sera

I dunno, sort of soulful.

Sera

You put so much feeling into it.

Christian

You liked it?

Sera

I loved it.

Christian

The song or me singing?

Sera

Both.

Sera

You’re a REALLY good singer.

Christian

Thanks. I guess I’ve had a lot of practice.

Christian

My band has been together for a while.

Sera

So what are you going to sing next?

Christian

I reckon it’s time for a duet.

Sera

Oh no —

Sera

I’d prefer another cocktail.

Sera

And the next question.

Christian

The next question?

Christian

Okay, sure. Here it goes —

Christian

Is there something you’ve always wanted to do, but have never done?

Sera

I need my next cocktail before I answer that one.

Sera

And a shot.

Christian

Waiter? Two more cocktails and two shots of tequila.

Sera

Thanks. Nicely done.

Sera

So tell me what YOU’VE always wanted to do first.

Christian

Easy.

Christian

Have sex on a plane.

Sera

CHRISTIAN!

Christian

Okay, okay…

Christian

I’ve always wanted to climb Mount Washington.

Sera

Wow — really?

Sera

That’s some serious mountain climbing.

Christian

Here’s your shot.

Sera

Shall we do them together?

Christian

Great idea. One, two three…GO!

Sera

Ugh! That was a STRONG shot!

Christian

I thought it tasted pretty good.

Sera

Okay — I need another one if I’m gonna sing.

Christian

No more shots for you.

Christian

I don’t want to be accused of getting you drunk.

Sera

So why do you want to climb Mount Washington?

Christian

I’m not answering that yet. Not until…

Christian

We sing.

Christian

Unchained Melody.

Christian

Together.

Sera

Christian…

Christian

Drink your cocktail and grab the mike.

Christian

Come on, science girl.

Christian

It can’t be any scarier than that apartment fire.

Christian

Come on! Take the mic.

Sera

Okay, fine.

Sera

Start the music.

Christian

Get ready — it’s a short intro.

Sera

Wait.

Sera

WAIT!

Sera

I can’t do this.

Christian

You can.

Christian

Trust me.

Christian

And trust yourself.

Christian

I’ll start singing first.

Sera

How can I sing with you?

Sera

You’re too good!

Christian starts singing Unchained Melody.

Sera watches him, captivated…

And then the pair looks into each other’s eyes.

Christian

Come on…join in for the chorus.

Sera

Okay. Here goes.

Sera joins in, stumbling over the words at first.

She laughs nervously, but then she meets Christian’s gaze again.

And the music takes over — the pair sings together.

Christian

You have an amazing smile.

Sera

I did it! I sang the whole song!

Christian

I loved hearing you do it.

Sera

I was terrible.

Christian

No, you sounded beautiful.

Christian

You just need to let go more.

Sera

Let go MORE?

Sera

I just sang —

Sera

And I never sing.

Christian

I’m impressed — you conquered your fears!

Sera

All it took was two cocktails and a shot of tequila…

Sera

Anyway, weren’t you going to tell me why you want to climb Mount Washington?

Christian

Yeah. It’s because of my brother, the one who died five years ago.

Sera

Oh…I don’t want to make you talk about him if you don’t want to.

Christian

No, I want to. I like remembering him.

Christian

He wanted to join the army…

Christian

But they wouldn’t let him in.

Christian

So he decided to prove he was manly enough, then reapply.

Christian

He and a buddy climbed Mount Washington.

Sera

Wow.

Christian

There was a bad storm.

Christian

And Leo got swept up in it.

Sera

Oh no.

Christian

They never found his body.

Christian

But I know he’s up there somewhere.

Sera

Your poor family.

Christian

I’ve always wanted to climb that mountain.

Christian

Reach the summit.

Christian

And say goodbye.

Sera

Christian, I’m so sorry.

Sera

Here — give me your hand.

Sera looks Christian in the eye. Below their gaze…

Their hands are clasped together.

Sera

Listen, I hope you get to say your goodbye.

Christian

If I tell more sad stories, will you keep holding my hand?

Sera

Don’t joke.

Christian

Yeah, I guess it’s not all that funny.

Christian

I miss him every day.

Christian

Your turn now.

Christian

What have you always wanted to do, but have never done?

Sera

It’s sort of embarrassing.

Christian

Embarrassing is better than heart-breaking.

Sera

You’re right.

Christian

So tell me.

Sera

Okay…
