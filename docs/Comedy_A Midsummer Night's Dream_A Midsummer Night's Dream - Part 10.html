A Midsummer Night's Dream - Part 10

Enter PUCK behind

PUCK

What hempen home-spuns have we swaggering here,

PUCK

So near the cradle of the fairy queen?

PUCK

What, a play toward! I'll be an auditor;

PUCK

An actor too, perhaps, if I see cause.

QUINCE

Speak, Pyramus. Thisby, stand forth.

BOTTOM

Thisby, the flowers of odious savours sweet,--

QUINCE

Odours, odours.

BOTTOM

--odours savours sweet:

BOTTOM

So hath thy breath, my dearest Thisby dear.

BOTTOM

But hark, a voice! stay thou but here awhile,

BOTTOM

And by and by I will to thee appear.

Exit

PUCK

A stranger Pyramus than e'er played here.

Exit

FLUTE

Must I speak now?

QUINCE

Ay, marry, must you; for you must understand he goes

QUINCE

but to see a noise that he heard, and is to come again.

FLUTE

Most radiant Pyramus, most lily-white of hue,

FLUTE

Of colour like the red rose on triumphant brier,

FLUTE

Most brisky juvenal and eke most lovely Jew,

FLUTE

As true as truest horse that yet would never tire,

FLUTE

I'll meet thee, Pyramus, at Ninny's tomb.

QUINCE

'Ninus' tomb,' man: why, you must not speak that

QUINCE

yet; that you answer to [Person 25]: you speak all your

Pyramus

[PERSON 8]: yet; that you answer to you speak all your

QUINCE

part at once, cues and all Pyramus enter: your cue

QUINCE

is past; it is, 'never tire.'

FLUTE

O,--As true as truest horse, that yet would

FLUTE

never tire.

Re-enter PUCK, and BOTTOM with an ass's head

BOTTOM

If I were fair, Thisby, I were only thine.

QUINCE

O monstrous! O strange! we are haunted. Pray,

QUINCE

masters! fly, masters! Help!

Exeunt QUINCE, SNUG, FLUTE, SNOUT, and STARVELING

PUCK

I'll follow you, I'll lead you about a round,

PUCK

Through bog, through bush, through brake, through brier:

PUCK

Sometime a horse I'll be, sometime a hound,

PUCK

A hog, a headless bear, sometime a fire;

PUCK

And neigh, and bark, and grunt, and roar, and burn,

PUCK

Like horse, hound, hog, bear, fire, at every turn.

Exit

BOTTOM

Why do they run away? this is a knavery of them to

BOTTOM

make me afeard.

Re-enter SNOUT

SNOUT

O Bottom, thou art changed! what do I see on thee?

BOTTOM

What do you see? you see an asshead of your own, do

BOTTOM

you?

Exit SNOUT

Re-enter QUINCE

QUINCE

Bless thee, Bottom! bless thee! thou art

QUINCE

translated.

Exit

BOTTOM

I see their knavery: this is to make an ass of me;

BOTTOM

to fright me, if they could. But I will not stir

BOTTOM

from this place, do what they can: I will walk up

BOTTOM

and down here, and I will sing, that they shall hear

BOTTOM

I am not afraid.

Sings

BOTTOM

The ousel cock so black of hue,

BOTTOM

With orange-tawny bill,

BOTTOM

The throstle with his note so true,

BOTTOM

The wren with little quill,--

TITANIA

What angel wakes me from my flowery bed?

BOTTOM

The finch, the sparrow and the lark,

BOTTOM

The plain-song cuckoo gray,

BOTTOM

Whose note full many a man doth mark,

BOTTOM

And dares not answer nay;--

BOTTOM

for, indeed, who would set his wit to so foolish

BOTTOM

a bird? who would give a bird the lie, though he cry

BOTTOM

'cuckoo' never so?

TITANIA

I pray thee, gentle mortal, sing again:

TITANIA

Mine ear is much enamour'd of thy note;

TITANIA

So is mine eye enthralled to thy shape;

TITANIA

And thy fair virtue's force perforce doth move me

TITANIA

On the first view to say, to swear, I love thee.

BOTTOM

Methinks, mistress, you should have little reason

BOTTOM

for that: and yet, to say the truth, reason and

BOTTOM

love keep little company together now-a-days; the

BOTTOM

more the pity that some honest neighbours will not

BOTTOM

make them friends. Nay, I can gleek upon occasion.

TITANIA

Thou art as wise as thou art beautiful.

BOTTOM

Not so, neither: but if I had wit enough to get out

BOTTOM

of this wood, I have enough to serve mine own turn.

TITANIA

Out of this wood do not desire to go:

TITANIA

Thou shalt remain here, whether thou wilt or no.

TITANIA

I am a spirit of no common rate;

TITANIA

The summer still doth tend upon my state;

TITANIA

And I do love thee: therefore, go with me;

TITANIA

I'll give thee fairies to attend on thee,

TITANIA

And they shall fetch thee jewels from the deep,

TITANIA

And sing while thou on pressed flowers dost sleep;

TITANIA

And I will purge thy mortal grossness so

TITANIA

That thou shalt like an airy spirit go.

TITANIA

Peaseblossom! Cobweb! Moth! and Mustardseed!

Enter PEASEBLOSSOM, COBWEB, MOTH, and MUSTARDSEED

PEASEBLOSSOM

Ready.

COBWEB

And I.

MOTH

And I.

MUSTARDSEED

And I.

ALL

Where shall we go?

TITANIA

Be kind and courteous to this gentleman;

TITANIA

Hop in his walks and gambol in his eyes;

TITANIA

Feed him with apricocks and dewberries,

TITANIA

With purple grapes, green figs, and mulberries;

TITANIA

The honey-bags steal from the humble-bees,

TITANIA

And for night-tapers crop their waxen thighs

TITANIA

And light them at the fiery glow-worm's eyes,

TITANIA

To have my love to bed and to arise;

TITANIA

And pluck the wings from Painted butterflies

TITANIA

To fan the moonbeams from his sleeping eyes:

TITANIA

Nod to him, elves, and do him courtesies.

PEASEBLOSSOM

Hail, mortal!

COBWEB

Hail!

MOTH

Hail!

MUSTARDSEED

Hail!

BOTTOM

I cry your worship's mercy, heartily: I beseech your

BOTTOM

worship's name.

COBWEB

Cobweb.

BOTTOM

I shall desire you of more acquaintance, good Master

BOTTOM

[Person 19]: if I cut my finger, I shall make bold with

COBWEB

[PERSON 9]: if I cut my finger, I shall make bold with

BOTTOM

you. Your name, honest gentleman?

PEASEBLOSSOM

Peaseblossom.

BOTTOM

I pray you, commend me to Mistress Squash, your

BOTTOM

mother, and to Master Peascod, your father. Good

BOTTOM

Master Peaseblossom, I shall desire you of more

BOTTOM

acquaintance too. Your name, I beseech you, sir?

MUSTARDSEED

Mustardseed.

BOTTOM

Good Master Mustardseed, I know your patience well:

BOTTOM

that same cowardly, giant-like ox-beef hath

BOTTOM

devoured many a gentleman of your house: I promise

BOTTOM

you your kindred had made my eyes water ere now. I

BOTTOM

desire your more acquaintance, good Master

BOTTOM

Mustardseed.

TITANIA

Come, wait upon him; lead him to my bower.

TITANIA

The moon methinks looks with a watery eye;

TITANIA

And when she weeps, weeps every little flower,

TITANIA

Lamenting some enforced chastity.

TITANIA

Tie up my love's tongue bring him silently.

Exeunt
