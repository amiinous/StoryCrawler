V-Card - Part 5

Luke draped an arm over my shoulder as we walked.

Luke

Seriously. The skirt’s hot. Whoever you’re dressed up for doesn’t stand a chance.

And now you see why Luke is so popular with the girls.

Jennifer

Thanks.

Luke kissed the top of my head and we parted ways, me heading off to World Religions, and him going to Music Theory.

By the time Christian and Kinsley showed up, Professor Weinberg had already started in on Taoism.

I took copious notes during class; I found the different religions and their practices pretty cool.

The class was an elective I’d picked just because I needed another one for the credits, but I’d actually really gotten into it.

An hour and a half later, my butt was numb from the hard, uncomfortable chair.

The rest of the day passed by in a blur.

After Anatomy and Physiology II was Ethics of Health Care.

The end of my day left me tired and wishing I’d worn jeans.

What had I been thinking?

There are only girls in my classes, since the Nursing program is overrun with them.

There were a few guys in the program, but none of them were even remotely interesting.

When I got home, I found Christian on the phone, pacing our small living room with his voice raised.

Christian

No, Mom, I’m not seeing anybody...

I waved at him and he waved back absently, but his eyebrows were furrowed as he continued to pace.

Christian

Why can’t I just come alone without you getting all over my back?

I flopped down onto the couch and propped my feet up, the arches throbbing from wearing the high wedges all day.

Christian

I think I’m a long way from considering marriage, Mom.

Christian

I didn’t come to college to find a wife.

Christian

I came to get a degree and play football.

Christian

Look, I have to go, I have practice...okay? Bye.

He hung up his cell phone and threw it.

It clattered across the countertop noisily.

Jennifer

What’s going on?

He joined me in the living room, dropping down into Luke’s gaming chair with a sigh.

Christian

My mom is on my back again.

I’ve been roommates with Luke, Kinsley, and Christian for two years now.

Chloe is new.

So I know all about Christian’s rich parents and their high expectations.

Christian’s father is waiting for him to stop caring about sports and change his major from Kinesiology to Law.

Christian wants to be a coach; his father would prefer it if here were a lawyer, and eventually, a judge like him.

His mother is hoping he’ll come home engaged to a nice girl.

In her mind, ‘nice’ means Southern, white, wealthy, polite, and pretty.

If she’s ready to stay home and have Christian’s babies while running charities and hosting tea parties, all the better.

Jennifer

Another party?

Christian nodded.

Christian

Yeah, some cocktail thing.

Christian

Dad wants to show off his star football player son.

Christian

Mom is hoping I show up with a pretty girl on my arm.

Jennifer

When’s the party?

Christian

Saturday night.

I reached for my phone and idly started playing Candy Crush.

Jennifer

I’ll go with you.

Jennifer

Just tell your mom we’re dating.

Christian

You?

I glared at him over my phone.

Jennifer

Yeah, me. What’s so hard to believe about that?

Christian

Nothing, except you’re not my usual type.

Jennifer

Sorry, when God was handing out blonde hair and big boobs, I was too busy in the line for brains.

Christian

If you’re half as smart as you think, you’ll stay away from these people, Jenn.

Christian

Rich people are boring. Their parties are boring.

Jennifer

Fine. Go alone.

Jennifer

Spend the night letting your mom try to set you up with all her single friends’ daughters.

Christian winced.

Christian

I hadn’t thought of that.

Jennifer

Hmmm.

I concentrated hard on my last couple of moves.

I’d been stuck on level one twenty-five forever, and the last ten moves always screwed me up.

Christian

Would you really do it?

Jennifer

Shit, I’m out of moves.

Jennifer

I’m sorry, what?

Christian

Would you really go with me to this party and pretend to be my girlfriend?

Christian

You know, to keep my mom off my back.

Christian

She hasn’t met you yet, so it could work.

I shrugged.

Jennifer

Get dressed up and mingle with the bluebloods?

Jennifer

Why not, I don’t have anything better to do.

Then I remembered my sixty-day plan.

Jennifer

Unless a guy asks me out on a real date.

Jennifer

Then, you’re on your own.

Christian

Yeah, I don’t think we need to worry about that.

Christian ran as I chucked a couch cushion at him.

His laughter faded as he retreated up the stairs to his room to change for football practice.

Once he was gone, I idly thought over the contents of my closet.

I didn’t have anything near fancy enough for one of Christian’s parents’ parties.

I was going to have to raid Chloe’s closet.
