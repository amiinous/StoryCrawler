V-Card - Part 45

Twenty-five years of marriage and they were still like teenagers.

Everyone knew they were crazy about each other.

Mom

Oh, you don’t want to hear that old story.

Jennifer

I do.

I insisted.

Jennifer

I really do.

Jennifer

I know you’ve told it before, but I’ll admit I didn’t pay much attention.

Jennifer

I know you guys were high school sweethearts, but that’s all I know.

Mom laughed.

Mom

I wouldn’t exactly put it that way.

Mom

Your father and I didn’t become sweethearts until we’d graduated, but we did meet in high school.

Mom

You see, your father has always been smart.

I nodded in agreement.

It was pretty much a fact.

It was why he’d been able to support our family on a single income.

He was a computer whiz and a math genius.

Whenever I brought Kinsley home with me, the two liked to give each other equations and try to stump each other.

I know, total nerd fest.

Mom

Well, back in high school, he hadn’t quite come into his own yet.

Mom

He was tall, but gangly and clumsy.

Mom

It was years before he put on the brawn he has now.

Mom

But even then he was sweet.

Mom

Poor thing was picked on mercilessly.

Mom

I felt so bad for him, that one day I just had to say something.

Jennifer

How sweet.

She shrugged.

Mom

I was a cheerleader, which meant I had a bit of sway with the popular kids.

Mom

I convinced them to leave him alone, and made sure they stayed true to their word.

Mom

Well, apparently, I must have made an impact, because one day he showed up at my house asking what he could do to repay me.

Mom

Well, you know I’m horrible at math, and everyone knew he was a straight A student. So I asked him to tutor me.

Jennifer

So that’s how it happened?

Mom

Goodness, no! It was hard for me to see him that way at first.

Mom

Not because he was a nerd or anything, but because he became my friend.

Mom

We could talk for hours, and he was always so sweet.

Mom

I would have never thought anything would come of it.

I frowned.

Jennifer

I always thought it was like love at first sight with you guys or something.

Mom shook her head.

Mom

If you let your father tell it, he fell in love with me the moment he saw me.

Mom

But for me, it was different.

Mom

When your father kissed me for the first time, we were at a Fourth of July barbecue.

Mom

We were standing near the grill, I was holding a plate waiting for the hotdogs to come off and he just … kissed me.

Mom

After that, I realized that I’d been missing what was right in front of me that whole time.

Mom

From then on we were inseparable.

My frown deepened as I thought of Luke.

Jennifer

Weren’t you worried that it wouldn’t work out?

Jennifer

I mean, if you and Dad ever broke up, it would have ruined a great friendship.

Jennifer

I always thought, when I fell in love, it would be like a lightning strike, you know?

Jennifer

Suddenly, it would just happen and sweep me away.

Mom chuckled.

Mom

Well, sometimes it happens that way, though that part of love never lasts long.

Mom

Sure, that wobbly-kneed, tongue-tied feeling is magical.

Mom

That initial spark is a powerful thing, but over time it fades, and all that’s left is a foundation.

Mom

It’s up to the people involved to make the foundation a sturdy one.

Mom

That’s why your father and I did so well together.

Mom

We already had a firm foundation as friends.

Mom

We knew each other, we were comfortable.

Mom

Intensity came and went, but friendship was always there.

Mom

That kind of love is deeper than anything you could imagine.

Mulling over her words, I sipped at my tepid cider, my eyes wandering to the window and the dark, wooded area beyond the house.

My mother’s handmade mason jar lanterns glowed softly in some of the trees, lighting a pathway to the little gazebo where she and my father had stood on their wedding day.

This house had a lot of history for my family.

Mom

I’m sorry if my story wasn’t exciting enough for you,

Mom

but it’s the truth.

I turned back to her and shook my head.

Jennifer

No, Mom, it’s a great story.

Jennifer

It just gave me a lot to think about, is all.

Mom

Well then, I’ll leave you to it.

Mom

I’ve got more baking to finish before tomorrow, and it’s getting late.

I drained my cup and gave it to her before standing.

Jennifer

Need any help?

Mom

Nah. You know I enjoy my baking time.

Mom

Just me and Billy Joel.

Mom

I know you don’t like it much.

Jennifer

It’s not so much that I dislike it, but if you want this kitchen still standing in the morning, it’s better if I get out of here.

Mom laughed.

Mom

That’s true.

Mom

Skeedaddle, kiddo.

Mom

I’ll see you in the morning.

Jennifer

Okay. ‘Night, Mom.

Mom

Good night, Jennifer.
