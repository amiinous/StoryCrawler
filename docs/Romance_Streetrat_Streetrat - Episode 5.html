Streetrat - Episode 5

Back at the Beverly Hills mansion, Jazz stands in front of a full-length mirror as Kara pulls at the laces of her Chanel dress.

Jazz

Ouch! Do you have to pull them so tight?

Kara

Sorry, Poodle.

Jazz

I need room to breathe!

Jazz

Do I really have to go to this benefit?

Jazz

I’m so sick of these events.

Kara

Jazz, maybe you need to change your way of thinking.

Jazz

What do you mean?

Kara

Well, you want to own your own business someday, right?

Jazz

You know I do. I’ve already finished the business plan.

Kara

Right. You’re going to be at an event with the best and the brightest.

Kara

Instead of moaning about how you’re sick of attending these events…

Kara

Why don’t you focus on networking with the right people?

Kara

Let them know you have plans of your own.

Kara

Get them interested.

Jazz

My father would never approve of a female business owner.

Jazz

The only reason I’ll inherit as much as I will is because I don’t have a brother.

Kara

Jazz, your father is a smart man.

Kara

Enlighten him.

Jazz

It’s not possible.

Kara

Oh? Backing down from a challenge?

Jazz’s eyes twinkle. Kara has Jazz right where she wants her.

Jazz

Never.

Kara

Good – now go down and show your father that you’re equal parts beauty AND brains.

Meanwhile, back at the Inglewood apartment, Abby walks in and sets down her things.

Abby

Alex? Are you here?

Alex steps out of the bathroom, and Abby’s eyes widen.

His once long, scrappy-looking hair has been cut into a short crop.

His usual T-shirt and jeans have been replaced by a crisp designer suit.

His old rubber watch is gone. A new white-gold Rolex is in its place.

Abby

Who the hell are you and what have you done with Alex?

Alex

What do you think?

Abby

Wow. You look … really good.

Alex

I don’t know – I feel like a fraud.

Abby

You know what they say.

Abby

Fake it till you make it. What’s all this for, though?

Alex

There’s a fundraising event tonight.

Alex

Milton Holt invited me.

Abby

Already? Didn’t you just start working for him?

Alex

He might owe me a favor or two.

Alex

Or three.

Abby

Really? Why?

Alex

I might have saved his life. From a speeding car.

Abby

WHAT?! Lucky you!

Alex

He was so grateful that he offered to help me out. He gave me three favors.

Abby

And so you asked him to dress you up like a show monkey?

Alex

I wanted him to help me fit in with the rich crowd.

Abby

Are you trying to impress anyone in particular?!

Alex

Shut up…

Abby

You ARE, aren’t you?

Alex

Remember that girl we saw at the market? Getting picked on?

Abby

Yes …

Alex

Turns out she’s Ezra Mohammed’s daughter. She’s an heiress.

Abby

Heiress-schmeiress.

Abby

Bet she doesn’t know how to start a fire with some dental floss and chewing gum.

Alex

Right – we’re so sophisticated.

Abby

You’ve got a lot that she doesn’t – don’t be intimidated.

Alex

You think so?

Just then, Alex’s cell rings. It’s Jack Proust. Alex hits ignore.

Abby

I know so.

Alex

Okay – wish me luck.

He quickly hugs Abby and runs out the door.

Abby

Good luck …

Abby plasters on a smile.

But as soon as Alex is out the door, her smile turns to sadness.

Fifteen miles away in Beverly Hills, Jazz makes her way down the stairs.

Ezra, Jack and Eduardo watch her descend.

Ezra

Habibi – you look beautiful!

Jazz

Thanks, Baba. Shall we go?

Jazz and Ezra head out to the drive, where a limo is waiting.

Jack and Eduardo follow at a respectful distance.

Eduardo

You know, if all else fails, you could just marry Jazz and inherit everything through her.

Jack

Don’t be stupid, Eduardo. I have no feelings for her.

Eduardo

But money has a way of moving the heart.

Jack eyes Jazz as she and Ezra walk to the car. His eyes darken.

Jack

You might have a point, my stupid little nephew.

Jack and Eduardo follow Ezra and Jazz out to the limo.

At around the same time, Alex is sitting a limo with Milton Holt, along with three of his top executives.

Alex

This is so cool.

Milton

You think?

Alex

Totally. I can’t believe I’m getting to experience an actual gala.

Milton

I suppose I found it exciting too, the first twenty times or so.

Alex

It doesn’t excite you anymore?

Milton

No. It’s become more of an obligation than anything.

Alex

Really?

Milton

Absolutely – someone is always trying to raise money for something to prove they’re the next big thing.

Milton

You have to show up in order to stay relevant.

Milton

The minute you stop attending, they start whispering.

Milton

“He’s going to retire.”

Milton

“He’s lost his touch.”

Milton

“He’s gone crazy.”

Milton

It’s about keeping up appearances.

Milton

You want to be rich, and I want what you have.

Alex

A rundown apartment and rats for roommates?

Milton

Maybe not that.

Alex

So what is it, then?

Before Milton can answer him, the chauffeur stops the car.

Chauffeur

We’re here.

Milton

Shall we?

Milton Holt, Alex and the three executive make their way up a grand, red-carpeted staircase.

Massive chandeliers hang from the ceilings.

Everyone who is anyone is here.

Movie stars, politicians, athletes.

Alex’s mouth hangs open.

As soon as Milton enters, people begin swarming him.

Athlete

Milt! How are you, man? Good to see you!

Milton

Chris – how’ve you been, buddy?

Athlete

Hanging in, hanging in.

Athlete

Hi Judy. Hi Glen. Hi Taylor.

Athlete

And who’s this young chap?

Milton

Ah – Chris, let me introduce you to Alex – my protégé.

Alex stands, frozen. Milton nudges him.

Alex

So nice to, um, meet you. Really. Nice to meet you.

And so it goes.

Person after person.

Introduction after introduction.

Until Alex’s fear and awkwardness melt away.

Slowly, he gains confidence and becomes the life of the party.

Unfortunately, he decides that the best way to fit in is to … embellish his history a bit

Alex

First he tries it out on some backup dancers.

Alex

My father owned a law firm, so growing up, I spent a lot of time in his office.

Alex

I was reading briefs before I was watching Barney.

Alex

He’s retired now – he wanted me to take over the family firm.

Alex

But I couldn’t’ pass up the opportunity to work with Milton.

Milton

Alex, if I could interrupt: There’s someone I want you to meet.

Alex

Absolutely – if you ladies will excuse me.

Milton

Ah – there’s the man of the hour. Ezra! How are you?

Ezra

Milton – so good to see you.

Milton

Ezra, I’d like you to meet ... Alexander. He’s my newest protégé.

Ezra

So nice to meet you, Alexander. Where are you from?

Alex

I’m originally from Chicago. My father opened a law firm there.

Alex

My mother was a business consultant, so it was quite a pairing.

Ezra

Is that so? So you grew up with entrepreneurs in the house?

Alex

Oh yes – they built their businesses from the ground up. They taught me the meaning of hard work.

Ezra

Very interesting. And what is it you’d like to do exactly?

Alex

I’m still exploring, but venture finance has always interested me.

Alex

Milton has been such a generous mentor.

Ezra

Very impressive!

Ezra

Say, Alexander. Have you met my daughter, Jazz?

Alex

No … I don’t believe I have.

Ezra

She’s a wonderful girl. I think you two would hit it off.

Alex

Really! Yes – I’d love to meet her.

Ezra and Alex do not realize that Jazz, approaching the two from behind, has overheard their entire discussion.

Ezra

Great – why don’t we find a time for you two to get dinner?

Alex

Dinner would be good. How about this coming week?

Ezra

That should work for her – she likes Italian food.

Alex

I can definitely find an Italian spot.

Jazz

How considerate of you both to take my tastes into account!

Ezra

Habibi – we were just talking –

Jazz

So I heard. Talking about me and deciding things for me.

Jazz

I’m not one of your work projects, father. You can’t package and sell me.

Jazz

I’m not some property to be acquired.

Jazz

I can make my own decisions, thank you, and I won’t be having dinner with anyone.

With that, Jazz storms off, leaving the men speechless.
