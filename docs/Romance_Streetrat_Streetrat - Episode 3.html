Streetrat - Episode 3

Back at the mansion, Jazz and Kara are having some fun with one of Jazz’s unsuspecting suitors.

Kara

And once a month Jazz needs to visit the podiatrist.

Kara

She has a big green growth on her foot that they’ve assured us is nothing.

Kara

They just WHACK it off with a hedge clipper and send her on her way.

Kara

It’s going to be so much fun having you in the family!

Kara

Send me your underwear size and boxer/brief preferences.

Kara

We like to have them on file.

The young man flees out the front door without a backward glance.

Kara shuts the door with a big smile. Mission accomplished.

Jazz

Is he gone?

Kara

And never coming back, I’m certain!

Jazz

I can’t believe you asked him his underwear size!!

Kara

And did you like the bit about the hedge clippers?!

Jazz

That was genius. We need to save that.

As Jazz and Kara keel over in laughter, Ezra Mohammed makes his way down the grand staircase into the entrance foyer.

Ezra

What happened to Winston Carlisle?

Jazz

He’s opposed to hedge clippers.

Ezra frowns, and Kara quickly stifles her laughter.

Ezra

Ya Allah – Jazz, will you please stop scaring every eligible young suitor I bring to meet you?

Kara shakes her head, as if agreeing with Ezra.

Ezra

Oh stop that, Kara. You’re worse than she is.

Kara looks guilty as charged.

Ezra

That young man was very bright – his resume was extremely impressive.

Jazz

That’s the problem, Dad.

Jazz

You’re not looking for a husband for me.

Jazz

You’re looking for a successor for you.

Jazz makes her way into the kitchen. Ezra and Kara follow her.

Kara puts on a pot of Turkish coffee.

Ezra

Habibi, what’s wrong with marrying a successful man?

Ezra

You know how much your mother struggled when she came here from Lebanon.

Ezra

She had nothing – she had to steal for food.

Ezra

I was able to give her a life of comfort.

Ezra

And we worked hard to provide better for you.

Ezra

But in case it’s not enough, I want you to be taken care of.

Jazz

I can take care of myself.

Jazz

I’m not one of your business deals. I don’t need to be negotiated.

Jazz

I want my own life.

Jazz

The life you built for you and Mama is fine for you and it was fine for her.

Jazz

I’m glad she had it before she died.

Jazz

But it’s not what I want for me.

Jazz

I want more adventure than this.

Ezra

You have plenty of adventure in your life.

Ezra

Like tomorrow, when you come to the office for my Forbes photoshoot.

Jazz glares at her father, takes her coffee and leaves the room.

The next day, Jack and Eduardo sit in the conference room at Mohammed Investments, waiting.

Jack

What’s taking him so long?

Eduardo

He’s probably never put on a suit before.

Jack

And this is supposed to be the smartest kid on the streets?

Just then, Alex walks through the door.

He looks a little like a pig wrapped in gold.

The designer suit clashes with his otherwise scruffy looks.

Jack

Not bad.

Alex

I don’t know. The pants seem a little short.

Eduardo It’s the style. Duh.

Jack

It fits you fine. So let’s prep for your interview. Sit down.

Jack and Eduardo take a seat at the long conference room table across from Alex.

Jack

Okay. What makes you the best candidate for an internship with the Milton Holt Group?

Alex pauses for a moment.

Alex

I was orphaned at three after my parents were killed in a car crash.

Alex

I was put into foster care.

Alex

My fosters parents’ only interest was the child support money they received.

Alex

I learned to take care of myself from a very young age.

Alex

I may not be as polished as everyone else.

Alex

But I’m a quick study.

Alex

I’m hungry – usually literally and figuratively – and I’m independent.

Jack

Decent. And what do you hope to learn from an internship with the Milton Holt Group?

Alex doesn’t respond.

Something – someONE, actually – has caught his eye.

Jack

Ahem?

A look of disbelief appears on Alex’s face.

Eduardo

Hellooooo!? Do you not understand the question?

Alex

Who is that?

Eduardo

What are you talking about?!

Alex

That girl. Who is that girl?

Jack follows Alex’s gaze.

Jack

That’s Jazz. Ezra Mohammed’s daughter.

Jazz is in the office for the Forbes photo shoot.

Alex is stunned.

Jazz

Can we please hurry this up?

Jazz

As if this isn’t enough, I have another fitting for the benefit.

At that moment, Alex realizes that his dream girl from the market is even further out of his league than he thought.

Alex

What benefit is she talking about?

Jack

All of the big financial groups throw a benefit to support the children’s hospital.

Jack

It’s this Saturday.

Alex

I see…

Alex looks longingly at Jazz.

Jack

Focus, boy.

Jack

Your interview is in an hour.

Jack

We’ve got to carve out the diamond in the rough here.

Once again, as she did at the market, Jazz slips out of view.

And Jack’s plan to use Alex to take over Mohammed Investments gathers momentum …

Two hours later, Jack and Eduardo wait inside a tinted limo parked near the Milton Holt Group.

Jack

He’s been in there for almost two hours!

Eduardo

Maybe he’s having trouble forming sentences. What a nerd.

Just then, the limo door opens and Alex gets in.

Jack

Well?!

Alex

You’re looking at the newest intern at the Milton Holt Group.

Jack

Excellent!

Jack pulls out a fifty-dollar bill and hands it to Alex.

It’s only the beginning.
