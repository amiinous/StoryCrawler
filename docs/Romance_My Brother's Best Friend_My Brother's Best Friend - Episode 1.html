My Brother's Best Friend - Episode 1

Jay

Hey—Want a ride to Gray’s party tonight?

Eileen

Lol, did you mean to text my brother?

Jay

No. I didn’t mean to text Pat.

Jay

I meant to text YOU, Eileen.

Jay

Let’s go to the party together.

Eileen

What? Don’t you think that’d be weird?

Jay

No. Why would it be?

Eileen

I don’t know. It’s just, showing up at a party together…

Eileen

It might seem like we’re dating or something.

Jay

So what? We’re friends, right?

Eileen

Yeah…so?

Jay

So, you know more about me than most of the girls I date.

Jay

And look, I just think it would be nice to go with you.

Jay

To actually get to know you.

Jay

WITHOUT Pat being around.

Eileen

I guess…

Jay

You need a ride anyway, right?

Eileen

Yeah…

Jay

So come with me. It’ll be fun.

Jay

And…I want to show you my new wheels.

Eileen

OMG, stop! Your car’s finally in?

Jay

Yeah. Dammit, I can’t wait—I’ve gotta show you now.

Eileen

OMG, amazing.

Eileen

Lol okay. I TOTALLY want a ride in that.

Jay

Great, I’ll be over in fifteen minutes.

Ten minutes later, Jay pulls into the driveway early.

Eileen is beginning to put on her makeup.

She answers the door in her curlers,

Nonchalantly calling over her shoulder.

Eileen

I’ll be in the downstairs bathroom.

Eileen

You know where the Doritos are in the kitchen.

Jay

See? You know me so well.

Jay

You even know my favorite food.

Eileen rolls her eyes and walks into the bathroom

As Jay pads behind her, unopened bag of Doritos in hand.

He leans forward and…

he whispers in her ear.

Jay

Is Pat home?

Eileen

No.

Jay

Good. Because for three years…

Jay

I’ve been dying to do this.

Jay places his hands on Eileen’s waist, pivoting her around to face him.

Very slowly, as if asking for permission,

He leans down to place a kiss on her lips.

It’s soft and tentative, as if they’re made of glass…

And the contact could shatter them completely.

Eileen

What…what was that?

Jay

Was it bad?

Eileen

No, it was…different.

Jay

Yeah, but different in a good or bad way?

Eileen

I don’t know, just different.

Eileen

You’ve always been my brother’s best friend, Jay.

Eileen

I’ve never imagined doing that with you.

Jay

Seriously? You’ve never thought of kissing me?

Eileen

Not really.

Jay

I think about kissing you all the time.

Jay

When you’re dressed up at a party—

Jay

When you’re all neat and polished at school—

Jay

But most of all, I’ve wanted to kiss you when you’re hanging out here.

Eileen

Haha, don’t be ridiculous.

Eileen

I look like a slob at home.

Jay

That’s exactly it!

Eileen

Watch yourself, haha.

Jay

Haha, no, listen—

Jay

I love when your hair’s on top of your head like Medusa’s,

Jay

And when you don’t have a stitch of makeup on your face.

Eileen

What?!

Jay

I’m serious.

Jay

You’re just so human, Eileen.

Jay

And I love that you’re never shy or awkward around me.

Jay

You’re different than other girls.

Jay

Look, I’m leaving for college in three months—

Jay

And if I don’t ask you this question, I’ll always wonder—

Jay

Will you be my girlfriend?

Eileen

Oh my God, Jay.

Eileen

What about Pat?

Jay

What about Pat?

Eileen

You guys have been best friends since kindergarten.

Eileen

And you know how protective he is of me.

Jay

But who better than me to treat you the way you deserve to be treated?

Eileen

I don’t know…don’t you think we should ask him first?

Jay

Ask him what—if I can court you?

Jay

This isn’t the 1800s, Eileen. You can speak for yourself.

Eileen

I know I can, but he’s my brother.

Eileen

It may be old fashioned, but his approval is important to me.

Eileen

Family’s important to me.

Jay

You’re right—what was I thinking?

Jay

This is exactly why I like you, Eileen.

Jay

You’re the most thoughtful person I know.

Jay

But wait, you can tell me—

Jay

Were you just using Pat as an excuse because you don’t want to date me?

Eileen

Actually…no.

Eileen

I hadn’t thought about kissing you before, Jay.

Eileen

But I know that you’re a good person.

Eileen

And I know you’d treat me well.

Eileen

So if Pat’s not too weirded out by it, I say…

Eileen

Why not?

Jay

Oh my God, that’s amazing.

Jay

I can’t wait another minute to make it official.

Jay

Can we ask Pat tonight, at the party?

Eileen

Sure. But first, I need to do something about this hair.

Jay waits patiently while Eileen perfects her hair and makeup,

And slips into a slinky summer dress with strappy gold heels.

The moment she finishes threading her gold earrings through her earlobes,

Jay’s hands are buried in the loose brown curls of her hair

And he has her trapped in a passionate kiss.

Eileen

Wow, Jay. You’re a really good kisser.

Eileen

Maybe we SHOULD stay home from the party…

Jay

No. You were right—family first.

Eileen

I was right, huh?

Eileen

I often am. You’re probably going to have to get used to that.

Jay

Haha, easy there, Eileen. That pedestal you put yourself on looks a little wobbly.

Eileen

Haha, no way. This thing is made of solid gold.

The pair continues laughing and joking on their way to the car,

As they drive to the party, and when they walk through the door.

But then Pat is suddenly before them,

And…

Their blissful feelings evaporate.

Pat

Dude, my sister?!

Pat

Are you SHITTING me right now??!

Jay

No, I’m not shitting you!

Jay

I’m dating her, and I don’t care what you think.

Eileen

Erm, Jay, that wasn’t really the plan.

Pat’s eyes stay furiously glued on Jay.

Pat

You don’t care what I THINK?

Pat

You don’t care that you’re throwing away THIRTEEN years of friendship?

Pat

I trusted you, man.

Pat

You know how important my family is to me.

Pat

And to do this here…

Jay

I couldn’t wait, man.

Pat

I did NOT need to hear that.

Jay

No, hear me out.

Pat

No dude, I can’t—I can’t even look at you.

Eileen

Then look at me.

Eileen’s loud, clear voice stops both men in their tracks.

A heavy silence, filled with all the pressure of a single wrong word—

Falls between them.

Eileen

That’s better.

Eileen

Pat, I care about Jay.

Pat

What the—

Eileen

I’m not finished.

Pat

Sorry.

Eileen

I care about him, but Pat, I care about you too.

Eileen

That’s why we came to you.

Eileen

As soon as we realized we liked each other.

Eileen

We want you to be okay with the idea of the two of us dating.

Eileen

I mean, who better than your little sister and your best friend, right?

Eileen

You already love both of us.

Pat

Yeah but—what if something happens between you two?

Pat

Something bad?

Eileen reaches out for Jay’s hand

And he grasps hers in return.

Eileen

Of course I want to believe it won’t end badly.

Eileen

But that’s always a real possibility.

Eileen

So you have to trust us.

Eileen

And believe that we’re adults who will carefully pull apart the pieces.

Eileen

Without hurting each other.

Jay

I’d never hurt you, Eileen.

Jay

Seriously, Pat. I wouldn’t hurt your sister.

There a long silence as Pat looks at the people in front of him—

The two most important people in his life.

Pat

Fine.

Pat

But under two conditions.

Pat

One: If you ever get married, I get to be the best man at your wedding.

Eileen

Pat! Don’t put that kind of pressure on us!

Jay

Dude, OF COURSE you’ll be my best man.

Eileen

Jay! We are NOT talking about our wedding right now.

Jay

Fine, fine.

Then he leans in and whispers to Pat.

Jay

Not yet, anyway…

Eileen

I heard that!

Pat

Quit bickering, you two.

Pat

I still have another condition.

Pat

There will be absolutely NO kissing in front of me.

Jay

In that case, I’d better take Eileen back home…

Pat

Dude! My conditions!

Jay

You never said anything about TALKING about kissing.

Jay

And I plan to kiss the ever-living hell out of this girl.
