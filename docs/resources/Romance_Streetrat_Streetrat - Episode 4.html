Streetrat - Episode 4

Back at the shabby apartment in Inglewood, Alex is telling Abby about his new job at the Milton Holt Group.

Abby

How are you even qualified for this?

Alex

They saw something in me, I guess.

Abby

What will your job responsibilities be?

Alex

I don’t know – assist around the office. Schedule meetings…

Abby

So in other words, fetch coffee and wipe their asses?

Alex

Shut up – everyone has to start somewhere.

Just then, Abby catches a glimpse of something in Alex’s shirt pocket.

Abby

What is THAT?! Is that a fifty?

Alex

What? No – it’s just –

Abby snatches the fifty-dollar bill from his pocket.

Abby

I’ve never seen a five with a zero after it on paper money! Who did you steal this from?

Alex

I didn’t steal it – I earned it.

Abby

Wait – can I fetch coffee and wipe asses with you?

Alex

Stop!

Abby

Are you selling drugs?! How did you get this?

Alex

I’m just helping someone out.

Alex

Quit asking questions and go to bed!

Abby

I’m not tired – how are you rolling in fifties?!

Alex

Abby – I have work tomorrow. Go to bed.

Abby lies down on her threadbare mattress with a huff.

Alex carefully puts the fifty in a rickety old safe.

On top of his encyclopedias.

The next morning, Alex leaves the Milton Holt Group with a piece of paper in his hand.

Abby has been half right so far.

He does have to fetch coffee.

He hopes she’s not right about the other half.

He reads off the sheet.

Alex

Grande caramel mach … mach … what the heck is this?

Alex

A frappuccino, an almond latte, a matcha latte…

Alex

What happened to a regular black coffee in the morning?

Alex looks up, searching for a Starbucks that is supposedly close by.

But he is distracted by a man’s angry-sounding voice.

Angry Man

No – no. That is not what we discussed!

Angry Man

No – you listen to me!

Across the street, the angry man gets out of his cab, still speaking into his phone.

Angry Man

That is not the appropriate way to mitigate loss!

Alex

Sheesh. Clearly, someone hasn’t had his frappuccino this morning.

Angry Man

I need a meeting in my office, STAT.

As Alex resumes his search for a Starbucks, his eyes widen.

The light at the crosswalk has changed from green to red.

The angry man, consumed in his conversation, doesn’t notice.

A car heads right towards him.

Alex

Hey! Watch out!!

Alex

You’re going to get hit!

Alex

Ugh – screw it.

Alex runs into the crosswalk and body checks the man.

They’re both sent flying to the curb.

Alex

Sorry – it just seemed like you’d be better off if I ran into you instead of that car.

Angry Man

Wow – thank you so much.

Alex

No worries. Sounds like your day is off to a bad start.

Alex

I just didn’t want it to get worse.

Angry Man

Thank you. You saved my life.

Alex

Eh – all in a day’s work.

Angry Man

Let me take you to breakfast.

Alex

That’s very nice of you.

Alex

But I’m supposed to be back at the office with like four different combinations of coffee and milk.

Angry Man

Where do you work?

Alex

The Milton Holt Group.

Alex

I’m an intern.

Angry Man

I think I can convince your boss to let you off for breakfast.

Alex

That’s really nice of you, but it’s my first day, and I don’t want to be late.

Angry Man

If you’re not going to accept my invitation, then I’ll just have to make it part of your job description.

Alex looks at him, perplexed.

The man extends his hand, looking significantly less angry.

Angry Man

I’m Milton Holt.

Alex’s eyes widen in disbelief.

Milton

I can provide ID, if you’d like.

A few minutes later, stare at their menus, deciding.

A waitress approaches.

Milton

I’ll just take a black coffee.

Alex looks at him, pleased.

Alex

I’ll have the same.

Milton

So – what made you decide to intern with us?

Alex

I was orphaned at three after my parents were killed in a car crash.

Alex

I was put into foster care.

Alex

I learned to take care of myself from a very young age …

Milton

Skip the elevator pitch, kid.

Milton

Why are you really here?

Alex

I want to impress a girl.

Milton

Now we’re talking.

Alex

She’s beautiful and smart and wealthy.

Alex

I feel like I don’t fit into this world at all.

Milton

I felt the same way – but look at me now.

Alex

I feel like street trash compared to her.

Milton

Well – you helped me out today.

Milton

Maybe I can help you.

Alex

You would do that?

Milton

I’m a numbers man, Alex, and three is my lucky number.

Milton

I’m going to grant you three favors.

Milton

I’ll help you in three ways – any way I can.

Milton

Some disclaimers. Number one, you can’t ask me for an infinite number of favors.

Milton

Number two, I won’t kill anyone for you.

Milton

Number three: I can’t make her fall in love with you

Milton

But I’m willing to help give you the tools.

Milton

So? What do you say?

Milton extends his hand. Alex takes it.

Alex

I’m in.
