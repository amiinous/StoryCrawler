Streetrat - Episode 6

After storming away from her father and his posse at the gala,

Jazz ducks into the ladies room.

Flustered, she looks into the mirror,

Not sure who she’s allowed herself to become.

Just then, the bathroom door swings open to reveal—Alex.

Jazz

What the hell, you creep!? This is the ladies room!

Alex

Wait, I’m not that kind of guy.

Jazz

Well you’re not exactly a girl either, so get out!

Alex

No, I mean—I’m sorry—for how I came off.

Jazz

It’s fine, but can you leave me alone please?

Alex

I know how you’re feeling. These fundraisers get so old.

Jazz

It didn’t seem like you were too bothered by it back there.

Alex

It’s a show—I do it for my family. But these suits aren’t my thing.

Alex

I much prefer not to have any obligations.

Alex

You start to feel like a puppet at these things.

Jazz

Exactly— that’s exactly how I feel.

Alex

Honestly, I’m just putting in my time right now.

Alex

What I really want is to see the world.

Alex

I’d love to take a trip to Europe.

Jazz

And stay at four star resorts?

Alex

No—I’d want to backpack. Hostels. Do it authentically.

This catches Jazz’s attention.

Alex

I’m Alexander.

Jazz

I’m Jazz.

Alex

Hey—want to ditch this place?

Jazz

Yes, but we’d never get out without them seeing us.

Alex

I know a back route.

Alex shows Jazz out a back-door to a fire escape.

They descend, delighted by their own disobedience.

When they reach the ground, Alex grabs Jazz’s hand and leads her to Milton’s car.

Milton’s chauffeur, Richie, has taken a liking to Alex, and he welcomes the two warmly.

Richie

What? Party end already?

Alex

We decided to take a bit of an intermission.

Richie

So they bored you to death, huh?

Richie turns to Jazz.

Richie

I’m Richie.

Jazz

Jazz. Nice to meet you.

Alex

Richie, would you be willing to take us for a ride?

Richie

Wheels up! Where are we going?

Alex

Where should we go, Jazz? The world is our oyster.

Jazz

To tell you the truth, I’m kind of hungry.

Alex

To Shake Shack, Rich!

Richie

Shake Shack it is!

one hour later

Jazz and Alex sit at Shake Shack, slurping milk shakes

And chomping on fries in their formalwear.

Alex

My superpower would be to fly, definitely.

Jazz

That is so lame.

Alex

That’s not lame! You think Superman was lame? He could fly!

Jazz

I think it’s a cliché.

Alex

Fine. What would you pick?

Jazz

Time travel.

Alex

So you could go back to the time when women had no rights?

Jazz

Funny. You should try stand-up.

Alex

Fine, sorry. So why would you choose time travel?

Jazz

So I could spend more time with my mom.

Alex puts down his burger and listens.

Jazz

She died when I was thirteen. Cancer.

Alex

I’m so sorry.

Jazz

It’s okay. I carry her with me every day.

Alex

I know what it’s like to—

But he stops himself, for fear of what Jazz will think if she finds out who he really is.

Alex

I know what it’s like to miss someone.

Alex

My grandparents died recently.

Alex

We were really close.

Jazz

It sucks. It’s like being in a reality that you didn’t choose and can’t escape.

Alex

Right. I guess we just have to control whatever we can in our reality.

Jazz stares at Alex, searching.

Alex

What? Do I have milkshake on my face?

Jazz

No! It’s just...I feel like we’ve met before.

Alex tries to hide his panic at the thought of being discovered.

Alex

No—I get that a lot. Ryan Reynolds stole my look.

Jazz laughs and rolls her eyes.

Jazz

So what should we control now?

Alex

I’m thinking we control another milkshake.

Jazz

Chocolate please!

one hour later

Alex and Jazz get back into Milton’s car after raiding a corner drug store for candy.

Richie

Whoa, whoa—how about we go easy on the sugar highs?

Richie

Or at the very least, share.

Alex

M&M’s or Skittles?

Richie

What do you think? Fork over the chocolate, man.

Jazz

Where should we go now?

Jazz

There’s a great view of the stars in Central Park! Richie—can you take us there?

Richie

Sure—

But before Richie can continue, his car phone rings.

Jazz and Alex can hear Milton’s booming voice on the other end.

Milton

Where are you?!

Richie

Sorry sir, I just...went to grab a bite to eat.

Milton

I need you to get back here—this thing is almost over, and I am finished making small talk.

Richie

No problem, boss—be there in five.

Milton

And have you seen Alex?!

Alex shoots Richie a panicked look.

Alex shakes his head violently, then mimes throwing up.

Richie

No, I haven’t. I think he—

Richie

—Went home sick.

Milton

He could have at least told me.

Richie

He looked pretty bad.

Richie

I’m on my way now.

Richie begins to drive back.

Richie

You guys are going to have to duck out of the car quickly.

Richie

He’ll be waiting on the curb.

Richie

He can’t find out that I lied to him.

As Richie pulls up to the gala, Alex and Jazz roll out of the street side door— narrowly avoiding Milton, who gets in curbside.

Alex and Jazz run to the fire escape where they left the gala earlier.

Alex

I had a really great time with you.

Jazz

Me too. Thanks for the candy.

Jazz waits expectantly.

Alex fidgets nervously, digging his heel in the ground.

They don’t break eye contact.

Alex reaches for Jazz’s hand.

She smiles at him warmly.

Then, slowly, Alex leans in and kisses her softly on the lips.

Jazz

Goodnight, Alexander.

Jazz disappears up the steps.

Alex, dazed and dreamy eyed from his kiss, wanders back around the building.

He doesn’t notice when two figures emerge from the bushes.

He briefly sees them coming out of the corner of his eye—

But before he can react, they’ve grabbed him, bound and gagged him, and dragged him off.
