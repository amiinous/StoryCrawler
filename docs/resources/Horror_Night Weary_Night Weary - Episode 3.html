Night Weary - Episode 3

David picks up his phone, dialing Mary’s number.

The second the call connects, he screams in her ear.

David

If Samson’s next to your window…

David

DRIVE!!!

Mary

That’s exactly what I told my cab driver to do.

Mary

We hit the gas.

David

Good—get yourself home.

David

I really don’t want you here.

David

I need to know that you’re somewhere safe.

Mary

I don’t think we’ll be able to find that somewhere safe, David.

Mary

He’s chasing us.

David

Shit.

Mary

Yeah. And I can’t get the image of his face out of my head.

Mary

When he was looking into my window…

Mary

His eyes were red and bugging out of their sockets.

Mary

And I swear, he was foaming at the mouth.

Mary

There was saliva running down his chin.

David

Mary, that’s so messed up. I can’t just listen to this while I sit here.

David

Maybe I can run over and intercept you guys.

David

That way, I can help you out.

David

Where are you?

Mary

David, you are NOT running over here on foot.

Mary

I’m terrified of what he might do, but at least I’m in the safety of a cab.

David

But I want to help!

Mary

I know. And that’s very sweet…

Mary

But you won’t be able to help me if you’re dead.

Mary

You need to protect yourself. Stay hidden.

Mary

I can’t live if something happens to you.

David

Okay, okay. But what do we do?

Mary

Crap!

David

What?! What was that banging noise??

David

Are you okay?

Mary

Yeah. He just lunged for the back of the cab.

Mary

Thank God he missed.

Mary

But the cab driver is literally screaming his head off.

Mary

He’s about two seconds from throwing me out of this cab.

David

Shit! This is horrible—I hate feeling helpless while you’re in this situation.

Mary

OMG, he’s doing it again…

Mary

Oh God, that noise is terrible.

Mary

I can hear his nails dragging across the metal.

David

Is he ON the cab??

Mary

Yeah. His feet are in the air and everything.

Mary

Oh my God, David…he’s crawling across the trunk…

Mary

He’s so close to the rear window…

David

Shit!

Mary

What do I do?! Oh my God, what do I do??

David

I don’t know!

Mary

David he’s literally INCHES away from the glass.

Mary

I can see his red eyes looking straight at me.

David

WHAT?!

David

That sounds so scary—shit!!

David

I should have never told you to come here. I should have never—

Mary

David, focus.

Mary

I need you now.

Mary

WHAT DO I DO??!

David

Umm…

David

Quick! Tell your driver to make a sharp turn!

David

Maybe that will throw him off the cab.

Mary

Okay.

Mary puts her hand on the receiver and hollers up to her cab driver.

Mary

Sir, can you please turn quickly?

Mary

AHHHHH!

Mary

The driver made a really tight turn onto 29th.

Mary

I slid across the backseat and everything.

David

Did you lose Samson?

Mary

I’m too afraid to look.

David

Sweetheart, you’re the bravest person I know.

David

Look behind you—I’ll keep texting you.

David

It’ll almost be like I’m there with you.

Mary

Okay. Thanks.

Mary

Oh my God, thank GOD.

Mary

Samson flew off the cab.

Mary

But oh no! He’s crumpled in a ball on the ground.

Mary

And he’s not getting up.

Mary

Oh God, David.

Mary

What if we hurt him?

David

We were protecting you.

Mary

Should we call the police?

David

Idk, I don’t want them to lock him up.

Mary

He’s starting to get up…

Mary

But I think we’ll get away in time. He’s moving very slowly.

David

Shit. This feels wrong.

David

Sweetheart, maybe you were right—SHOULD we call the police?

David

Or is there a way that we can help him instead?

Mary

Let’s talk about it over text.

Mary

We drove away, so I can’t see him anymore.

Mary

And that means I don’t know where he is.

Mary

I don’t want him sneaking up behind you if he hears you talking on the phone.

David

Okay, talk soon. I love you.

Mary

Love you too.

Mary disconnects the calls, and types out a quick text to David.

Mary

So what do you think we CAN do?

Mary

Is there a way for us to turn him back into a human?

David

Well, once that full moon goes away, we should be good.

Mary

Yeah, but it’s only 3:45 a.m.

Mary

The sun won’t rise for two hours!

David

That’s not that long.

David

He was down on the ground for a while, right?

Mary

Yeah, so?

David

Hopefully he won’t be able to get me if he’s hurt.

David

Plus, I’m still hiding behind this grave.

David

I’ve got my back covered.

Mary

Idk, David. I’d feel better if we were actually DOING something to fix Samson.

Mary

I don’t want to just wait around.

Mary

The driver’s telling me that I either need to get out of the cab, or give him a new address.

David

Then what do you suggest we do?

Mary

Well, I just googled werewolves.

Mary

Apparently if Samson ingests the right amount of wolfsbane, he’ll become human again.

David

What’s wolfsbane?

Mary

Idk, some kind of a plant.

Mary

The article has pictures. It kind of looks like a purple flower.

David

And where are we supposed to find it?

Mary

Well, my Aunt Angela has a huge garden at her house in Chestnut Hill.

Mary

She grows almost every plant known to mankind.

Mary

Maybe she has some in there.

David

Holy shit, that’s so far away.

David

I have to just sit around and wait until you’re back?

Mary

I’m already on my way.

Mary

I mean, what the hell else are we supposed to do??

David

Idk—I guess that’s fine.

David

Wait, sweetheart?

David

You said that if Samson ingests the RIGHT amount of wolfsbane it could fix him—

David

But what if he ingests the WRONG amount?

Mary

Idk if I want to tell you.

David

You have to.

Mary

All right. But don’t say I didn’t warn you…

Mary

If Samson ingests the wrong amount of wolfsbane…

Mary

He could die.
