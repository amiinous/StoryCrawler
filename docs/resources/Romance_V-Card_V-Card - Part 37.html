V-Card - Part 37

Tears blinded me as I confronted Dain and his friends.

The two other guys had been sitting on the bed, and jumped to their feet at my intrusion.

Christian winced and Dain’s eyes grew round as saucers.

Dain

Jenn.

Dain's voice changed back to the soft, sweet tone he’d been using on me for the past month.

He came toward me, removing his eye patch.

Dain

Babe, I can explain.

Jennifer

Shut up.

I snapped, shaking my head.

I speared his friends with a narrow glare.

Jennifer

Get out.

Shooting Dain pitying glances, they obliged.

Only Christian remained.

Jennifer

You too.

Christian stared back and forth between me and Dain.

Christian

You sure?

I nodded.

Jennifer

Yes.

He folded his arms across his chest and tried to look intimidating, which actually worked since he was dressed as the Caped Crusader.

Christian

Fine.

His jaw was clenched.

He turned to Dain.

Christian

I’ll be right outside this door, and if she even whimpers, I’m breaking it down.

Ignoring him, Dain came toward me as Christian closed the door.

He reached for me and I backed away, holding my hands up to ward him off.

Jennifer

Don’t touch me.

I hissed, blinking back the tears starting to cloud my vision.

I would not cry in front of him.

Jennifer

Don’t ever touch me again.

Dain

Jenn, babe, come on. You know that’s just how guys talk.

Dain

I didn’t mean any of it.

Jennifer

No? Not even the part about my luscious tits?

Jennifer

Or how about this stupid necklace you give to all your girlfriends.

Dain winced.

Dain

Okay, the part about your breasts was tacky, but I didn’t mean anything by it.

Dain

It’s just locker room talk, no one takes it seriously.

Dain

As far as the necklace … I may have bought some like it in the past, but you’re the only one I care about, Jenn, I swear.

I shook my head.

Jennifer

All this time I thought you really liked me, but I was just a plaything for you, wasn’t I?

Jennifer

Rich, spoiled Dain can have any girl he wants, but he wants a good girl.

Jennifer

One that knows her place, right?

Dain ran a hand over his hair and sighed.

Dain

Look, sweetheart, life isn’t all rainbows and candy, okay?

Dain

You’re a big girl, you know how it goes.

Dain

I’m a man, and I have needs.

Jennifer

Yeah, well, from what I hear your little friend down at the coffee shop has been taking care of them just fine.

Dain

Look, you were the one who asked me just a few weeks ago how I felt about some other guy putting the moves on you.

Dain

You’re in no position to talk.

Jennifer

I was honest with you about that!

Jennifer

And when he tried to kiss me, I told him I wanted you, not him.

Jennifer

After that, nothing ever happened again.

Jennifer

You, on the other hand, have been screwing that girl and God knows who else.

I reached up to the chain around my neck and found the pendant.

Yanked it, the clasp snapped and the chain fell noiselessly to the floor.

Jennifer

This is over.

I brushed past him.

Jennifer

I don’t ever want to see you again.

Dain shrugged as I brushed past him.

Dain

Fine.

Dain

It’s not like I’ll have trouble replacing you.

Dain

There are plenty of others who are willing, and way hotter than you.

Jennifer

They’re welcome to you, then.

I replied and turned to the doorway.

Christian stood, watching on the other side as he’d promised.

Jennifer

Oh … and, Dain?

Dain

What?

Jennifer

One last thing.

Before he could blink, my arm lashed out and my fist connected with his face.

Blood spurted from his nose and stained my knuckles; pain radiated up my wrist and arm.

Dain

Ah, damn it! Bitch!

Dain roared, pressing a hand to his gushing nose.

A slight wave of satisfaction rolled over me.

I turned and walked out.

Once I was clear of the room, I kept walking, down the hall and through the living room.

Christian

Jenn!

Christian called out behind me, but I ignored him.

Christian

Jenn!

Faster I went, until I was jogging, past Kinsley and Chloe, toward the front door.

Kinsley

What’s wrong?

I heard Kinsley ask.

Christian murmured a response I couldn’t hear.

My blood was pounding and creating a dull roar in my ears and acidic bile had risen up in my throat.

My lungs were burning with the effort it took not to cry, and all I wanted was to be left alone.

I flung the front door open, running now as I neared the stairs.

Luke

Jenn!

This time, the voice was Luke’s.

He was coming behind me, his sunglasses removed and his eyebrows furrowed with worry.

Luke

Jenn, wait up!

I shook my head and went faster, going down the stairs toward the parking garage.

This was the last thing I needed, to be confronted with confusing feelings toward Luke after what Dain had just put me through.

A sob tore up from my chest and there was no stopping it now.

Hot tears splashed my face, and I didn’t bother trying to stop them this time.

I careened forward on the stairs, thrown off balance once again by Chloe’s heels, catching myself on the banister just in time.

My knee hit the concrete step and I cried out, pain now shooting up my leg and into my hip.

When I stood, Chloe’s undoubtedly expensive jumpsuit was torn at the knee and a scrape was welling up with blood.

I clung to the stair rail, and lowered myself onto the bottom step, wrapping my arms around my knees.

Burying my face there, I gave in to the hurt and embarrassment tearing through me.

Just when I’d thought things were going so well, everything I’d thought to be true had turned out to be a lie.

I felt so stupid for being fooled by a pair of pretty green eyes.

Most importantly, I was angry over the time I’d wasted on Dain.

A hand rested between my shoulder blades and I felt a body on the steps beside mine.

Warm, solid, and steady.

Luke.

I glanced up at him, sniffling as a fresh wave of tears came.

He wiped them away gently, his face a mask of anger and empathy.

In that moment, I didn’t care about anything else that had happened between us.

The fact that he was here when I needed him was enough.

I let him help me to my feet and didn’t push him away when he put an arm around my waist.

Luke

Come on.

He led me away from the building.

Luke

I’m taking you home.
