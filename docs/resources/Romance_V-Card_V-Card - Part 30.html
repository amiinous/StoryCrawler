V-Card - Part 30

Day 22

The next morning, I sat in the cafeteria across from Kinsley, poking at cold scrambled eggs.

Not even my favorite biscuits could snap me out of the daze I’d been in since waking up that morning.

My eyelids were heavy and my stomach was in knots.

Kinsley

Are you okay?

Kinsley was watching me from her side of the table.

Kinsley

You look like crap.

I wanted to be offended, but she was right.

I’d had just enough energy this morning to pick out a pair of holey jeans and a worn, long-sleeved top.

I think I might have finger-combed my hair before pulling it into a messy bun at the back of my head.

Stray strands were everywhere and last night’s leftover mascara accentuated the dark circles under my eyes.

At least I’d showered and brushed my teeth … I wasn’t that tired.

Jennifer

Weird dreams last night.

I answered honestly.

Without Chloe around I could talk to Kinsley about anything.

I loved Chloe to death, but that girl takes nothing seriously.

Kinsley

Really? About what?

Jennifer

About Dain … and Luke.

Kinsley’s eyebrows shot up.

Kinsley

A threesome? Kinky.

I pursed my lips at her and threw a straw.

She ducked and laughed.

Jennifer

Not that kind of dream!

Jennifer

It was just weird.

Jennifer

I was in Dain’s apartment and we were kissing on the couch.

Jennifer

It was nice, you know? Just like it always is when I’m with him.

Jennifer

Then I closed my eyes, and when I opened them again, he turned into Luke.

Jennifer

I got up and started to run away from him, and he followed me.

Jennifer

When I turned to look at him over my shoulder, he was Dain again. He grabbed my hand and started running with me down the hall.

Jennifer

For some reason, the hallway was really long and filled with doors, but no matter which door we went into, Luke was there.

Kinsley

Really? What was he doing?

Jennifer

Just … standing there, looking at me.

It had been unnerving, staring into those deep, brown eyes.

Even in my dreams they were probing, as if Dream Luke could see the things I didn’t want anyone else to see.

Jennifer

I couldn’t get away from him, even after I woke up.

Jennifer

Every time I fell back asleep, the dream would replay itself.

Kinsley chewed absently on a piece of bacon.

Kinsley

What do you think it means? Do you have a thing for Luke?

Jennifer

No!

Jennifer

I’m with Dain. I want to be with him.

Kinsley shrugged.

Kinsley

He has one for you, you know.

My mouth dropped open, my fingers tightening around my fork.

Jennifer

What?

Kinsley

Didn’t you know? God, Jenn, everyone knows.

Kinsley

At least everyone in the house does.

Jennifer

How do you know that? Did he say something to you?

Kinsley

He didn’t have to.

Kinsley

It’s so obvious.

Kinsley

He’s always had a soft spot for you, but I think he’s just been too scared to say anything.

Jennifer

Scared? Luke? Kinsley, come on.

Jennifer

I know him just as well as you do, we’ve lived in that apartment together for a while now.

Jennifer

Luke isn’t afraid of any girl.

Kinsley

Not the ones he doesn’t care about.

Kinsley

Think about it, Jenn. He hasn’t had a serious girlfriend since Rebecca.

Rebecca had been Luke’s high school sweetheart.

They’d graduated together, but Rebecca had been accepted to the University of Houston, while Luke had chosen UT.

The long distance relationship had lasted about a year, and had ended when she’d dumped him for another guy.

Luke had been a mess for the first few months after that, and once he’d recovered, Luke the playboy had been born.

Between him and Christian, they’d gone through more girls than an NBA team.

Kinsley

Ever since then, he’s been playing around, but the truth is, he’s pretty much in love with you.

Kinsley

Haven’t you ever noticed how protective he gets when you start dating someone new?

I frowned and tried to remember.

It wasn’t like I’d had a lot of promising prospects lately, but now that Kinsley mentioned it, Luke did tend to act weird around my dates.

Jennifer

The guys are protective of all of us.

Jennifer

They always have been.

Kinsley

It’s different with you for Luke.

Kinsley

Geesh, I can’t believe you didn’t know.

Jennifer

And I can’t believe you didn’t tell me!

Jennifer

All this information would have been really helpful before now.

Jennifer

Maybe I wouldn’t have been so caught off guard when he kissed me.

Kinsley’s eyes gaped wide and her mouth made a round ‘o’ of surprise.

Kinsley

What? When did this happen?

Jennifer

You can’t tell anybody.

Jennifer

Especially not Chloe.

Kinsley

Of course not!

She leaned toward me and smiled as if I’d just given her all the answers to this week’s Calculus test.

Kinsley

When was this?

Jennifer

Friday.

Jennifer

When we were at Kingdom.

Amazingly, her eyes got even wider.

Kinsley

I was wondering where you guys disappeared to.

Jennifer

It kind of just happened.

I confided.

Jennifer

One minute we were all dancing, and somehow the two of us got separated from you, Christian, and Chloe.

Jennifer

He went for it and I …

Kinsley

You what? Did you kiss him back?

How to answer that question without giving Kinsley false hope that something could happen between me and Luke eluded me.

I had no doubt that if she’d known about Luke’s supposed crush on me all this time, she’d been hoping he’d eventually make a move.

Jennifer

Yes, but I felt horrible after.

Jennifer

I think things between Dain and me could really be going somewhere and I almost ruined it.

Kinsley

How was it? Was it good? Better than Dain?

I thought of Dain’s kiss, gentle yet searching, versus Luke’s kiss, possessive and dominating.

It was like apples and oranges.

Jennifer

It was different, not better.

Jennifer

I can’t say it wasn’t good.

Kinsley squealed.

Kinsley

Oh, I knew this would happen!

Jennifer

Nothing is happening.

I stood and loaded all of my trash onto a tray.

Jennifer

So forget I told you.

Jennifer

If Luke really does like me, he has a funny way of showing it.

Jennifer

Instead of plowing through half the cheer squad, he could have come to me before and said something.

Jennifer

Now, he’s only speaking up because he’s jealous of Dain and I’m not about to let that ruin my new relationship.

Jennifer

If jealousy is motivating him, then he’s coming after me for the wrong reasons.

Jennifer

I don’t want someone who wants me because he doesn’t want to see me with anyone else. I think I deserve better than that.

Kinsley followed suit, draining her Styrofoam coffee cup before slinging her backpack over her shoulder.

Kinsley

You’re right.

Kinsley

I guess I didn’t see it that way.

Kinsley

Dain’s a nice guy and you’ve been happy since you started dating him.

Kinsley

Forget I said anything about Luke.

Easier said than done, when he was everywhere, all the time.
