V-Card - Part 46

I found Luke still on the porch with Jared and my dad.

Dad smiled when I approached.

Dad

Hey, princess.

He wrapped an arm around me and pulled me against his side.

A cigar rested in an ashtray on the porch rail, its smell mingling with the scent of cedar and grass.

Jennifer

Hi, Daddy.

Dad

I was just talking about heading off to the kitchen to help your mom.

Dad

I’ll see you in the morning, okay?

Jennifer

Okay.

He leaned down to kiss the top of my head.

Dad

By the way,

Dad

I really like your boyfriend.

Dad

But don’t tell him I said that.

Dad

I’m enjoying giving him a hard time.

I giggled.

Jennifer

Daddy!

He shrugged.

Dad

It’s my right as a father and you’re my only little girl.

Dad

Don’t take this away from me.

Jennifer

Don’t be too hard on him.

He stuck his tongue out at me.

Dad

You’re no fun.

Good night, guys!

Jared and Luke stood on the other side of the porch.

Luke has brought his guitar from the car and was leaning against the rail, strumming on it absently.

Dad

Luke, I keep a loaded shotgun by the bed. So, no funny business.

The look on Luke’s face was priceless as he straightened like a soldier standing in front of his superior officer.

Luke

Yes, sir.

I hid a smile behind my hand.

Dad winked and then put out his cigar before turning to go into the house.

I sauntered to where the guys were debating who’d been a better lead singer, Mick Jagger or Freddie Mercury.

Jared was spinning his drumsticks around his fingers expertly, flipping them from one hand to the other with practiced flare.

Luke held a guitar pick between his teeth, shifting it around as he talked.

Jared

Dude, no way, Jagger’s the man!

Jared

The look, the voice, the moves … come on!

Luke shrugged.

Luke

Jagger’s cool, but Freddie’s a true artist.

Luke

He had the moves.

Jared

Seriously? Moves Like Jagger?

Jared

I mean, Jagger was so iconic they wrote a song just about his dance moves.

Jared

You can’t beat that.

Jennifer

You boys gonna have a fight?

I joked as I joined them near the rail.

Jennifer

Drumsticks versus guitar, in a battle to the death?

Jared swung out with one of his drumsticks, tapping me light on the hand.

Jared

Shut up, squirt.

Jared

So, how’s my future wife, Chloe?

I rolled my eyes and shook my head.

Jared had a huge crush on Chloe, who wouldn’t give him the time of day.

The last time they’d been around each other he’d harped on her so much she’d emptied a glass of lemonade over his head.

Jennifer

There’s a match made in Hell if ever there was one.

I shook my head.

Jared grinned.

Jared

She wants me, she’s just playing hard to get.

Jared

Tell her I’m waiting when she’s ready.

Luke laughed at that and struck a chord on his guitar, still chewing on the guitar pick.

I shot Jared a glance that said ‘get lost’, and he quickly took the hint.

He forced a yawn and stood, stretching.

Jared

Wow, I’m beat. I think I’ll head up to bed.

Jared

Later, Luke. Squirt.

I made a face at his back as he retreated, still spinning one of his drumsticks.
