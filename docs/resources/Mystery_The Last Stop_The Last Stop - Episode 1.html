The Last Stop - Episode 1

Thomas

Mom, I’m going to be a little late.

Thomas

I fell asleep and missed my stop.

Thomas

Can you wait there for me at the station?

Mom

Honestly, Thomas.

Mom

Why do you keep doing this?

Mom

Yes, I’ll wait, but hurry up.

Mom

I’ve got a Skype interview in 30 minutes.

Thomas

I’m sorry!

Thomas

We’re getting close to the park.

Thomas

I’ll get on the first train back.

Thomas

I should be there in 10 minutes, okay?

Mom

When are you going to grow up?

Thomas

You’re right mom…sorry!

Thomas

Don’t be mad!

Thomas

Oh no! The train didn’t stop!

Mom

Jesus, Thomas! Did you press the button?

Thomas

I did! I swear I did!

Thomas

I don’t know what’s going on.

Thomas

We’re going so fast now.

Thomas

What should I do?

Mom

I don’t know. Ask somebody!

Thomas

There’s no one left in my car!

Thomas

Should I keep pressing the button?

Mom

Yes.

Mom

Do you see any people in the other car?

Thomas

Yeah, I see the ticket collector.

Thomas

Should I go talk to him?

Mom

Yes!

Mom

The train is probably going back to the depot.

Mom

Ask him what you should do.

Three minutes later.

Mom

Are you there? What did the ticket collector say?

Mom

Thomas?

Thomas

Mom…I’m scared!

Thomas

He…disappeared!

Mom

Thomas, this isn’t funny.

Thomas

I know! It’s scary!

Thomas

I went to the other car and he wasn’t there anymore.

Thomas

It was like he just…vanished!

Mom

Oh come on, Thomas!

Mom

What did you do at school?

Mom

Did you smoke pot?

Mom

Did you take something?

Thomas

Mom, I did NOT!

Thomas

I’m telling you he disappeared.

Thomas

And the train is going faster and faster!

Thomas

I don’t know where we are anymore!

Mom

Okay, don’t worry Tommy.

Mom

Just walk toward the front of the train.

Mom

You can ask the conductor what’s going on, okay?

Thomas

Alright. I’m in the back, It’s going to take a few minutes.

Mom

I’ll be here.

Five minutes later.

Thomas

Oh my God, Mom.

Mom

What?

Thomas

There is NO CONDUCTOR!!

Mom

This has to be a joke.

Mom

Thomas, you are going to be so grounded.

Thomas

Mom, I promise this is not a joke!

Thomas

I’m scared! What’s going on?

Mom

Which train did you get on, Tommy?

Mom

What was the destination?

Thomas

There was no destination.

Thomas

I just jumped on this train because I was late!

Thomas

I thought it was the usual train!

Mom

Well, it’s obviously not.

Mom

Give me some more information about it.

Mom

I can ask them which one it is here at the station.

Thomas

The train left from North Square at 8:05 p.m.

Thomas

That’s all I know.

Thomas

Mom, I’m panicking, please help me!

Mom

Hold on, let me talk to the people at the station and I’ll get back to you.

Mom

Stay calm, there’s nothing to worry about.

Thomas

Okay, but HURRY UP!

Thomas

I think the train is going even faster!

Mom

Look out the window.

Mom

Do you recognize where you are?

Thomas

It’s dark.

Thomas

I can’t see anything.

Thomas

Trees…bushes…nothing else.

Mom

Okay, the people at the station are looking up your train.

Mom

Hold on.

Thomas

Oh my God, it feels like it’s derailing!

Mom

I’m sure it’s nothing, sweetie.

Thomas

How can it be nothing?

Thomas

What did they say, Mom?

Thomas

Where is this train going?

Mom

They haven’t found which train it is yet.

Mom

Keep talking to me!

Thomas

What do you mean they haven’t found it yet?

Thomas

It’s going at a crazy speed.

Thomas

And I don’t think it’s on a track anymore!!

Mom

They’ll find it, sweetie. Hang tight!

Thomas

Mom, I see something!

Thomas

It’s a lake or the ocean.

Thomas

I can’t tell which.

Thomas

There’s a lot of water though.

Mom

Let me tell them that.

Mom

Keep looking out the window and tell me what you see.

Thomas

We’re crossing a bridge. It’s foggy.

Thomas

There’s a white light.

Thomas

Oh my God, I’m so scared.

One minute later.

Thomas

I just saw someone on the train!

Thomas

The ticket collector from before.

Thomas

What should I do? Should I go over to him?

Mom

Yes, go talk to him!

Thomas

I’m going.

Thomas

Keep your phone close.

Mom

I’m here honey.

Thomas goes into the next car and approaches the ticket collector.

The man looks at Thomas and smiles gently.

Ticket Collector

Hello, son.

Ticket Collector

I’ve been waiting for you.

Thomas

Can you tell me what train I’m on?

Thomas

And where it’s going?

Thomas

I need to get back home.

Ticket Collector

Thomas, I’m afraid you won’t be able to go back home.

Thomas

Wh…Why not?

Ticket Collector

You didn’t fall asleep earlier.

Ticket Collector

That isn’t why you missed your train.

Ticket Collector

You had a brain aneurysm.

Thomas

What?

Ticket Collector

I’m afraid so.

Ticket Collector

And it’s going to be fatal.

Ticket Collector

You’re on the journey that takes you to the other side.

Thomas

But I’ve been texting with my mom all this time!

Thomas

How can that be?

Ticket Collector

Sometimes, when it takes a while for people to pass…

Ticket Collector

They are able to communicate with the person closest to them.

Ticket Collector

That’s what is happening now.

Ticket Collector

But you don’t have much time.

Ticket Collector

You need to say goodbye.

Thomas sees the whitish light outside the train window become whiter.

Then he turns and sees that the ticket collector has vanished again.

A sense of calm suddenly envelops him, and he texts his mother.

Thomas

Mom, I found out what happened.

Mom

Thomas, I just got a call from the hospital!

Mom

They said you had a brain aneurysm.

Mom

You’re not on some crazy train.

Mom

You’re delirious!

Mom

I’m in a taxi now.

Mom

I’ll be at the hospital soon.

Mom

I love you.

Mom

I know I haven’t been good about showing it lately.

Thomas

Mom, I love you too.

Mom

I feel terrible that I said those things to you.

Mom

Thomas, I’ve prioritized my job over you for too long.

Mom

From now on, I’m going to be a better mother, I promise.

Thomas

Thanks, Mom. I know you would’ve done your best.

Thomas

I love you so much.

Thomas

Goodbye, for now.

Thomas

And when this train comes for you someday.

Thomas

I’ll meet you at the last stop.
