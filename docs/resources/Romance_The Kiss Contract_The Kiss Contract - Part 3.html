Something dumb like that.

It’s about time you cash in on one of the nights you’ve helped me.

Not quite.

Actually, who am I kidding? Not at all.

She’s going on a date tonight with someone else.

WHAAAT?

I know.

Goddammit!

All right, dude, chill.

I’m coming over.

Less than a minute later, Luke is at Neil’s office.

He has one of his arms stuffed into his blazer,

And he’s still putting a notebook in his bag.

Luke

I came as fast as I could.

Neil

I can see that.

Listen, thank you.

No need for that, mon frère.

Where should we go?

Not McKellan’s, right?

I know that’s where you usually go with Kim.

Neil

Actually, that’s EXACTLY where I think we should go.

Neil

Kim was being super sketchy about where they were going tonight.

Neil

And if she takes him to the place she goes to with me…

Luke

I get it, man. Let’s go.

They race down the street and throw open the door to McKellan’s Pub.

And there, at the bar, is a sight that makes Neil’s stomach drop to the floor.

It’s Kim, twirling her straw as she talks to a handsome blonde man,

And they’re sitting in THEIR spot - the exact same spot from last night.

Donny, THEIR favorite bartender, is chatting happily with them.

It’s as if they’re living in Neil’s dream world.

Luke

Dude, don’t torture yourself.

Luke

Let’s just go to another bar. There are so many places around here.

Neil feels his jaw tighten.

Neil

No. We’re staying.

Neil

Look, there’s a whole table of women in the back corner.

Neil

Let’s buy them a round of drinks.

Luke

Are you sure that’s the best thing for you to do right now?

Neil

I said, let’s buy them a round of drinks.

Luke

Jesus. Okay man, whatever you say.

Neil swiftly orders a bottle of La Mondotte, speaking loudly so Kim can hear.

He knows it’s petty, but he can feel her eyes on him

And he can’t help but relish the sensation.

When the girls smile and wave him over, he turns to Kim, shrugs,

And then makes his way to their table.

Neil

Hello.

Woman

Hello yourself. Aren’t you going to sit down?

Neil

I think I should know your name first.

Woman

That didn’t stop you from sending over a very expensive bottle of Merlot.

Neil

I had to make sure you’d want to talk to me.

Woman

Listen, I’d want to talk to you without the wine. I’m Rosalind.

Neil

Neil.

Neil

It’s very nice to meet you.

Rosalind

Likewise - now have a seat.

Kim watches Neil slide into the booth full of stunning women

And tries to keep her eyes on Brad.

Kim

So, HR is your passion, huh?

Brad

It really is. I just love people.

Brad

People are the building blocks of a good company.

Brad

Without good, happy people…

But Kim is having a hard time concentrating on Brad’s words.

She watches Neil press up against a beautiful woman,

And her jaw drops as the woman’s arm wraps around Neil’s neck.

But Kim knows she shouldn’t be shocked -

Because, of course, what single woman wouldn’t want to be with Neil?

With HER Neil.

Brad

Kim, why are you standing?

Brad

Where are you going?

But Kim doesn’t even hear the words coming out of Brad’s mouth.

Instead, she’s focusing on Neil’s lips…

As they move closer to the woman next to him.
