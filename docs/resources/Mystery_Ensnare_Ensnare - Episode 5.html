Ensnare - Episode 5

The morning after the Governor’s Ball, Angie receives a text from the Source…

Unknown

We’ve received the pictures.

Angie flashes back to the Senator’s hands on her hips—

His tongue in her mouth—

The click of Kyle’s camera.

She took a thirty-minute shower afterward, scrubbing her skin raw.

Unknown

We know this must have been a very degrading experience for you.

Unknown

But we appreciate your cooperation and will see that your efforts are rewarded.

Unknown

We have one more task for you to complete.

Angie

I don’t think I can.

Unknown

We understand the previous task was abhorrent.

Unknown

But we would not be asking for this next task if it weren’t absolutely necessary.

Unknown

We promise you, it is much simpler.

Angie

What do you need me to do?

Unknown

There is a blue file on the desk in your father’s bedroom.

Unknown

Inside, there are three pages marked BAR — Burn After Reading.

Unknown

He and your mother are currently taking coffee in the sitting room.

Angie

How do you know that?

Unknown

That information is classified.

Unknown

We need you to take a picture of the three pages and text them to this number.

Angie

Why? What’s in the file?

Unknown

The intelligence we need to ensure that our agenda is being handled correctly.

Angie

What agenda?

Unknown

That information is classified.

Angie switches to Kyle’s text chain.

Angie

Why does the Source want me to take pictures of a file on my father’s desk?

Kyle

I don’t know. Did you ask them?

Angie

They said the information was classified.

Kyle

Then that’s all you need to know.

Angie

Nope, sorry. I’m not going to spy on my dad…

Angie

Or on the FEDERAL GOVERNMENT with only that information.

Kyle

Angie, there’s something I should tell you.

Kyle

You know how I told you the Source has been running the government for the past century?

Angie

Yeah…so?

Kyle

That means they usually succeed in getting the president they want elected into office.

Kyle

But sometimes, a dark horse comes from out of nowhere and gets elected.

Kyle

That’s what happened with your dad.

Angie

Wait, my dad isn’t part of the Source?

Angie

I mean…I just assumed when you said the entire government was run by them…

Kyle

No. He has no idea that they’ve been running the government.

Kyle

He doesn’t even know that they exist.

Kyle

When your dad was elected, the Source lost the influence they’ve been so carefully crafting over the years.

Kyle

A lot can change in the 4-8 years your dad is going to be president.

Kyle

The Source needs someone particularly close to him to give them information.

Kyle

It’s the only way they can stay one step ahead of him and keep their agendas afloat.

Angie

So you’re asking me to go behind my dad’s back?

Angie

To mess up the plans he has for his presidency?

Angie

My dad was elected because the American people decided it was time for a change.

Angie

People didn’t like the way things were running in Washington.

Angie

I refuse to work against my dad and the wishes of the American people.

Kyle

The American people don’t know what’s good for them.

Angie

But our system of government says that they do.

Angie

If I go against it, I go against everything I believe in.

Kyle

Our system of government is outdated.

Kyle

There are too many people in this country to let them in on what really goes on behind closed doors.

Kyle

They have no clue when it comes to the nitty, gritty details of political reality.

Kyle

To them, you’re either “for” or “against” something.

Kyle

To those of us who know better, there is no “for” or “against.”

Kyle

There’s simply “point,” “counterpoint,” and “compromise.”

Angie

I know. That’s what keeps the country moving.

Kyle

You know that because you’re an insider.

Kyle

And look, I appreciate why your dad got elected — I really do…

Kyle

But this is real-world stuff we’re talking about here.

Kyle

If the Source doesn’t get the information they need—

Kyle

Just because the American people thought they knew better, even though they know nothing at all—

Kyle

Then a lot of bad things can happen.

Kyle

VERY quickly.

Angie

You know what?

Angie

I don’t care.

Angie

I’m out.

Kyle

Wait, Ang—

Angie flips back to her text chain with UNKNOWN, ignoring Kyle’s texts.

Angie

Sorry, but I don’t feel comfortable doing that.

Angie

You’ll have to find someone else.

Unknown

You understand that by refusing this task—

Unknown

You are refusing all of the benefits that come with membership.

Unknown

Including our personal guarantee that you will go to the law school of your choice.

Angie

I don’t care.

Unknown

You won’t be given the internships, clerkships, and job offers of your choice, either.

Unknown

We had planned to make you a Supreme Court Justice by the time you’re thirty-five.

Angie

35? Isn’t that a little young?

Unknown

There is no age requirement for joining the bench.

Unknown

Joseph Story was thirty-two when he became a justice in 1811.

Angie

That was 1811!

Unknown

Exactly. Imagine what he could have accomplished if he had the power of the Source behind him.

Angie

Is there another task I can do?

Unknown

No.

Unknown

The tasks are not arbitrary.

Unknown

This is the task we have assigned for you because you are the only one who can complete it.

Angie

I’m sorry. I can’t do what you’re asking me to do — it’s going too far.

Angie

I’ll just have to make my dreams happen on my own.

Unknown

Then you leave us no choice.

Unknown

We have a video of you participating in underage drinking at a Thornhollow Party.

Unknown

Not only are you undeniably intoxicated in the video, but you’re playing a very lewd game.

Unknown

A game which encouraged you to say some very offensive and perverted things.

Unknown

When taken out of context, those soundbites will make you look like a drunken bigot, at best.

Angie

That’s blackmail!

Unknown

We’ll do what needs to be done.

Angie

Don’t — please. I remember that party.

Angie

I was new, and Kyle took me to it to help me meet people.

Angie

I was really nervous…I don’t usually drink.

Angie

And besides, that’s a common game. People will know I didn’t really mean those things.

Unknown

Our video is a close up of your face.

Unknown

There is no evidence you are playing a game.

Unknown

We can easily make it look like you meant every word you said.

Unknown

No law school, clerkship, or firm will want you.

Unknown

If that video gets out, you’ll certainly never become a Supreme Court Justice.

Angie

You can’t threaten me.

Angie

So what if I don’t become a Supreme Court Justice?

Angie

At least I won’t have to betray my dad’s trust.

Unknown

What do you think he’ll say when these soundbites leak in conjunction with the news of your unexpected pregnancy?

Unknown

Without our protection, the news about your pregnancy and plans for abortion will become public knowledge.

Unknown

You’ll be lucky if Georgetown doesn’t revoke the acceptance letter you received last week.

Unknown

Your father’s approval ratings will be in the gutter.

Unknown

And your choices will hang over his presidency for the rest of time.

Angie

Stop. Why are you saying this to me?

Unknown

Because we have the power to make your future and your father’s legacy disappear in an instant.

Unknown

We recommend that you do all you can to prevent that from happening.

Unknown

We’re not asking for much.

Unknown

Just send us a few pictures of a file we should have had in our hands all along.

Unknown

Do you think you can do that?

Angie

Fine. I’ll do it.
