V-Card - Part 20

His nostrils flared as he inhaled noisily.

Luke

Sure.

His voice was clipped and abrupt.

Luke

Of course I do.

Luke

Don’t worry about it, it was nothing.

Luke

A reflex.

I nodded.

Jennifer

That’s what I thought.

I brushed past him to leave the kitchen, but his arm shot out and his hand grasped my elbow.

I paused, forced to look at him as he stared me down.

Luke

You shouldn’t believe everything a guy tells you.

Luke

Not even Dain.

Luke

Especially not him.

My eyes narrowed, but I didn’t say anything as I pulled away from his hold.

Without another word I left the kitchen, retrieving my backpack before leaving the house altogether.

I found Christian out front, talking on his cell phone.

Christian

Sure, Mom.

He nodded his head and rolled his eyes.

Christian

Yeah, that sounds great …

Christian

Okay, I gotta go, Mom. I have a midterm in half an hour … Bye.

He groaned in frustration as he ended the call and shoved the phone into the pocket of his jeans.

I laughed as we left together, both heading toward the main campus.

Jennifer

Isn’t it a little early for mom drama?

Christian shook his head.

Christian

Why don’t you try telling her that? I swear, nothing is good enough for that woman.

Jennifer

The ole ‘you should go to law school instead of being a coach’ speech?

His jaw clenched as he nodded.

Christian

What else? If it’s not that, it’s the apartment I live in, or the people out with, or the fact that I’m almost a senior and don’t have a single prospect for marriage yet.

Jennifer

Your parents have unreachable standards.

Christian

Tell me about it.

Christian

Anyway, I don’t want to talk about that.

Jennifer

Okay, what do you want to talk about?

Christian shot me a sly glance.

Christian

We could talk about Dain.

I smiled at the mention of Dain and my skin tingled as it remembered his wandering hands.

Jennifer

Only if you’re going to be nice about it.

Christian

Dain’s my friend, and a good guy.

Christian

If you two like each other I’m cool with it. Why wouldn’t I be?

Jennifer

Why don’t you ask Luke?

I scoffed.

Jennifer

He went all caveman on me last night when he thought me and Dain had … well, it’s not his business what we did or didn’t do.

Christian’s eyebrows shot up.

Christian

Really?

Jennifer

Yeah.

I took a sip of my coffee.

Jennifer

I don’t know what his deal is. For some reason he doesn’t like Dain.

Christian laughed.

Christian

They’re both blond and good looking.

Christian

Guys don’t like other roosters in their henhouse.

I paused on the sidewalk, hands on hips and one eyebrow raised.

Jennifer

Do I look like a hen to you?

He shrugged.

Christian

You know what I mean. He’s just being protective.

Christian

You guys are like our sisters or something. We don’t want anybody messing with you.

Christian

I’d probably act the same way if I didn’t know Dain.

Christian

I’ve never seen him disrespect a girl he’s with, so you’re in good hands.

I’d known that all along, but hearing Christian reaffirm it helped a lot.

Especially if he was going to be the one.

We reached the quad, which branched off toward different areas of campus.

Christian needed to go left, and I was headed right.

Christian

See you later, Jenn.

Christian

Cut Luke some slack, okay? After a while he’ll get used to Dain and things will go back to normal.

Jennifer

Fine.

Normal … I could do normal.

As long as Luke kept his shirt on.
