For the Love of Pete - Episode 1

Travis

Hey, this is Travis. Jack’s lawyer gave me your number.

Travis

I’m going to come by and pick up Pete later today.

Hayley

Right. Joint custody. What was Jack thinking?

Hayley

Why would he leave his beloved dog to two people who don’t even know each other?

Travis

Who knows why Jack did any of the things he did?

Travis

Like going hang gliding in Costa Rica and getting killed.

Hayley

I know.

Travis

Damn it. He was my best friend.

Travis

You there?

Hayley

Yes.

Hayley

I’m sorry you lost him.

Travis

Yeah, me too.

Hayley

Jack and I worked together. He was a good friend to me too.

Hayley

Listen, let’s not do this over texts.

Hayley

For today, why don’t we make the exchange at the Washington Square Park dog run at 3?

Hayley

We can discuss future arrangements there.

Travis

Ok. See you then.

Later that day at 3, Travis sees a pretty brunette sitting on a bench at the dog run.

He tries to adopt a businesslike tone.

Travis

Hayley?

Travis

I’m Travis.

Hayley stands and looks up at Travis as the two shake hands.

Travis is tall, with dark hair and piercing blue eyes and a light dusting of facial hair.

She does her best to sound nonchalant.

Hayley

Pete’s just over by that tree.

Travis

I see him.

Travis

Pete! Over here, buddy!

Pete, a chubby white Frenchie, comes lumbering over on short wrinkly knees.

His large ears are flapping, and his little knob of a tail is moving back and forth in his excitement to see Travis.

Travis bends to greet him.

Travis

Hello, old friend. It’s good to see you.

Travis

How do you know Pete? Did you and Jack…?

Hayley

No, nothing like that. We were friends.

Hayley

Jack used to bring Pete to the office.

Hayley

He mentioned you a few times.

Travis

Oh yeah?

He straightens and smiles at her.

Travis

Good stuff, I hope.

Hayley

He said you went to college together.

Hayley

That you’re single.

Hayley

That you’re a member of the famous Callahan family.

Hayley

And that you’re something of a ladies’ man.

Hayley

Did I get any of it wrong?

Travis

Nope.

Travis

He mentioned you a few times too.

Hayley

Oh?

Travis

He said you’re smart, stubborn and single.

Hayley

That sounds like how Jack would describe me.

Hayley

He was always trying to fix me up.

Hayley

But I don’t have time for a man in my life right now.

Travis

But you do have time for a dog?

Hayley

What do you mean?

Travis

You lead a busy life in Manhattan.

Travis

This is no place for a dog to live out half his life alone waiting for you to come home.

Hayley

Really? Because he was with me at work all day, sleeping under my desk.

Travis

Sleeping under a desk. Nice.

Travis

He should be running around outside having fun.

Travis

The way he can in Connecticut.

Hayley

You think all these dogs at the dog run are unhappy?

Hayley

I take him here every day.

Hayley

You should let him spend all year here.

Hayley

All his friends are here, after all.

Travis

Sorry, but you’re not getting rid of me that easily.

Travis

We can get lawyers involved, but I don’t have time for that.

Travis

I also don’t have time to be driving four hours round trip every weekend for an exchange.

Hayley

Okay, so let’s do monthly exchanges and see how that works out.

Travis looks at Hayley and shakes his head.

Travis

Here I am with no kids, and yet I’m in a custody battle.

Hayley rolls her eyes.

Hayley

This is hardly a battle.

Hayley

Oh, quick, look at Pete!

Travis and Hayley both laugh as Pete holds one end of a stick between his teeth while a Great Dane holds the other end.

Pete fends off the giant dog and runs away with the prize in his jaws.

Then he takes a tumble on his short legs, somersaults, and comes back up with the stick still in his mouth.

Hayley

He’s seriously so cute.

Travis

He’s not afraid of anything.

Out of the corner of his eye, Travis sees a man in black jeans and a red cap worn low over his eyes.

He doesn’t seem to be watching any dog in particular.

Travis points him out to Hayley.

Travis

Which dog does that guy belong to?

Hayley

Hmm, I don’t know. He looks a little suspicious.

Hayley

Let’s keep an eye on him.

Travis

Maybe we should put Pete on his leash.

Securing Pete to his lead, they walk him to the grass and sit down, continuing to watch the lone man.

Pete stares at them both inquisitively with his soulful brown eyes.

Hayley

Do you ever wonder what he’s thinking about?

Travis

Sticks.

Hayley

I think he misses Jack.

Travis

We all do. He was a good guy.

Hayley

Yes. He was.

Hayley

So are you okay making monthly exchanges for now?

Travis

For now, I guess.

Travis

But I’m not sure it will be good for Pete.

Travis

Every time he settles in, he’ll be uprooted again for another month.

Hayley

It’s better than the weekly alternative of never settling in at all.

Travis

Even better is if we can decide on a more permanent agreement.

Travis

Like me keeping him full time.

Hayley

Nope, sorry. I’m not giving him up.

Travis

Neither am I.

Hayley

So then the exchanges will take place every month. For now.

Pete has finally given up on getting Travis and Hayley to move and joins them on the grass.

Since he has no tail, he’s able to sit completely on his backside, leaving his back legs splayed out beneath him.

Travis turns to Hayley to see her reaction.

She’s already holding up her phone, taking pictures.

When Pete scoots over to Travis and climbs onto his lap, she takes a picture of that too.
