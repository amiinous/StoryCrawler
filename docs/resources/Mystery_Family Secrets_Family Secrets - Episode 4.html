Family Secrets - Episode 4

Virginia Pynchon

Are you surprised to see this?

The old woman raises her hand to the right side of her face…

Where a long angry scar runs from the side of her eye to her mouth.

Hannah

I…

Ms. Pynchon

Usually, I have special makeup that covers it.

Ms. Pynchon

But I don’t want to cover up things in front of you.

Hannah

How did it happen, Ms. Pynchon?

Ms. Pynchon

Hannah, I want to tell you the story…

Ms. Pynchon

Well, one day I HOPE to tell you the story.

Ms. Pynchon

But for now, won’t you sit down?

Ms. Pynchon

Would you like some tea?

What Hannah would really like is a big plate of scrambled eggs…

And some buttered toast.

But she accepts Ms. Pynchon’s offer.

Ms. Pynchon

I’m well aware of my reputation, Hannah.

Ms. Pynchon

ROB they call me.

Ms. Pynchon

Ruthless Old Bitch.

Ms. Pynchon

But I needed to seem that way to get ahead in business.

Hannah

I’m not sure that’s true, Ms. Pynchon.

Hannah

Especially not in today’s world.

Ms. Pynchon

Already disagreeing with me, are you?

Ms. Pynchon

I like that.

Ms. Pynchon

I wish more people at my company had the courage to stand up to me.

Ms. Pynchon

I’m glad I chose you to write my biography.

Hannah

Ms. Pynchon, why did you decide to have a biography written in the first place?

Hannah

Your family has always been so…

Hannah

Secretive.

Hannah

There is so little information on the Pynchons on the Internet.

Ms. Pynchon

Yes, and I have hired people to keep it that way.

Ms. Pynchon

It’s true, we do like our privacy, but…

Before she can continue, the drawing room door opens.

A handsome, gray-haired man in his 70s enters.

Ms. Pynchon

Lionel! I told you the meeting isn’t until 10 a.m.

Lionel

Yes darling, but I was wondering if you could write a check for me.

Lionel

There’s a Swiss watch I saw in a catalog.

Ms. Pynchon

Hannah, I’d like you to meet my husband, Lionel.

Ms. Pynchon

He married me for my money.

Lionel

Now, Ginny.

Ms. Pynchon

You know there’s no need to pretend, dear.

Ms. Pynchon

Not after all these years.

Ms. Pynchon

I married you for your looks, after all.

Ms. Pynchon

And that’s almost as bad.

Lionel

Whatever you say, darling.

Lionel

Now about the watch.

Ms. Pynchon

How much is it?

Lionel

Only $10,000.

Ms. Pynchon

Fortunately, Lionel here never learned how to use the Internet.

Ms. Pynchon

And I keep a firm grip on the checkbook and credit cards.

Ms. Pynchon

So that helps keep my costs down.

Lionel

If you could, make the check out to Patek Philippe & Co., darling.

Ms. Pynchon

There you are, dear.

Ms. Pynchon

Now please leave me alone with Hannah.

Ms. Pynchon

And return at 10 a.m. for the all meeting.

Check in hand, Lionel tries to kiss Virginia.

But she turns away.

Ms. Pynchon

What a useless man.

Ms. Pynchon

My first husband, Allan, was so different.

Ms. Pynchon

I’m sure you know I took over the business after he died.

Hannah

Yes, that much is on the Internet.

Hannah

You built Greathall Industries into what it is today.

Ms. Pynchon

Yes. And I attracted Lionel in the process.

Ms. Pynchon

Well, it’s not accurate to say that I attracted him.

Ms. Pynchon

It was my money that got him to marry me.

Hannah

Do you really think it was ONLY your money?

Ms. Pynchon

Oh, yes.

Ms. Pynchon

I was too devastated by my first husband’s death to see it.

Ms. Pynchon

I was lonely and fell for his superficial charms.

Hannah

I’m sorry.

Ms. Pynchon

Lionel’s purpose in life is to sponge off my fortune.

Ms. Pynchon

And he does nothing in return.

Ms. Pynchon

Except look good.

Ms. Pynchon

I was never his match in the looks department.

Ms. Pynchon

At my peak, I was at best considered a handsome woman.

Ms. Pynchon

Without my money, I would have been far beneath Lionel’s interest.

Ms. Pynchon pauses as if she’s about to say something.

But then thinks better of it.

Hannah

So, you were about to tell me why you wanted this book to be written.

Ms. Pynchon

Oh, yes.

Ms. Pynchon

As I get older, I’m beginning to realize that it’s important for certain secrets to be told.

Hannah

Such as?

Ms. Pynchon

Such as, when a mother…

Ms. Pynchon

Goodness! What was that noise?

Hannah

It was…my stomach.

Hannah

I didn’t get any breakfast this morning.

Ms. Pynchon

Didn’t Bernadette tell you about the buffet?

Hannah

Yes, but I got there at 8:01, and all the food had been removed.

Ms. Pynchon, exasperated, rings a bell.

Bernadette quickly appears.

Ms. Pynchon

I heard you refused Hannah breakfast because she was one minute late.

Ms. Pynchon

That is no way to treat our guest!

Ms. Pynchon

Bring some scrambled eggs and toast out immediately.

Bernadette

Yes, ma’am. I’m sorry, ma’am.

She glares at Hannah and leaves the room.

Ms. Pynchon

You’ll have to excuse Bernadette.

Ms. Pynchon

She’s had a tragic past.

Hannah

That’s what Lucinda told me too.

Ms. Pynchon

Oh, so you met Lucinda!

Hannah

Yes, she was nice enough to stop by and introduce herself last night.

Hannah

She’s your son Eric’s daughter, right?

Ms. Pynchon

Yes.

Hannah

And what about Lucinda’s mother?

Ms. Pynchon

Eric’s wife died giving birth to her.

Hannah

Wow, that’s really sad.

Ms. Pynchon

Yes. Eric became even more aimless and irresponsible after his wife died.

Ms. Pynchon

I don’t even know where he is right now.

Ms. Pynchon

He’s probably halfway across the country or the world.

Ms. Pynchon

Probably drinking and doing drugs.

Ms. Pynchon

The family ambition skipped a generation and went straight to Lucinda.

Ms. Pynchon

She will probably lead my foundation one day.

Ms. Pynchon

Long ago, I had hoped…it would be someone else.

Hannah

Who?

Ms. Pynchon

My daughter.

Ms. Pynchon

But my daughter died.
