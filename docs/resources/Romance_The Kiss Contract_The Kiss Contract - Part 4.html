The Kiss Contract - Part 4

Kim

STOP!

Abruptly, Rosalind pulls away from Neil. She swings her head toward Kim.

And then turns back at Neil, narrowing her eyes at him.

Rosalind

Who the hell is that?

Kim

I’m the girl who’s sleeping with him.

Rosalind

Seriously?!

Rosalind

Here I thought I’d finally found the one nice guy left in New York.

Rosalind

Neil, you’re just a two-timing jerk like all the others.

To his credit, Neil doesn’t say a thing as Rosalind storms away.

He simply gets up from the table and watches her go, shouldering the blame.

And to Kim, it’s the sexiest thing she’s ever seen.

She takes a step closer to Neil,

Until their bodies are just a breath away from contact.

Neil

Who knew that you were one of those crazy girls, huh?

Kim

Haha, hasn’t anyone ever taught you that you should never call a girl crazy?

Neil

You know that I was joking.

Neil

Besides, you weren’t being crazy. You were…

Kim

Jealous.

Neil

Jealous?

Kim

Oh, stop fishing for compliments!

Kim

Yes, I was jealous. Because I’m the only one who gets to do this…

Kim pushes up on her toes, pressing her lips against Neil’s.

Hungry for him, she tangles her tongue with his,

And In each stroke, she gives him a promise that she cares.

The world around them seems to disappear

Until they part slowly, savoring every moment of the kiss.

Neil

Wow…

Neil

That was amazing. I…

Neil

Do you want to go somewhere?

Kim searches Neil’s eyes, and then catches a movement by the door.

It’s Brad, skulking away.

Kim wanted Neil before, but now - she’s got nothing to lose.

She grabs Neil’s hand.

The same jolt of electricity pulses through them,

Just like it did the night before.

Kim

Where to?

Neil

I know the perfect place - but I can’t just TELL you.

Neil

It has to be a surprise.

Kim

When did you become such a romantic?

Neil

Maybe you never looked hard enough.

Neil

Follow me.

New York City transforms into a playground as they race out the door.

They reach the green lawn of Battery Park

And take in the glow of the Statue of Liberty against the sunset.

Through the twinkles of fireflies, Neil points to Castle Clinton

Standing proud and stoic.

Neil

Come on! Over there.

Kim

What? We can go IN there?

Neil

Sure we can - it’s open to the public.

Kim

I don’t know, it looks kinda dark, but…

Kim

Race you there!

Kim runs so hard that her legs burn.

She can’t remember the last time she had this much fun -

Probably when she was a child.

When she reaches the doorway before Neil,

She doubles over laughing.

Kim

I knew it! I knew it’d be closed.

Neil

Huh. I could’ve sworn it stayed open late in the summer.

Kim

Is that so?

Kim

Are you sure this wasn’t all some clever ruse to get me here alone with you?

Neil

I have to say, I wish I HAD been that smooth.

Neil

But listen, how ‘bout we take advantage of our privacy?

Kim

Neil…

Neil

Not to do that.

Neil

Not yet, anyway.

Neil

To do this…

Neil laces his fingers between hers

And gently pulls them down to the ground.

They sit, legs crossed in their business clothes,

Facing one another under the summer sky.

Neil

Let’s talk.

Neil

Because we’re obviously not just friends.

Kim

No, we’re not.

Neil

So what ARE we?

Kim looks down at their lap, where they’re holding hands.

She mindlessly draws a symbol in Neil’s palm,

Not realizing it stands for infinity.

Neil

I know you don’t want me to be your boyfriend, and that’s okay.

Neil

I’ve said it a million times -

Neil

I’m not going to force you to do anything you don’t want to do.

Neil

But if I’m not your boyfriend…

Neil

You can’t exactly get mad if I kiss people, Kim.

Kim

I know.

Kim looks up suddenly, excitedly.

Kim

What if we make a contract?

Neil

A contract?

Neil

You hate contracts.

Kim

Not this one. This one is…

Kim

A Kiss Contract!

Kim

It’s a contract that lists who we’re allowed to kiss.

Neil

Okay, and who’s on the list?

Kim

We’re both on the list.

Kim

We can kiss each other.

Kim

And no one else.

Neil

Kim, it’s probably really stupid for me to even ask this question but…

Neil

How is a Kiss Contract different from dating?

Kim

Because there’s no label,

Kim

So there are no hidden promises…

Kim

Or pressures to move forward, or to build a future.

Kim

We just get to have fun.

Neil

Kim, we DID have fun when we were dating.

Neil

We don’t need to make it a sterile contract.

Kim

But I do - I want to.

Kim

It’s… it’ll make me feel better about the whole thing, okay?

Neil

I don’t know…

Neil

Don’t you think it’s belittling what we have?

Kim

It’s not belittling, it’s making it better.

Kim

Less messy - more about the kisses.

Kim

Please…

Kim

Do it for me?

Neil

Of course.

Neil

I’d do anything for you.

Kim

Then let’s seal it with a kiss.
