Right after David clicks his phone to sleep mode,

He hears a scratching noise at the base of the tree and a gravelly voice that matches it.

It sounds like his roommate—but at the same time, it doesn’t.

There’s something sinister and dark beneath Samson’s usual timbre.

Samson

Don’t play with me, David…

Samson

Turning off your cellphone light isn’t going to help you escape me,

Samson

Because I can see you perfectly fine.

Samson

In fact, let’s make this game fair.

Samson

Turn it on. So we can look each other in the eyes.

Scared speechless, David presses the flashlight button on his phone.

Samson’s figure is illuminated below, and it’s more hideous than David perceived.

On a face full of cuts and bruises,

Every one of Samson’s features seems larger in the sterile, eerie glow of light.

David gasps, and struggles to find his voice.

David

Samson?

David

Is that really you?

Samson

Yeah it’s me, dumbass.

Samson

Now are you going to come down here and face me like a man?

Samson

Or am I going to have to chase you up there?

David doesn’t answer, and instead climbs higher and higher in the tree.

But suddenly, his body lurches. David looks down,

Where Samson has dug all four of his limbs in the trunk.

Samson starts plodding up the tree, and David slides toward the end of his branch—

Which starts to wobble under his weight. Samson screams.

Samson

Give up now, David…

Samson

You can’t escape me!

David

Oh yeah? That’s where you’re wrong.

David closes his eyes, summoning his last bit of bravery,

And lets himself drop from the tree.

He lands—HARD—on the ground, and rolls to the side.

He starts to scurry to his feet when he hears a thud next to him.

Samson has thrown himself off the tree too.

Samson

What the hell, man?

With every sentence Samson speaks, he paces closer to David.

Samson

You are SUCH an asshole.

Samson

Completely selfish.

Samson

I wish I’d never met—

Mary

STOP!

Mary

Back OFF him, you monster! You werewolf!

Both of the guys go completely still,

And then turn their heads toward the sound of her screaming voice.

Mary slams her cab door shut and marches over to them.

But then the clouds shift in the sky,

And the moon gets tucked away from view.

In the same moment that the moon slides back into view,

And then they wait…
