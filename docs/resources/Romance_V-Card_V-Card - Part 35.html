V-Card - Part 35

The door was answered by a tall, blonde amazon dressed as a Greek goddess.

I recognized her from the party at Christian’s parents’ house.

Amber

Chris, honey!

She leaped into his arms and kissed him full on the mouth.

Dain appeared behind her dressed like a pirate.

He looked dashing in his open, white shirt and boots, and was even sporting an eye patch.

Dain

There’s my girl!

He swept me into his arms.

He kissed me like there was no one else in the room.

He smiled as he gazed down at me.

Dain

You’re wearing it.

I fingered the pendant against my chest and nodded.

Jennifer

Yeah.

Dain

Looks great on you. Catwoman?

I shrugged.

Jennifer

Just a cat.

Jennifer

I was Betty Boop earlier, but I got into a whipped cream fight.

He laughed and draped an arm over my shoulders, drawing me further into the room.

Kara clung to Luke as they joined Christian, Chloe, and Kinsley near the kitchen.

Dain had the bar covered in food, and in the kitchen were a keg and a few bottles of liquor.

Dain

You want a drink?

Jennifer

I really shouldn’t.

Dain smiled down at me.

Dain

Of course not. You’re such a good girl.

Dain

You’ll be twenty-one soon, though, right?

Jennifer

Just another month.

Dain

Sweet. On your birthday I’ll get you good and drunk.

Dain

Come on, I want you to meet some of the guys from work.

I followed Dain to the kitchen where a group of people were milling around the beer keg, sipping from red Solo cups.

I met four guys named Charles, Jack, James, and Louis, but after that couldn’t remember which face went with which name.

I munched on chips and drank soda while I watched Dain interact with his guests.

I didn’t want to monopolize him, especially when I knew I’d have all night with him.

He really came alive when there were a lot of people around.

His smile was magnetic and his friends hung on his every word.

A voice from behind me.

Kara

He’s really something.

I turned to find Kara there, watching Dain and his friends over my shoulder.

I fought not to roll my eyes, but my distaste for her must have showed because Kara sighed.

Kara

You don’t like me, do you?

Her question was so unexpected, that for a moment, I was speechless.

Jennifer

I … well, I … of course I like you.

Kara didn’t hold back her eye roll.

Kara

Look, you don’t have to lie to me.

Kara

I get it, okay?

Kara

You and Luke … you’re friends, but there’s more there, I think.

I frowned, spotting Luke and Christian in the living room.

Jennifer

What makes you think that?

Kara

I’m not blind, you know.

Kara

I see the way he looks at you.

Kara

I think the only person that doesn’t know Luke’s in love with you, is you.

I folded my arms across my chest.

Jennifer

If you think Luke is in love with me, why are you with him?

Kara

Oh, we’re not really together.

She lowered her eyes.

Kara

Not that I wouldn’t like us to be, but everyone knows Luke doesn’t do commitment.

It was true.

Everyone did know it.

Which was why I couldn’t figure out why everyone kept trying to play matchmaker between us.

Jennifer

Are you hoping he’ll change for you?

Kara snorted sarcastically.

Kara

I can hope, but it’s an empty hope.

Kara

I know there’s no chance, but I really like him, so I take what I can get.

Kara

It’s pitiful, I know.

Suddenly, Kara didn’t seem so bad.

Her forced smiles hid the truth—she wanted a guy who didn’t really want her back.

Jennifer

You’re not pitiful.

I said gently.

Jennifer

Girls are different than guys.

Jennifer

They can be with a girl and not let their emotions get in the way.

Jennifer

It’s harder for us.

Kara nodded.

Kara

Exactly.

Kara

Anyway, I knew who Luke was from the beginning, so it’s not like he’s leading me on.

Kara

I think he could change, though. For the right person.

She stared at me pointedly, but I shook my head.

Jennifer

I’m with Dain.

I insisted, which was becoming a habit.

Why couldn’t everyone just understand that?

Jennifer

And I’m happy.

Kara smiled, but I could see that it was forced.

Kara

That’s great. He seems like an awesome guy.

I watched Dain laughing with one of his friends, his smile wide.

Jennifer

Yeah, he is.

Kara

Good for you.

Kara

Anyway, I just wanted you to know I don’t have any hard feelings.

She turned to walk away, but I grabbed her shoulder, stopping her.

Jennifer

Thanks.

Jennifer

And I’m sorry if I came off like a bitch.

Jennifer

I just … well, there’s no excuse and I really am sorry.

This time Kara’s smile was genuine.

Kara

It’s fine.

Kara

Thanks, Jenn.
