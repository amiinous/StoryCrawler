V-Card - Part 13

Day 11

Dain showed up for our date ten minutes early.

I was just touching up my lip gloss when the doorbell rang.

Kinsley and Chloe were in my room, one on the bed and the other in my swiveling desk chair.

Chloe Person 7

That outfit is tragic.

Chloe Person 7

You should have let me dress you again.

Jennifer

We’ve been over this.

Jennifer

If I’m going to date this guy, he needs to get to know me. Not some girl he thinks dresses in Louboutin.

Jennifer

Besides, when I wear your shoes, bad things happen to me.

Chloe Person 7

Don’t blame the shoes, blame the wearer.

Chloe Person 7

I guess it’ll do for a first date.

I thought my soft pink sundress and jean halter vest were perfect.

My strappy sandals showed off Chloe’s fuchsia nail polish.

I’d decided I kind of liked it, and kept it.

My makeup was minimal—eyeliner, blush, and gloss.

My only jewelry were a pair of small silver hoops and a diamond pendant my parents got me for graduation.

Kinsley

You look great.

Kinsley

Where are you guys going?

I shrugged, reaching for my bag.

I could hear Dain’s deep voice from downstairs, mingling with Christian’s.

Jennifer

I don’t know, he didn’t tell me. Do you think I should have asked?

Chloe Person 7

Definitely.

Chloe Person 7

You said his dad is a CEO, which means he’s rich.

Chloe Person 7

Maybe he plans to take you somewhere upscale.

I bit my lower lip and looked down at my dress.

Anxiety assaulted me, when I’d been feeling so confident while getting ready.

I’d always liked this outfit.

Jennifer

Crap, I didn’t think about that.

Chloe Person 7

Poor people seldom do.

I shot Chloe a look and slung my purse over my shoulder.

Jennifer

I’m sure I’ll be fine.

Kinsley

You will.

Kinsley

You’ll have a great time, then come back and tell us all about it.

Jennifer

Definitely.

I paused at the door, wiping my sweaty palms on the skirt of my dress before reaching for the knob.

My heart was hammering as I went down the stairs.

Anxiety swelled in the first moment that I laid eyes on Dain.

Then, it abated when I took in his jeans, white T-shirt, and leather jacket.

I breathed a sigh of relief and smiled.

Jennifer

Hi.

He smiled back and came forward, those piercing eyes raking me from head to toe.

I shivered, feeling like he was peeling back the layers of my clothes.

My lower lip disappeared between my teeth again—a nervous habit.

Dain

You look great.

Dain

Looks like you’re feeling better.

Jennifer

Yeah, good as new.

The doorbell rang again and I reached to open it, revealing Kara, one of Luke’s guitar students.

Friend with benefits was a more fitting description, but Luke liked to let us think he was teaching her to play guitar.

Lessons were how he paid the rent, but with Kara we knew there was more to it.

We all knew what was happening when that door was closed and Christian was banished to the living room for a few hours.

If the warbled chords coming from under his door were any indication, she was a crappy guitar student anyway.

Kara

Hi Jenn!

She had a loud, grating voice.

For some reason, the fact that she’s screwing Luke makes her think she’s friends with all of us.

We barely tolerate her.

Kara

Ooh, cute dress!

Jennifer

Thanks.

I stood aside to let her in.

Jennifer

Luke’s in his room.

She made her way past me, toting her pink guitar case.

Kara

Who’s the guy?

She whispered, a little too loud.

Dain pretended not to hear and kept talking with Christian, but I could see amusement pulling at the corners of his mouth.

Jennifer

That’s Dain.

I wasn't really comfortable discussing him with Luke’s pillow partner.

Jennifer

My date.

Her eyebrows shot up.

Kara

Wow. Lucky you.

Somehow, her tone indicated disbelief that a guy like Dain would be interested in little ole me.

I frowned.

Jennifer

Maybe lucky him.

Dain

Oh, I’m definitely the lucky one.

Dain snaked an arm around my waist as he came up beside me.

Dain

Ready, Jenn?

I smiled at Kara’s open-mouthed expression.

That felt good.

Jennifer

Sure am.

Christian

Have her back by ten, Peterson.

Dain laughed.

Dain

Don’t wait up, Carver.

The door slammed behind us and Dain led me to the black Ford F-150 parked at the curb.

He opened my door and then held my hand as I stepped up into the high cab.

A few minutes later, we were navigating the city streets.

He leaned back in his seat, his hands relaxed on the wheel.

I released a shaky breath and told myself to calm down.

This was a casual first date and Dain was a nice guy.

I’d already embarrassed myself in front of him once, and he still called me.

Jennifer

So, are you going to tell me where we’re going?

Dain

You’re going to think it’s dumb.

Jennifer

Why would I think that?

He shrugged.

Dain

It’s a little corny.

Jennifer

Dain, you’re talking about a girl who sings the songs from Hairspray into her brush while watching the movie and performing the choreography.

Jennifer

Corny is my middle name.

The corny place turned out to be Blue Starlight Mini Urban Drive-In.
