Our Adventure - Episode 3

Martha

Oh my God! Oh my God, Caroline.

Martha

I feel like I just made a huge mistake.

Caroline

What?! Why?

Caroline

What’d he do? I swear I’ll—

Martha

No, he didn’t do anything wrong, he just…

Martha

We’re going to meet!

Caroline

You’re going to meet?!

Caroline

That’s great!

Martha

But you said—

Caroline

Look, I’m trying to be supportive here.

Caroline

And besides, you were right, Martha. You deserve to be in a happy relationship.

Caroline

You’ve gotta put yourself out there to be in one, you know?

Martha

Yeah, but what about what YOU said? What if he’s like Justin?

Martha

Justin didn’t have a clue what he was getting into and neither does this guy…

Caroline

Yeah, but Justin’s a jerk.

Caroline

And sure, there’s a chance this guy is too…

Martha

Not helping.

Caroline

Haha, let me finish then!

Caroline

I was GOING to say that there’s a chance this guy is nothing like your ex.

Martha

I don’t know…

Caroline

Look, you already initiated the plans.

Caroline

I say go for it.

Martha

I feel like this is how people get murdered. They meet strangers from dating apps in the woods and then they die.

Caroline

Don’t be ridiculous. You’re obviously not going to meet him in the woods!

Caroline

How about this?

Caroline

You guys pick a public place, and then I’ll go and sit nearby to make sure nothing bad happens.

Martha

But what will you be able to DO, exactly?

Martha

No offense, Caroline, but I don’t think you’re burly enough to beat up a predator.

Caroline

Hey! Look, at least I can call the cops or something.

Caroline

And stop calling Juan names!

Caroline

Here, give me your phone.

Martha reluctantly hands it over, and Caroline starts typing a text message.

Before she hits send, she shows it to Martha.

Martha reads it over.

Martha

Change ‘shall’ to ‘should.’ I don’t want it to seem like I think I live in the 1800s.

Caroline

Haha, good point.

Caroline hits send and tosses the phone to Martha.

Martha looks down at the text chain with Juan.

Martha

Tomorrow is great. Where should we go?

Juan

How about Spoonful in Cedar Village? 11:30?

Martha

Sounds good.

Martha looks up from the phone and meets Caroline’s eyes.

Caroline

So…you’re meeting him?

Martha

Yeah, at the coffee shop in Cedar Village.

Caroline

Ooh. Good choice. I love that place.

Caroline

And see? That wasn’t very hard.

Martha

Yeah, yeah. We haven’t even gotten to the hard part yet.

Martha

What the hell am I gonna wear?

The next day, Martha straightens her tunic sweater over her lap so it covers the top half of her Spandex-clad legs.

She glances around the coffee shop, looking for a guy who resembles the one in Juan’s photos.

She startles when she hears a deep voice behind her.

Juan

Peppermint mocha, right? You basically wrote an ode to them in your profile.

Martha

Haha, well, I’m still campaigning to have them added as an everyday menu item.

Martha

Would you be willing to join the cause?

Juan

For you? I guess I could hand out a few brochures.

Juan

I’m Juan, by the way.

Martha

I had a hunch you might be. I’m Martha.

The two shake hands. Juan’s dark brown eyes stay linked with Martha’s the entire time —

They don’t stray down to her chair.

Martha, on the other hand, can’t help but peruse Juan’s muscular body.

Over his shoulder, Caroline gives Martha a thumbs up. Juan is hot.

Juan

Like what you see?

Martha feels herself blush furiously.

Juan

Haha, I’m just teasing you. I swear I’m more than a piece of meat.

Martha

I just don’t get it. HOW are you on a dating app?

Martha

Isn’t dating easy enough for you at school?

Juan’s eyes crinkle and suddenly he looks very tired.

Juan

That’s what my sister always asks me.

Juan

She says I’m too picky.

Martha

Your sister? I didn’t even know you had one.

Juan

Yeah. Maria’s my half-sister, technically. She’s a lot older than me.

Juan

Her dad’s in the army too — that’s where our parents met.

Martha

Ohhh, that’s why you move around a lot.

Juan

Yeah. It’s been surprisingly nice to have an older sister now.

Juan

She gives me lots of good advice and has been helping me cope with all the moves.

Martha

Nice. So…do I have to worry about her, haha?

Juan

Well, she’s not a D1 linebacker…

Juan

But she did marry her ELEMENTARY school sweetheart -- so she thinks I need help. ASAP.

Martha

Her elementary school sweetheart?

Juan

Yeah. This dude was in her class in kindergarten. They used to pretend to get married at recess.

Juan

When she moved away, they became pen pals.

Juan

Then twenty years later…

Juan

They actually DID get married.

Martha

Awww that’s so cute!

Juan

Is it though?

Juan

She’s always going on about their story and telling me to marry my best friend.

Juan

But I don’t even want to DATE any of my best friends.

Juan

There’s got to be something more to love than that.

Martha

I don’t know…I might have to agree with your sister here.

Martha

It’d be nice to marry my best friend because there’s a foundation of trust in that relationship.

Juan

I get that — but also, can’t two people build trustover time?

Martha

I guess…

Juan

I dunno — I want that feeling, you know?

Juan

The one you were talking about — the one you get when you’re on top of a mountain.

Juan

When you’re hanging in a harness and feel potential energy surging through your veins.

Martha lowers her head. She tries to hide her emotions but her voice is deep and husky.

Martha

Yeah, I know. I remember that feeling.

Juan

You don’t have to only remember it.

Juan

We can go now — we can experience it.

Juan

Together.

Juan reaches out his hand toward Martha and his eyes light up in earnest.

Juan

What do you say, Martha?

Juan

Do you want to have an adventure?

Martha

But how…how can we?

Martha

I’m paralyzed, Juan, that isn’t going to change.

Juan

What if I told you I had a trick up my sleeve?

Martha raises an eyebrow.

Juan

Come on, Martha. Do you trust me?
