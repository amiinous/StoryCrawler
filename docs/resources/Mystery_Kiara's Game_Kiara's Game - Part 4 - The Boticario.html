Kiara's Game - Part 4 - The Boticario

The Fernandez Boticario had all the essentials of an old-time apothecary—

Dried herbs, powders, potions, and snake skin. I wanted to examine everything.

But I couldn’t stop staring at the old book behind the counter.

Abby

That’s the book that’s caused all this drama?

Maya

Uh-huh.

Miss Maria

If you didn’t want trouble, maybe you shouldn’t have invaded someone else’s space.

Maya’s Aunt Maria surprised us when her soft Spanish accent interrupted our words.

Though she was beautiful, her demeanor was frighteningly intense as she sized me up.

And for the second time that day, a complete stranger seemed to recognize me.

Maya

Oh, hey, Aunt Maria.

Kiara

Hi, Ms. Fernandez, I’m-

Miss Maria

Kiara Reynard. I know. I’m Miss Maria.

Miss Maria

It’s about time we finally met.

Miss Maria

Maya’s mentioned you quite often.

Kiara

She has?

Miss Maria

Yes. And I would like to talk about what happened at the mall…

Kiara

Is that the same book?

Miss Maria

It is.

Maya

Why do you have it?

Aunt Maria hesitated.

Miss Maria

Why don’t you girls have a look around while Maya and I sort this out?

Miss Maria

Not you, Kiara.

Kiara

Me?

Miss Maria leaned closer, waiting until Abby and Zoe were out of earshot.

Miss Maria

This involves you.

Kiara

What involves me?

Maya

The book, obviously.

Maya

Since you said you don’t know those old crones.

Kiara

But I DON’T know them.

Maya

Bull sh—

Miss Maria

She doesn’t know them, Maya.

Maya

How do you know that?

Miss Maria

Because I know them. I have for a long time.

Maya

Does my mom?

Miss Maria

Yes.

Maya

Then she’ll be interested to know they almost stabbed me.

Miss Maria

Trust me, Maya. You were never in danger.

Miss Maria

The knife was just for show.

Miss Maria

A warning to anyone else watching.

Maya

Who else would be watching?

Maria’s gaze fell squarely on me.

Miss Maria

That book is quite valuable to certain individuals.

Miss Maria

By stomping over there and going through their bags…

Miss Maria

You called unwanted attention to them and the book.

Maya

If it’s so valuable, why trust two old ladies with it?

Miss Maria

Seems to me, those ‘old ladies’ proved quite capable.

Miss Maria

Just be thankful they recognized you, Maya.

Maya

I am. But what I really want to know is—

I knew Maya was going to ask how they knew who I was.

But Miss Maria adeptly steered the conversation away from that topic.

Miss Maria

Why you’ve never met them before?

Miss Maria

Your mother and I don’t always tell you everything.

Miss Maria

But perhaps it’s time we started.

Miss Maria

Shall the three of us arrange a get-together tomorrow?

Kiara

Yes please.

Miss Maria

Good. I’ll let Maya know the location and time.

Miss Maria

But for now, you should get going.

Miss Maria

It’s been a long day for all of us.

Kiara

Can I ask you something first, Miss Maria?

Miss Maria

Of course.

Kiara

Do you know me, somehow?

Miss Maria

I know of you, Kiara.

Kiara

I don’t understand.

Miss Maria

I know. And I’m sorry.

Miss Maria

But the answers will come… with time.

Miss Maria

Just keep an open mind, because what we THINK we know about this world…

Miss Maria

Wouldn’t fill a thimble, compared to what’s actually out there.
