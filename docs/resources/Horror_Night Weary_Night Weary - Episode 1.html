Night Weary - Episode 1

Samson

Dude, I just woke up on a grave.

David

A WHAT?!

Samson

A grave.

Samson

My clothes are in tatters and there are scratches up and down my arms.

Samson

And—OH GOD—my hands…

Samson

They’re covered in blood.

David

That’s SO messed up.

David

But dw man, I’ll come pick you up.

David

It’s two in the morning. I’m not gonna let you stay there by yourself.

Samson

Thanks. It’s weird to admit this…but I’m really scared.

David

I get it, man. I’d be beyond freaked.

David

Do you know which cemetery you’re in?

Samson

Idk, but I can see one of those square mausoleums.

Samson

It looks brand new…just like the one where we buried my brother.

Samson

The marble walls are so white they’re practically glowing in the darkness.

David

Samson, stay focused. I wanna get you out of there.

David

Can you see anything else? Maybe a sign or something?

Samson

Hold on, lemme turn on the flashlight on my phone.

Samson

Dude, this is so creepy.

Samson

I feel like a hand is going to shoot out of a grave and grab my ankle.

David

Ugh, don’t even say that!

Samson

It’s okay. I think I found the entrance.

Samson

There’s this old iron gate with an iron archway.

David

Does it say the name of the cemetery on it?

Samson

Yeah. I see it now. It’s Mount Peace.

David

You’re at Mount Peace Cemetery??!!

David

How the hell did you get all the way up there, man??

Samson

If I knew, I wouldn’t be in this situation.

Samson

Just…hurry. All right?

David

Okay. I’m going as fast as I can.

David

I just got in a cab.

Samson

Okay.

Samson

Dude…I feel like I hear rustling.

David

It’s probably just the wind. Let’s focus on something else—

David

You really don’t know how you got there?

Samson

I don’t have the slightest idea.

David

Damn. What’s the last thing you remember?

Samson

I don’t know…

David

Did you go out tonight?

Samson

No, I stayed in.

David

Yeah—but now you’re in a CEMETERY.

David

You must have left the apartment at some point.

Samson

I really don’t remember doing it.

David

Did you leave to get dinner?

Samson

No. I ordered in.

David

Shit—could it have been the delivery man?

Samson

What do you mean?

David

I mean, could he have slipped something weird in your food?

Samson

Dude, why would he do that?

David

I DON’T KNOW—why would YOU be lying on a grave in the middle of the night, Samson?

Samson

I HAVE NO IDEA.

Samson

But I think this situation is worse for me than it is for you right now.

Samson

So I don’t need you yelling at me.

David

Look, I’m just trying to think of all the possibilities.

David

Did you check our video surveillance first to make sure the doorman wasn’t sketchy?

Samson

Of course I did.

Samson

Wait—I DO remember something weird about him.

David

What was it?

Samson

He kind of looked…

Samson

He looked a lot like my brother.

David

Whoa, that must have been weird for you.

Samson

Yeah, and like…I expected to get sad when I saw the guy at the door.

Samson

Because that asshole got to live while my brother had to die…

Samson

But I didn’t feel sad.

David

That’s good, right?

David

Maybe that means you’re learning to deal with your grief.

Samson

No, that’s the thing. I don’t think I am.

Samson

Because I got REALLY MAD when I saw the dude.

Samson

I saw red, man.

Samson

And that was the last thing I remember.

Samson

Shit, there’s literally BLOOD on my hands.

Samson

You don’t think…

David

No. Samson, I know you.

David

You wouldn’t hurt anyone.

David

You didn’t do anything wrong.

Samson

Idk, David. Ever since my brother died, it’s like I’ve been a different person.

Samson

I just get so angry.

Samson

It’s almost like I can’t trust myself.

David

Stop it. I trust you.

Samson

Maybe you shouldn’t.

Samson

Look at where I am right now.

Samson

I look like some kind of monster and I’m in a graveyard.

Samson

It’s so messed up.

Samson

And this graveyard…it’s really spooky at night.

David

I know, but I’ll be there soon. Hang in there.

David

Just…convince yourself that it’s a park or something.

Samson

I can’t. My hands are dripping with blood.

Samson

And these graves…they look like soldiers in the moonlight.

David

Dude, chill out.

David

I’m almost there.

Samson

I can’t, man.

Samson

There’s a full moon tonight.

Samson

My brother used to always love nights like these.

Samson

We’d sit out on the front porch together, drinking a beer…

Samson

But now they just make me furious.

Samson

Because my brother doesn’t get to be here to enjoy them.

Samson

And that’s so disgustingly unfair.

David

He wouldn’t want you to be angry, man.

David

Try to just relax.

Samson

DON’T tell me what to do.

Samson

And don’t you EVER tell me what my brother would want.

David

Dude, chill.

Samson

NO—you hardly knew him.

Samson

You think because he came by the apartment a few times, you get what I’m going through?

David

I never said that, Samson.

Samson

SHUT UP. I can’t talk to you, David.

David

But Samson, I’m here.

David

My cab’s pulling up to the cemetery.

David

Please—stay where you are.
