Night Weary - Episode 5

But they don’t wait for more than a second…

Before the moon slides into view.

Samson crushes the flower in his hand and lets out an enraged cry.

Samson

You have GOT to be kidding me!

Mary shields David with her body…

And starts to back up, very slowly…

Until there’s a safe distance between them and the werewolf.

Mary

I…I can’t believe it.

Mary

The wolfsbane didn’t work.

Samson

Damn straight it didn’t work!

Samson

I’m still absolutely FURIOUS.

Samson throws his head back, and his teeth glisten with saliva.

He surges toward David.

Mary

David, RUN!

And David runs. He weaves around the headstones

So quickly that Samson trips over one of them as he follows in fast pursuit.

Mary keeps pace with her boyfriend, encouraging him along the way.

Mary

Keep going, babe. Keep going!

But suddenly, they’re trapped—

They run headlong into a giant mausoleum.

Samson lets out a bellowing laugh.

Samson

There’s nowhere to go now, roomie!

Suddenly, David’s very scared—

Because this could be the moment he breathes his last breath.

David

Samson, don’t—

But Samson growls again, and jumps on David,

Pinning him to the ground.

His foaming mouth moves closer to David’s neck…

But then the clouds shift again, and the threesome is cloaked in total darkness.

Mary

Oh my God, David!

Mary

Get out of there—RUN!!!

David

I—I can’t.

Mary

What do you mean you can’t?

Mary

There’s a werewolf on top of you!!

Mary

This is a monster who THREW himself at my cab.

Mary

He has blood ALL over his hands.

David

But he’s—he’s my friend.

David backs out of the tackle, leaping to his feet.

The sudden moment throws Samson off him and onto the dry dirt,

But Samson pops up, regaining his stance as well.

The two roommates square off.

Mary

David, don’t trust him…

David

I have to—because somewhere inside him, I know that he’s good.

Mary

David, don’t…

Mary

He’s only like this because the clouds are hiding the moon.

Mary

As soon as they move, he’ll transform.

Mary

He’ll attack.

David

So maybe now’s the time.

David

Maybe now I can reason with him.

Mary

I don’t know, David. I think it’s too risky.

David

But it’s Samson, Mary.

David

He’s worth the risk.

In a shaky voice, David calls out to the monster in front of him.

David

Samson? It’s me.

Samson

Dude, I know. I’m so glad to see you.

Samson

It’s been a horrible night.

Samson

I keep slipping in and out of consciousness.

Samson

I hardly remember anything since I texted you.

Samson

But I have all these bruises on my torso and it hurts when I breathe.

Samson

Do you think I broke a rib?

David

Maybe. Mary saw you fall off the back of a taxi.

David

We’re worried about you.

Everything seems to go silent in the cemetery

As Samson looks out into the distance with a forlorn look on his face.

David follows his gaze to the giant mausoleum before them—

The mausoleum where they buried Samson’s brother.

David

Samson…

Samson

Don’t.

Samson

Look, I think you guys are right. I think I am a werewolf.

Samson

It’s just…I keep blacking out.

Samson

And look at me…I look like a freak.

Samson

A monster.

Samson

What do I do?

Samson

We tried the flower thing. What else can I do to make this better?

David

You just need to know that this isn’t who you are.

Samson

Dude, I don’t think that’s enough.

Samson

Look at my bloody hands. I’m worried I could hurt you guys…or myself.

David

Yeah, but you didn’t actually hurt anybody.

David

The full moon got covered by the clouds.

David

Everything’s okay. Nothing bad happened.

Samson

Yeah, but something bad COULD have happened.

Samson

Maybe I’m not REALLY a monster…

Samson

But my grief…my grief is making me this way.

Samson

And GODDAMN it, I need to do something to fix it.

Samson

But I don’t have the slightest idea about what that something is.

Just then, Mary approaches Samson in the silence.

Shakily, she slips her hand into his.

Mary

I know what to do.

Samson

Are you going to give me another deathly flower?

Mary

No.

Mary

I’m gonna help you help yourself by giving you some advice.

Mary

You’ve gotta talk about him, Samson.

Samson

What?!

Samson

Back the HELL off, Mary—you don’t know the first thing about me.

David

Hey! DON’T talk to my girlfriend that way!

Samson

Oh God, I should’ve known you’d take her side.

Samson

You know what? I absolve you of your responsibilities, ROOMMATE.

Samson

Go ahead, run off with Mary.

Samson

Leave me in the dust—you don’t need me anyway, right?

Samson

Because I’m dangerous?

David

Goddamn it! You’re NOT really dangerous!!

David

This isn’t who you really are.

David

You are—you’re my friend.

David

And if you’d just talk to me, or damn it, talk to SOMEBODY—

David

Then maybe this wouldn’t have to be so hard.

David’s drops his voice to a whisper.

David

This doesn’t have to be so hard, man.

Samson goes still. When he speaks,

It’s so soft that David has to take a step closer to hear him.

A little bit louder this time, Samson repeats his admission.

Samson

Yes, it does.

David

What?

Samson

It HAS to be hard for me, because then I know that I’m remembering him.

Samson

If it gets easier, then it means I’m not honoring his memory.

Samson

I don’t want to go to some shrink who’s going to teach me to forget my brother.

Samson

He was the most important person in the world to me.

Mary

Samson, I’m glad you want to remember your brother.

Mary

That’s a good thing.

Mary

But you won’t forget him by going to a psychiatrist. You’ll learn to remember him in a better way.

Samson

I…

Nobody speaks for a few moments, and Samson seems to stand up taller.

David

Samson? What is it?

Samson

I’m ready.

Mary

Oh my God! Seriously??

Mary

Because if you aren’t ready…

Mary

If you don’t want this…

Mary

Then the therapy isn’t going to work.

Samson

No, I’m ready.

Samson

I promise.

Mary runs toward Samson and wraps him in a hug,

And David claps his best friend on the shoulder.

After two awkward smacks,

He pulls Samson away from Mary to wrap him into a big bear hug too.

Samson

God—this is amazing.

Samson

For the first time tonight, I don’t feel mad or sad.

Samson

And I don’t feel that terrible sinking feeling in my stomach.

Samson

I feel…calm.

Samson

Like everything’s going to be okay.

And although no one noticed it,

The clouds had parted a solid five minutes prior to this moment.

But for all those three knew, with this admission,

Samson was forever cured of being a werewolf.

And you know how these stories end, dear reader…

They all lived happily ever after.
