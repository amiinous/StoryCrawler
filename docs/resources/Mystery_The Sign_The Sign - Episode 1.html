The Sign - Episode 1

Unknown

Hello?

Kevin

Hi. Who’s this?

Unknown

You don’t know me…but I was feeling lonely.

Unknown

So I just texted your number randomly.

Kevin

Um, okay. Is everything alright?

Unknown

Yeah. I just…I could use someone to talk to…

Kevin

Well…maybe I can be that someone for you.

Unknown

I’d really like that.

Kevin

So um…tell me something about yourself?

Unknown

Okay, like what?

Kevin

Like, how old are you? And…are you a girl or guy?

Unknown

I’m 16 – and a girl.

Kevin

Cool. I’m a guy 🙂

Unknown

🙂

Unknown

What else?

Kevin

Let’s see…where are you right now?

Kevin

Maybe we’re under the same night sky.

Unknown

Hmm, maybe…I’m on a park bench by the river.

Kevin

In the dark? Are you alone?

Unknown

Yeah. Sometimes I come here to think.

Unknown

Is that weird?

Unknown

Oh my gosh, it’s weird. Look, I’m sorry to bother you—

Unknown

I just wanted to…idk…

Kevin

No need to apologize.

Kevin

And for what it’s worth, I don’t think you’re actually alone.

Kevin

Because I’m with you—

Kevin

You’re talking to me 🙂

Unknown

Aw that’s really sweet. You seem like a really nice person. Will you…

Unknown

Tell me more about yourself?

Unknown

Who are you?

Kevin

Well, my name is Kevin. I’m a senior at South Bronx Academy.

Kevin

My parents are divorced. This week I’m staying with my mom.

Unknown

I’m sorry to hear that. Is it hard for you to alternate like that?

Kevin

Honestly, yes. I’m the only one at home who has to deal with it—

Kevin

My brother left for college before they split. So he’s the lucky one.

Unknown

Maybe you’re the stronger one 💪

Kevin

Haha, I like that. Thanks.

Kevin

Wow, it’s really easy to talk to you about this stuff.

Kevin

Like, easier than any therapy session my mom’s dragged me to.

Unknown

See? Maybe you’ll start texting random numbers too 😉

Unknown

Do you want to share anything else with me?

Kevin

You mean like my deepest darkest secrets…?

Kevin

That kinda thing?

Unknown

Sure.

Kevin

Um, idk. I’m feeling a little weird now—

Kevin

I mean, I know nothing about you.

Unknown

What do you wanna know?

Kevin

Why are you sitting by yourself at night?

Kevin

And which bench are you on by the river?

Kevin

Maybe I can come meet you—

Kevin

We can talk in person instead.

Unknown

Like I’m gonna tell you my exact location!

Kevin

Fair enough. But then maybe I feel like taking a long walk…

Kevin

You know, since your area code is the same as mine…

Kevin

I might find you eventually.

Unknown

Don’t waste your time.

Kevin

Just gimme one hint.

Kevin

Are there any landmarks around you?

Unknown

A blue neon sign. The light is off on the letter O.

Kevin

Okay, thanks. And to repay you for that hint…

Kevin

I’ll tell you a secret.

Kevin

This is probably a terrible idea though…

Kevin

I mean, the guys are gonna kill me…

Kevin

But you know what? Screw it.

Kevin

No one else will know, right?

Unknown

You can say anything to me.

Unknown

What’s the secret?

Kevin

Do you remember the house that burned down three months ago…

Kevin

Up by St. John’s Church?

Unknown

That sounds vaguely familiar.

Kevin

Yeah, the Lee family lived there.

Kevin

It started out just as a stupid dare.

Unknown

What do you mean?

Kevin

Mr. and Mrs. Lee were out at dinner that night, with their older daughter…

Kevin

They got a sitter to watch the baby at home.

Kevin

My friend Scott said we needed to get revenge.

Unknown

Revenge for what?

Kevin

For what Mr. Lee said to us at the convenience store…

Unknown

Oh yeah? And what was that?

Kevin

He caught us trying to buy booze at his store underage, and he threatened to tell the cops.

Unknown

So you decided to burn his house down?!

Kevin

We didn’t mean to burn it down! Things got out of hand…

Kevin

We made a couple of Molotov cocktails. One thing led to another…

Kevin

The babysitter must have been asleep…

Kevin

God, I hate even talking about this —

Kevin

I regretted it as soon as the bottle left my hand.

Kevin

I should’ve never thrown it.

Kevin

Hey! I think I see the neon sign! It’s not so far away…

Kevin

We can probably meet up soon. I mean, if you want.

Unknown

You could have called the fire department.

Unknown

You could have done something.

Unknown

You didn’t have to let the house burn to the ground.

Kevin

Excuse me?

Unknown

Two people died because of YOU.

Kevin

It’s not that simple—

Kevin

I was scared!

Kevin

And the guys were there — what could I have possibly done?

Kevin

I didn’t want to seem like a wuss.

Unknown

This is funny. This is really funny.

Unknown

I wanted to talk to someone kind when I was feeling alone…

Unknown

But instead I found you — an arsonist.

Kevin

Stop — I’m not!

Kevin

Come on, I made a mistake. Haven’t you ever made one?

Unknown

Can you do me a favor?

Kevin

If I do it, will you stop making me feel guilty?

Unknown

I think it’ll make YOU stop feeling guilty—

Unknown

Can you confess?

Unknown

Will you go to the police?

Kevin

No way! Are you kidding me?

Kevin

I’ll end up in jail. I just turned 18 last month — it’d be a full sentence.

Kevin

I’m not gonna rot in a jail cell for the rest of my life.

Unknown

You have a chance to redeem yourself, if you face what you did.

Unknown

Otherwise, you’re always going to be this person—

Unknown

An arsonist.

Unknown

A terrible person.

Kevin

And who are you? I doubt you’re a saint.

Unknown

We all make mistakes—

Unknown

The difference is that a good person apologizes.

Unknown

How do you live with yourself?

Kevin

Stop it! You don’t understand—

Kevin

The Lees are jerks anyway. They had it coming.

Unknown

I see. Now you’re justifying yourself.

Unknown

That makes it even worse.

Kevin

No it doesn’t!

Kevin

Look, can’t we talk about something else?

Kevin

Leave it alone — please.

Unknown

I CAN’T leave it alone. I deal with your “mistake” every day.

Kevin

What are you talking about?

Unknown

My dad lost his store because of you!

Unknown

He can’t even hold himself together.

Unknown

My mother had a mental breakdown and is now hospitalized in a psych ward.

Unknown

You tore our family apart.

Unknown

You killed my baby brother.

Kevin

You’re the Lee girl?? Hyo?

Hyo

Yes.

Kevin

What?! I thought this was an anonymous conversation…

Kevin

I thought you texted me randomly.

Hyo

Well you were wrong, Kevin Mendels.

Hyo

I always planned to text you.

Hyo

I lured you in, making you think you could trust me…

Hyo

Because I needed to make you confess so I had evidence.

Hyo

Now I’m taking my cell phone straight to the cops—

Hyo

No one can bring my baby brother back.

Hyo

But at least my family can finally get some peace…

Hyo

By knowing you’ll rot in a jail cell the rest of your life.

Less than a month later, Kevin and Scott were convicted of murder.

They’ve been in jail ever since.

Kevin often thinks back to his conversation with that lonely girl, before he knew her identity…

And it always shakes him that earlier in the night—

It had seemed like they would get along.

Now, when he thinks about what he did to Mr. Lee’s daughter, he realizes…

He did that to a friend.
