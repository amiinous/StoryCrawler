Family Secrets - Episode 5

Hannah

Ms. Pynchon, I’m so sorry.

Hannah

I know you had a daughter and her name was Allison.

Hannah

And that she died when she was 18.

Hannah

But that’s almost all I know.

Ms. Pynchon

Yes, we kept the details vague.

Ms. Pynchon

It was 25 years ago.

Ms. Pynchon

It was easier to keep secrets back then.

Ms. Pynchon

Away from the prying eyes of the Internet.

Hannah

So…was it an illness?

Ms. Pynchon

No, it wasn’t an illness.

Other possibilities race through Hannah’s mind.

Ms. Pynchon

Allison’s story is part of the reason I brought you here.

Ms. Pynchon

I want to clear away the shadows of the past.

Ms. Pynchon

But the shadows are going to have to remain for a while longer.

Ms. Pynchon

Because the others are here. It’s time for our next meeting.

The door opens, and in walks Lionel, Virginia’s husband.

Then Lucinda and Tyler come in together, laughing.

Hannah sees Tyler give a start as he greets Ms. Pynchon.

Clearly, he has never seen her scar before.

Ms. Pynchon

Sit down, everyone!

Ms. Pynchon

The reason I called you all here today…

Ms. Pynchon

Is to introduce you to Hannah Bright, my biographer.

Ms. Pynchon

Some of you have met her already.

Tyler

Yes we have!

Ms. Pynchon looks from Tyler to Hannah.

Ms. Pynchon

You’ve met Tyler too?

Ms. Pynchon

My, Hannah, you certainly get to work quickly.

Hannah can feel herself blushing.

She wills herself to stop.

Ms. Pynchon

Now, I want to make something very clear.

Ms. Pynchon

You are to make yourselves completely available to Hannah in the coming weeks.

Ms. Pynchon

And answer her questions about the family and the company.

Lionel

ALL of them, darling?

Ms. Pynchon

Yes dear, all of them.

Lucinda

We’re so excited to have you here, Hannah. Welcome!

Hannah

Thank you! I’m looking forward to learning the family’s story.

Tyler

Excuse me, Ms. Pynchon?

Tyler

I’m happy to be here for now and see your beautiful estate.

Tyler

But I have a company to run.

Tyler

YOUR company.

Tyler

When can I get back to Hartford?

Ms. Pynchon

When I say you can.

Ms. Pynchon

It is, as you note, MY company.

Tyler

But I’m not a member of the family.

Ms. Pynchon

But there is so much you can teach Hannah about the business.

Ms. Pynchon

And so much you can learn about the family.

Ms. Pynchon

So I want you here for now.

Ms. Pynchon

We’ve hired excellent vice presidents for a reason.

Tyler

Yes, Ms. Pynchon.

Lucinda

And Tyler, we’ll do EVERYTHING we can…

Lucinda

To make your stay here as painless as possible.

This time Ms. Pynchon looks from Tyler to Lucinda.

And then over at Hannah.

Hannah

Thanks for meeting with me, everyone.

Hannah

I’m excited to talk to all of you.

Hannah

And hear your stories.

Ms. Pynchon

All right everyone, you can leave.

Ms. Pynchon

But let’s meet again tomorrow at this same time.

Ms. Pynchon

And then Hannah can start setting up individual interviews.

Everyone begins filing out of the drawing out.

Ms. Pynchon

Hannah! I didn’t mean you.

Ms. Pynchon

Please stay.

Ms. Pynchon

Sit down, dear.

Ms. Pynchon

So what did you think?

Hannah

I think…that it’s going to a very interesting month.

Ms. Pynchon

It is, indeed.

Ms. Pynchon

Tell me dear, what kind of family do you have?

Ms. Pynchon

Not much like this one, I’m willing to bet.

Hannah

No, definitely not.

Hannah

My father is a construction worker.

Hannah

And my mother is a teacher.

Hannah

They adopted me when I was a baby.

Hannah

And a year later they adopted my sister, Megan.

Ms. Pynchon

And do you know anything about your real parents?

Hannah

My adoptive parents ARE my real parents.

Hannah

I was told that my biological parents were considered unfit to raise me.

Ms. Pynchon

That must have been hard for you.

Hannah

Not at all. I ended up with the best parents a person could have.

Hannah

And the best sister.

Ms. Pynchon

Then I stand corrected.

Ms. Pynchon

You’re a lucky woman.

Ms. Pynchon

Unlike poor Lucinda, who grew up without a mother.

Ms. Pynchon

And has an unsuitable father.

Hannah

But she had you.

Ms. Pynchon

Ah, but that’s where you’re wrong.

Ms. Pynchon

I was barely there for her—or anyone for that matter.

Ms. Pynchon

I was too busy running the company to pay much attention to her.

Ms. Pynchon

Bernadette is the one who raised Lucinda.

Just then Bernadette enters the drawing room.

Bernadette

Madame, your mail.

She sets the mail on a table.

As she leaves, the dirty look she gives Hannah is unmistakable.

Ms. Pynchon

My, Bernadette has certainly taken a disliking to you!

Hannah

I know, and I have no idea what I could have done to offend her.

Ms. Pynchon

She’s very protective of me.

Ms. Pynchon

That might help explain it.

Hannah

How did Bernadette come to work here?

Ms. Pynchon

It’s straight out of a fairy tale.

Ms. Pynchon

She was found as a baby.

Ms. Pynchon

In a basket. At the front door of the estate.

Hannah

Oh my god!

Ms. Pynchon

Yes. Maria, the housekeeper at the time, raised her as her own.

Ms. Pynchon

Bernadette and my daughter became great friends.

Ms. Pynchon

Although I’m ashamed to say I tried to discourage it.

Ms. Pynchon

Class differences, you know. It was a different world then.

Ms. Pynchon

When Bernadette was 16, she became pregnant by one of our cooks.

Ms. Pynchon

The cook ran away.

Ms. Pynchon

And the baby was stillborn.

Hannah

That’s so sad!

Ms. Pynchon

My daughter was so good to her when that happened.

Ms. Pynchon

And Bernadette was very grateful

Ms. Pynchon

She does so much for us now.

Ms. Pynchon

So we make allowances for her.

Ms. Pynchon

I hope you will too.

Hannah

I’ll certainly try.

Ms. Pynchon

I think that’s enough for today, Hannah.

Ms. Pynchon

I’ve told you more than I intended!

Ms. Pynchon

But maybe that’s a good thing. We only have a month together.

Ms. Pynchon

Let’s meet again tomorrow.

After Hannah says goodbye to Ms. Pynchon, she leaves the drawing room

And is startled to see Lionel, Virginia’s husband,

Lurking outside the door.

Has he been eavesdropping?

Lionel

Hannah, if I could have a word…

Hannah

Yes, sir.

Lionel

You know, when Ginny told me about her idea

Lionel

Of bringing in a biographer.

Lionel

I thought it was utterly idiotic.

Lionel

And I still do.

Lionel

If you know what’s good for you.

Lionel

You’ll leave this house.

Lionel

Now.
