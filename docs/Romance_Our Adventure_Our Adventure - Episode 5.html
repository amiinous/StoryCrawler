Our Adventure - Episode 5

Martha struggles to find the words to describe how far out of her element she feels.

How can she possibly climb a rock wall?

She thought she told Juan that she wasn’t that kind of girl anymore.

Martha

But I—

But then Juan does something that immediately stops Martha’s worries — he laughs.

He pitches his voice in a high falsetto, imitating her as he speaks.

Juan

Martha, deep breaths.

Martha can’t help but laugh along —

And it feels so good, to be laughing and joking about a subject that’s been solemn for a very long time.

Martha

That was amazing! You sounded just like me.

Juan

Hmmm… does that mean that I know you better than you know yourself?

Martha

Eh, let’s not go that far.

Martha and Juan walk into the Sports Pier, and even though she expected her nerves to freeze her completely…

She feels nothing but joy and comfort in Juan’s smile.

After checking in at the front desk, Juan leads her to the back wall, where they meet their trainer.

Juan

Martha, this is Britni. Britni, this is the girl I was telling you about.

Martha

You told her…?

Juan

Well yeah — I wasn’t going to bring you here without making sure that it was safe.

Martha

You…what?

Juan

Britni has worked with paraplegics before. She says it’s totally okay for you to rock climb here with her.

Martha feels something in her stomach twist.

She can’t quite locate her source of discomfort, so she tries to smooth it over…

Because she likes Juan.

And that’s the thing people do in relationships — they compromise, right?

Martha looks up at Juan.

Martha

Oh, um…great.

Britni

You’re not nervous, Martha, are you?

Britni

Because I can just tell that you’re a fighter, and fighters ALWAYS make it up my wall!

It takes every fiber in Martha’s body to stop herself from rolling her eyes.

She hates when strangers tell her that she’s strong or brave —

Especially when she’s holding onto herself by a thread.

She swallows.

Martha

Um, no problem. I’ll climb.

Juan transforms into the labradoodle puppy again, but this time —

It’s not enough to pull up Martha’s sinking heart.

He lifts her out of her chair so Britni can help her get her harness on —

But Martha doesn’t feel the familiar zing of excitement when his hands touch her.

There’s only dread.

Juan

You okay?

Martha shakes her head — just to clear it.

Martha

I’m fine.

Juan nods, and then starts up the wall after getting the go-ahead from the trainer who’s spotting him.

Martha rushes to follow, reaching for the first resin hold on the wall.

She calls back to Britni before she climbs.

Martha

Got me?

Britni

Sure do, you Fighter! I’ll keep the tension nice and tight to reduce the amount of effort you’ll need to apply.

Since she’s facing the wall now, Martha allows herself to roll her eyes.

She moves her other arm up the wall, lifting her feet off the ground. The rush she feels is incredible —

And it pushes her to climb using only her arms, and then climb some more.

Invigorated, she stops and looks down at her progress.

And her heart stops.

Because for all those feelings of triumph, she’s still just in a gym. And her lifeless legs hang below her as she pretends to climb a mountain.

Juan

Whoa — Martha, are you okay?

Juan

I swear to God, your face is as white as a bedsheet.

Juan

Brit, pull her down! She’s—

Martha

I…

Britni

Juan, don’t you dare mansplain over my fighter!

Britni

Let her speak for herself.

But Martha CAN’T speak, because despair has swallowed her voice.

In fact, she can hardly breathe. She’s in a full-out panic now.

Britni

Yo, Fighter Chick? You all right up there?

For a horrible minute, no one says anything. Not until Martha struggles to find a single word.

Martha

Down.

Juan

Oh my God, Brit! Bring her down! I told you—

Britni

I AM bringing her down, goddammit!

Juan clears it with his trainer, and then jumps down the wall.

He’s there when Britni’s pulled Martha down to the padding, but Martha’s too ashamed to look at him.

Hot tears fill her eyes, threatening to overflow her lids…

But after all she’s been through, she’s perfected the art of never letting anyone see her cry.

Juan

Martha, are you okay?

Juan

It’s all right. Don’t be upset. We can—

Juan

We can go back to our adventure in the coffee shop. Have a different adven—

Martha

No more adventures.

Martha

No more dates. No more guys who don’t understand me and what I’m going through.

Martha

Just…no more.

Martha takes another moment, then clears her throat and looks up at her trainer.

Her voice is low and carefully controlled when she speaks.

Martha

Britni, will you please put me in my chair?

As Britni puts Martha in her chair, Juan begs and pleads with Martha — but she doesn’t even let herself hear his words.

Instead, she collects herself, and then looks him squarely in the eyes.

Martha

I don’t know why you enjoy this…messing with a person’s feelings….

Martha

But I have zero respect for you right now.

Martha

Am I…a charity case to you?

Martha

Is this going to be some fun anecdote for the next time you move?

Martha

Like, will you seamlessly ditch me and then have a story about the one time you took out a cripple?

Martha

Hope that works out for you.

Juan

But Martha—

Martha ignores him, wheeling towards the door. She gives him one final glance before she crosses the threshold.

Martha

And DON’T try to contact me again.

And the look on Juan’s face — from his sad eyes to his gaping mouth — it’s enough to haunt Martha forever.
