V-Card - Part 12

Day 8

He still hadn’t called.

I kept telling myself I was being ridiculous.

I had way too much to worry about; Dain should not have been a concern.

I had midterms coming up and a term paper due.

He had a full-time job and a life I knew nothing about.

For all I knew he was waist deep in corporate mail at work.

Or doing charity work for blind kids and puppies.

Or on a date with his leggy, blonde girlfriend.

To top it all off, my dunk in the lake had made me sick.

I’d been fighting a cold all week, but by Wednesday morning, I’d lost.

I barely made it through my Ethics in Nursing class before I retreated back to the apartment and my bed, skipping out on my only other class of the day.

Kinsley left me a stack of magazines before heading off to class and Chloe told me I looked like crap before doing the same.

I did look awful.

Puffy, watery eyes, red nose dripping goo … not a pretty picture.

At this point, I didn’t want Dain to call.

He’d be able to hear my stuffy nose through the phone.

I was lying in bed, having a Vampire Diaries marathon with a box of tissues, when Luke poked his head into my room.

Luke

Hey, feeling any better?

My answer was a round of sneezes that undoubtedly turned my nose even redder.

Luke winced and stepped into the room.

I noticed for the first time that he was holding a bowl in his hands.

Steam wafted from the bowl.

Luke

I cooked. It’s my mom’s recipe for chicken soup.

Luke

She always made it when I was sick, so …

He trailed off, walking into the room and setting the bowl on my tiny nightstand.

I sat up, propping my pillows against the headboard of my bed and leaning against them before taking up the bowl.

Jennifer

Thanks, Luke.

I was surprised as I sniffed its contents.

It tasted even better than it smelled.

Jennifer

Wow, this is good. I didn’t know you cooked.

He shrugged.

Luke

I don’t. At least, not that often.

He sat on the edge of the bed near the footboard as I ate, staring absently at the TV.

After a while, he spoke again.

Luke

Did he call?

I paused, the spoon halfway to my lips.

Of course Christian had filled him in on what happened at the party.

They’d been teasing me about it for days.

Jennifer

Not yet.

I tried not to let on how disappointed that made me.

Luke leaned back against the mattress, half laying half sitting.

His head was practically in my lap.

Luke

He’s stupid if he doesn’t.

This is why people love Luke.

He always knows just what to say.

I smiled.

Jennifer

Thanks. It’s okay if he doesn’t, really.

Jennifer

We only met the one time and … well, I don’t care if he doesn’t call.

Luke

Jenn?

Jennifer

Yeah.

Luke

You’re a terrible liar.

I reached out and tugged one of his curls.

Hard.

Jennifer

Jerk.

Luke

Spaz.

Jennifer

Man whore!

He sat up, holding his hands up defensively.

Luke

Whoa, whoa. Harsh!

I laughed.

Jennifer

The truth hurts.

He lifted one eyebrow.

Luke

Jealous?

Jennifer

No, I don’t like girls.

Luke

I meant, jealous of the girls.

I scoffed.

Jennifer

Not likely.

Suddenly, my phone chirped and lit up.

I nearly dove across the bed trying to grab it.

The area code was local, but the number wasn’t familiar.

My pulse jumped.

Jennifer

It’s him!

My voice was high and squeaky.

Luke stood, shoving his hands into the pockets of his cargo pants.

Luke

Not so stupid after all.

I took a deep breath and fought to steady my voice as I answered.

Luke left, sliding my door closed with a silent click.

Jennifer

Hello?

Dain

Hey gorgeous.

I couldn’t help the audible sigh of relief that escaped my mouth.

Jennifer

Hey.

I tried to sound casual.

He sneezed on the other end.

Dain

Sorry.

For the first time I realized that he sounded stuffy, too.

Dain

I’m sick.

I laughed, but it turned into a cough.

Jennifer

Me too.

Dain

Talk about ironic.

His voice was deep, deeper than I remembered.

Dain sounded sexy with a cold.

I could just picture him lounging in bed with the phone pressed to his ear.

Dain

Dive into a lake to rescue a pretty girl and this is what I get.

Jennifer

Sorry.

Dain

Hey, it was worth it.

Dain

So worth it, I was wondering if you were free this weekend.

Dain

Hopefully by then, my nose will have stopped dripping.

Dain

I wouldn’t want to gross you out during our date.

My cheeks hurt from my face-splitting smile, but I just couldn’t stop.

Jennifer

This weekend?

Midterms were in two weeks and I was supposed to be studying, but one night off wouldn’t hurt.

Plus, I hadn’t forgotten about my mission.

A date was a step in the right direction.

Jennifer

Sure, I’m free. As long as we stay away from large bodies of water, I’m good.

Dain

There won’t be a drop of water in sight.

He chuckled.

Dain

Not even a puddle for you to step in. I swear.

Jennifer

That sounds great.

Walking barefoot in a snowstorm would be great with you.

Dain

Good.

Dain

Friday night at eight?

I nodded, but then remembered he couldn’t see me.

Jennifer

Sure. I live on campus; I’ll text you the address.

Dain

Perfect. I can’t wait to see you again, Jenn.

My stomach turned a little flip.

I loved the way he said the shortened version of my name.

It sounded so smooth on his tongue.

Jennifer

Me either.

Jennifer

I mean to see you. I can’t …

He laughed. The sound was warm.

Dain

Bye, Jenn. Feel better.

Jennifer

You too.

I hung up and muffled my squeal of happiness in my pillow.

I fell back onto the bed, a dopey grin stretching from ear to ear.

It had been a while since I’d dated anyone.

After my last relationship ended six months ago—remember creepy art guy?—I’d pretty much given up on the idea of a steady boyfriend.

Even my mission to lose my virginity wasn’t contingent on getting a boyfriend.

I’d been content with the idea of going on a few dates with a guy, doing the deed, and going our separate ways.

Dain was an unexpected and delightful complication.

With a guy like him, I could dare to hope for more.
