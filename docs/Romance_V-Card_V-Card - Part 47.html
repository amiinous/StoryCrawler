V-Card - Part 47

Luke

Your brother’s cool.

Luke slipped the guitar pick into his pocket and set the instrument on one of Mom’s rocking chairs.

He sat on the porch swing and I lowered myself beside him.

Jennifer

I knew you guys would hit it off.

Jennifer

My dad likes you, too.

Luke snorted.

Luke

The guy threatened to shoot me, Jenn.

Jennifer

Oh, that means he really likes you.

Jennifer

If he didn’t he would have threatened to beat you within an inch of your life first, then shoot you.

Luke

It might just be worth it for one of these.

He leaned toward me and placed a hand at the back of my neck.

His fingers were light but firm as he pulled me in for a kiss.

I leaned into him, dizzy from the slow rock of the swing and Luke’s lips on mine.

It was sweet and light, with just the barest hint of tongue sweeping against my lower lip.

Luke

Oh yeah.

He pulled away.

Luke

So worth it.

He leaned back against the swing and took me with him.

My head rested on his shoulder and my hand on his chest as we rocked.

Silence fell between us, but it was comfortable.

The sound of bugs chirping mingled with the sound of my mom’s music coming through one of the open kitchen windows.

Through the white lace curtains, I could see Mom, kneading dough, and Dad standing beside her talking in hush tones.

She giggled and swatted at him with her rolling pin.

We watched as he snatched the rolling pin from her and pull her into his arms as Billy Joel sang Just the Way You Are.

My dad may have been a nerd back in the day, but the man sure could dance.

He twirled her around the kitchen, singing off key as she giggled like a schoolgirl.

Luke

Your parents are awesome.

Luke

I wish my parents were more like them.

Jennifer

Your parents don’t act like this?

The idea was shocking for me.

I’d never experienced anything different.

Luke shrugged.

Luke

Dad’s always drunk and Mom’s always yelling.

Luke

They fight, then they make up, and they fight some more.

Luke

They always say they love each other, but you wouldn’t know it by the way they treat each other.

Luke

I used to watch sitcoms and see those happy couples with their perfect kids and wish my family could be like that.

Luke

I know all the girls want a love like Romeo and Juliet, but me … I always wanted a love like Homer and Marge.

I laughed and pushed him, causing the swing to sway wildly.

Jennifer

Homer and Marge? Seriously?

He laughed, too.

Luke

Oh definitely.

Luke

They’re not perfect, but they love each other.

Luke

Marge accepts Homer’s faults, and even though he has a lot of them, you just know he’s crazy about Marge.

Luke

They don’t do too bad, you know. Nice home, three kids, loads of fun.

Luke

That’s what I want.

Jennifer

You want to be fat, drunk, and obnoxious, and marry a toothpick with blue hair?

I asked with a smirk.

Luke stood.

Luke

Maybe we can leave off the beer belly, though I think you’d look incredible with blue hair.

Silence fell between us again as my eyes widened.

Jennifer

You think of me as your Marge?

Luke leaned against the rail again, running a hand through his hair.

Luke

Does that freak you out?

I stood, facing him.

I grabbed his hands and pulled him toward me.

Jennifer

Yes, but in a good way.

Jennifer

Not a run for the hills screaming kind of way.

Jennifer

More like that second right before you jump out of an airplane, or before you perform in front of an audience for the first time.

Jennifer

It’s not a bad feeling. It’s sort of like—

Luke

Jenn.

Jennifer

Yes?

Luke

Shut up.

Luke pulled me against him, putting one arm around my waist and taking my hand in his.

Jennifer

What are you doing?

Luke

Apparently it’s a night for dancing.

He swayed with me to the music.

Luke

Besides, you were about to say something to me.

Luke

Something very important.

Luke

You have to wait until exactly the right moment.

Luke

Make it count.
