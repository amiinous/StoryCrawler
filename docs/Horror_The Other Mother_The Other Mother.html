The Other Mother

Ella

Something’s wrong with my mom.

Jerry

Wrong like hurt from the accident?

Ella

Wrong like strange.

Ella

She’s not herself.

Jerry

It was a pretty bad car crash.

Ella

I know. I was there too.

Ella

I lost my dad, but not my mind.

Jerry

She acting crazy?

Ella

Not really, just - different.

Jerry

How?

Ella

She hates peanuts - but she used to love them.

Ella

She can’t stand the color orange.

Ella

PLUS, she now listens to rap...

Ella

and dresses like Nicki Minaj...

Jerry

Scary.

Ella

But the worst is how she treats me.

Ella

She won’t let me go out any more.

Jerry

Not cool. She can’t stop us.

Jerry

Let me talk to her.

Ella

No! If she sees you here, she’ll lock me back in the closet!

Jerry

Seriously??

Ella

Dead serious. She’s gotten so mean.

Ella

Today she hit me for the 1st time!

Jerry

Call the cops.

Ella

That will make it worse. Her “cousin Hank” is a cop.

Ella

I think he’s actually her new bf.

Jerry

She’s a fast mover.

Ella

He hit me today too.

Jerry

No! Ella, that’s bad. I’ll beat the crap outta him.

Jerry

I’m coming to your house after midnight tonight to help you sneak out.

Ella

She put me in the maid’s room - no window, no chance…

Jerry

I won’t let them do this to you, Ella.

Ella

Gotta go...she’s coming downstairs.

the next day

Ella

Sorry I couldn’t return your texts last night.

Jerry

U ok??

Ella

They beat me again. Locked me up without food.

Ella

My battery ran out. Could only charge it in the hallway bathroom this morning.

Jerry

We have to get you out of there.

Ella

I heard them talking about putting me in reform school.

Jerry

They can’t do that!

Ella

I hope not.

Ella

Saw something in the bathroom today.

Ella

Some baby photos my mom saved & hid under the sink.

Ella

Please don’t think I’m crazy but -

Jerry

You can tell me anything, Ella. I believe you.

Ella

This woman in my house is not my mom.

Jerry

?

Ella

My real mom has a mole on the back of her neck...since she was a baby.

Ella

This woman does not.

Jerry

Could it be surgery from the accident?

Ella

Nope. She didn’t break a bone, hardly a scrape.

Ella

I was asleep in back seat when we crashed and I broke my nose.

Ella

My parents were up front.

Ella

I always wondered how dad died but mom lived…

Jerry

What are you saying?

Ella

I can’t prove that it wasn’t really an accident...

Ella

But something’s very wrong here…

Jerry

Didn’t you say that your mom had a sister?

Ella

Yeah - she ran away at 14 and never came back.

Ella

At least that’s what my mom told me.

Jerry

Did you ever see any photos of her?

Ella

Yes, they always dressed alike. Twins.

Jerry

What if she came back? Wanted not just to see your mom...

Jerry

...but BE your mom.

Ella

You think so?!

Jerry

That would explain a lot.

Ella

Unbelievable!

Ella

Shit she’s coming…

a little later

Ella

I don’t think they heard me - but I heard them...

Jerry

What did they say?

Ella

They were talking about the house, the stocks...

Ella

and my inheritance.

Ella

Jerry, I’m really scared.

Jerry

Tell me why.

Ella

They wanted me to die in that car crash...and now -

Jerry

What?!

Ella

They don’t think reform school is such a good idea anymore…

Ella

Jerry - they’re planning to kill me.

Jerry

Listen, stay there. If I can’t get you out -

Jerry

I’ll get them away from you.

Ella

Careful. If they suspect anything -

Jerry

I’ll be fine. Just stay where you are.

a little later

Jerry

Ella, they’re gone now - you can come out.

Ella

They locked my door. I think the key is in the cookie jar.

Jerry

Give me a minute.

Ella

I heard sirens - What did you do?

Jerry

Called 911, told them there was a domestic dispute.

Jerry

After the cops left, they really began to fight.

Jerry

He blamed her for calling the cops. She denied it.

Jerry

They both left in separate cars, still screaming at each other.

Ella

Brilliant!

Jerry

Can’t find key - cookie jar is empty - only crumbs...

Ella

Just like my life…

Jerry

Need a hammer to break open your door. Where are the tools?

Ella

He keeps them locked up too.

Jerry

That bastard!

Jerry

Listen, I’m going over to my dad’s work, at his construction company.

Jerry

Plenty of tools there.

Ella

Hurry please!

Ella

I don’t want to spend another night beaten and hungry…

Jerry

I have an idea that will keep them away for a long time.

Ella

What?

Jerry

Did you ever see one of those big earthmovers?

ten days later

private chat between Ella and Aunt Sue

Ella

Dear Aunt Sue. When Jerry’s dad dug up your sister’s body - my mom - at the crash site...

Ella

I knew your luck was changing.

Ella

Hank may think his badge and cop car make him invincible...

Ella

but when he forced my dad’s car into the ditch, and you buried mom and took her place...

Ella

you forgot that a new DNA test can now tell identical twins apart.

Ella

When you enter adult reform school today, just remember:

Ella

these will be the best 20 years of your life.

Ella

Too bad you don’t like orange.
