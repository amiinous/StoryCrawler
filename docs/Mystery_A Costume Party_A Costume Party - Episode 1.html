A Costume Party - Episode 1

Suri

Why did I agree to this?

Suri

I feel ridiculous with this mask on.

Elle

Everyone will be wearing a mask — that’s how costume parties work!

Suri

I swear, if I find out I’m the only one looking like I belong in a horror movie…

Elle

Relax. Meet me inside.

Elle

I promise *everyone* is in costume.

Elle

And you should see how he’s decorated the place!

Elle

Where are you??

Suri

Getting out of the car. OH MY GOD—

Suri

You didn’t tell me it was a mansion.

Elle

Wait ‘til you see the inside. Hurry up!

Suri

I’m at the door, but no one’s answering.

Elle

Are you sure I’m in the right place?

Elle

Of course you are. There’s a keypad to your right. Do you see it?

Elle

Put in the code — it’s 112233. The door will open.

Elle

Do you have the bag I told you to bring?

Suri

Yes, I have the bag. But isn’t it weird to just let myself in with the keypad?

Suri

Can’t you come and open the door?

Elle

It’s fine — everybody does it. The place is so big it’s easier this way.

Suri

Alright, I’m opening it now…

Suri

Why is it so dark?

Suri

I don’t see anyone. Where are you?

Elle

Head through the archway and you’ll see a sweeping staircase.

Elle

Go down it.

Suri

The front door just shut behind me!

Elle

It’s just the security system. Can you imagine how much everything must cost in here?

Suri

Well I can’t tell because it’s so dark!

Suri

Seriously, Elle, the only light I have is my phone. I’m heading for the staircase.

Suri

I don’t hear any music or see any decorations…

Suri

Where is everybody?

Suri

Where are you??

Elle

There’s a basement. That’s where you need to be.

Suri

I’m walking down the stairs.

Suri

This would be easier if someone would turn some lights on…

Suri

Or if I could take this mask off.

Elle

Don’t take the mask off!!

Suri

Why? I can’t see properly with it on!

Elle

The cameras will see your face if you do.

Suri

What? Why does that matter?

Elle

Look in front of you. Use the flashlight on your phone.

Suri

OMG what is this??

Suri

Elle, I don’t like this. I’m leaving.

Elle

I wouldn’t do that if I were you…

Elle

The door’s locked now.

Elle

You can’t leave.

Suri

What? Elle, something feels off. I seriously want to go.

Elle

Then you’ll be arrested for breaking and entering.

Elle

How are you going to explain being alone in a stranger’s house with a mask and gloves on?

Suri

What?? Why are you doing this?

Suri

Are you framing me or something? I thought we were friends.

Elle

We are. Look, they won’t let me explain now.

Elle

You just have to follow the instructions and no one will get hurt.

Suri

What are you talking about? Are you threatening me?

Elle

Suri, what do you see in front of you?

Suri

A long corridor…

Suri

There’s a big steel door in the wall at the end of it.

Suri

It looks like a bank vault. What am I doing here?

Elle

Look in the bag you brought for me.

Suri

What is this? An aerosol can?

Elle

Spray it in front of you.

Suri

Woah, now I can see all these red lines all over the place!

Elle

If you touch one, you’ll trigger the alarm.

Elle

So use the can to reveal the lasers and get to the vault as fast as you can.

Elle

We don’t have much time.

Suri

What do you mean, we don’t have much time?

Suri

I never agreed to do this.

Elle

You have to.

Suri

I don’t have to do anything!

Suri

This is crazy. Who does this house belong to?

Suri

Why are you trying to make me do this?

Elle

You need to see what’s in that vault.

Suri

I don’t care what’s in it. This is sick.

Elle

Let me show you something.

Elle sends Suri a picture of Suri's brother, Ben.

Suri

Ben! Why do you have a picture of my brother?

Elle

Why do you think?

Suri

Oh god. Is he…he’s not in the vault, is he?

Suri

Elle?

Suri

ELLE??

Suri

Okay, I’m spraying the can. I’ll go through the grid.

Suri

I have to put my phone in my pocket, but I’m doing it.

Suri

I said, I’m doing it, okay?

5 minutes later

Suri

I’m at the vault. What now?

Elle

You have to open it. But listen, you triggered the silent alarm.

Elle

That means the guy who owns the house knows you’re here.

Elle

He’s coming for you—

Elle

You have to hurry!

Suri

WHAT? What’s he going to do if he finds me here?

Elle

Focus, Suri. Please. You have to do this.

Suri

How am I supposed to get the vault open?

Elle

Keep calm. They have the code.

Suri

Who’s ‘they?’

Suri

How long do I have to wait?

Suri

I can’t be found here. I have to get Ben out!

A minute passes and Suri starts to sweat.

Elle

1203-1111

Suri frantically taps the numbers the keypad.

Suri

I’ve put the code in.

Suri

The vault door is opening.

Suri

I hear something upstairs! I think there’s a car pulling up!!

Suri

ELLE??!

Elle

It’s the guy who owns the place. He’s dangerous.

Suri

Wait, why is the vault empty?

Suri

Where’s my brother??

Elle

We never said your brother was in there.

Elle

You were the one who said that, remember?

Suri

Stop playing games with me. Is any of this real?

Elle

It’s very real. Two lives are in danger right now.

Suri

Whose lives are in danger? Mine and who else’s life?

Suri

If you hurt Ben…

Elle

Not Ben. Me.

Elle

My life is in danger.

Suri

What?! You’re the one making me do this!

Elle

I would never do this to you willingly. They’re making me do it.

Elle

Suri, there’s a gun to my head.

Suri

OMG—

Elle

I know. So look in the vault. Do you see a folder?

Elle

If you bring it to them, then they’ll let me go.

Suri

I see it! But wait…

Suri

I hear the front door opening upstairs!

Elle

Grab the folder and get out. Run!

Suri

I have it. What about the security system?

Elle

You have to get out before the front door closes. Run!

Elle

Suri?

Elle

SURI??

Elle

Please be there Suri!

Suri

I’m on the grounds!

Suri

I hid behind a tree, but Elle — he’s chasing me!

Elle

Are you close to your car?

Suri

Yeah.

Elle

RUN!

Elle

Text me when you’re safe.

Breathless, Suri races into her car, slamming the door behind her.

She presses the talk to text function on her screen.

Suri

Okay — in my car. I slammed my foot on the gas.

Elle

Did you get away??

Suri

I’m driving fast and didn’t put my lights on—

Suri

I don’t know if he’s following me.

Elle

Do you have the folder? Is it safe?

Suri

I have it. What’s in it?

Suri

Why is this important enough to risk our lives?

Elle

I’m sorry you got dragged into this…

Elle

I got mixed up with some people I shouldn’t have.

Elle

They smuggle drugs and they want to destroy a rival operation.

Elle

The papers are the contact details of everyone involved in the rival smuggling ring.

Elle

If you get away and drop the folder at the location I text you, they’ll let me go.

Suri receives a text of the coordinates. She punches them into her GPS.

Suri

I’m on my way, Elle. Hold on.

Ten frantic minutes later, Suri pulls down a long dirt track deep into a nameless patch of woods.

Suri

I’m here. It’s so dark…I don’t see you.

Suri

Elle??

Elle

They want you to drop the folder before they let me go.

Elle

Leave it on the tree stump in the clearing, okay?

Suri

No way! After the night I’ve had I’m not trusting anybody.

Elle

Please Suri. I just want this to end.

Suri

Fine. Let me gather up the papers.

Suri quickly takes photos of each of the papers with her phone.

Elle

Hurry!

5 minutes later

Suri

Okay. I put the folder where you said to leave it. Where are you?

Suri

Elle??

Elle lurches into the clearing as if shoved from behind.

Suri

I see you. Run toward my voice.

Suri shouts.

Suri

Run Elle!

Elle runs. Suri jumps in her car and starts the engine.

Elle throws herself into the passenger seat, out of breath and shaking.

Elle

I’m so sorry, Suri.

Suri

Don’t worry. I’m going to fix this.

Suri

They’re going to regret everything they’ve done—

Suri

Nobody gets away with treating us like this.

Suri hits the gas and the wheels spin dirt as the car speeds out of the woods.

Twenty minutes later, they reach Suri’s house.

Elle

What are you going to do?

Suri

End their operation.

Suri takes out her cell phone.

She looks up an email address and starts crafting a note.

And then she relishes in the swooshing noise she hears as she sends it—

Along with every picture from the folder—

To the capable hands of the police.
