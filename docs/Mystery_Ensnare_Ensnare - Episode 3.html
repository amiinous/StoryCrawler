Ensnare - Episode 3

Angie

They want me to become a member?

Kyle

Yes. They think you have a lot of potential.

Kyle

They love that you want to be a Supreme Court Justice. They’ll make that dream a reality.

Angie

Why? What do they get in return?

Kyle

Think of the Source like a network at a country club.

Kyle

Everyone helps each other out in the hope that the rest of the members will benefit.

Angie

So, it’s like a nepotism thing?

Kyle

No, it’s like a supportive community thing.

Angie

Sorry, but it sounds shady.

Angie

Who’s in this “secret society?”

Angie

And how can they guarantee anything?

Kyle

Lots of influential people.

Kyle

Politicians, businessmen, lawyers, doctors—

Kyle

Even some celebrities.

Angie

That still doesn’t explain why they think they can just MAKE me a Supreme Court Justice.

Angie

Not sure if you’re aware, but it’s a super hard thing to accomplish.

Kyle

Look, you can’t repeat this to anyone…

Kyle

But the Source has been practically running our entire government since the early 1900s.

Kyle

That’s when my grandpa got in, back when he was a senator.

Kyle

My family’s been a part of it ever since.

Angie

Kyle, this is a lot to take in. Can we back up?

Angie

What if I don’t want an abortion?

Kyle

They’ve made it very clear that if I want to be president someday…

Kyle

And if you want to be a Supreme Court Justice…

Kyle

We can’t have a baby this young.

Kyle

It’d completely change the way people would look at us politically.

Kyle

No one would take us seriously.

Angie

What if I don’t care how people look at me?

Kyle

Angie, please. You totally do. Besides—

Kyle

You’d have to put all of your dreams on hold to have this baby.

Kyle

You wouldn’t be able to go to college, let alone law school, for years—

Kyle

If you ever go at all.

Kyle

And that’s assuming your parents won’t cut you off financially.

Kyle

Chances are you wouldn’t be able to swing the tuition.

Angie

I didn’t think of that…

Kyle

Well, I can’t stop thinking about it.

Kyle

Let’s say you did go to college at some point.

Kyle

You’d have to raise our kid and hold down a steady job to support it—

Kyle

So you’d have to go to school part-time. It’d be exhausting.

Kyle

And you’d be setting yourself back by decades, at least.

Angie

You mean *we’d* be setting ourselves back by decades.

Angie

Or are you not as “in this with me” as you say you are?

Kyle

Angie, don’t be mad. You know that’s what I meant.

Kyle

I’m just trying to make you see that this decision has huge consequences.

Kyle

I don’t want you to make it lightly.

Kyle

Especially since you’ve worked so hard to maintain a 4.0 GPA and score almost-perfect SAT scores.

Kyle

AND you landed that summer internship at that law firm…

Angie

I know.

Kyle

Well, just know they won’t be happy with their ONE high school intern if she shows up pregnant.

Kyle

At that point, it wouldn’t even matter that she’s the daughter of the President.

Kyle

I know this may sound harsh, but I’m just trying to warn you…

Kyle

That’s not exactly the image they’ll want for their firm.

Angie

I know. You’re right.

Angie

So if I accepted the invitation from the Source…

Angie

What would I have to do as a member?

Angie

Is there like…a monthly meeting or something?

Kyle

No, nothing like that.

Kyle

Members rarely meet up in large group settings.

Kyle

It’s usually more one-on-one, for business purposes.

Angie

So…I’d just be a member, and they’d help me become a Supreme Court Justice?

Kyle

They do ask for favors from time to time.

Kyle

It’s one of those “you scratch my back, I scratch yours” kind of organizations.

Angie

What kind of favors?

Kyle

They’ll text you with more details.

Kyle

Just promise me you won’t shut them down right away.

Kyle

You haven’t been initiated yet, so you’re not committed to anything.

Kyle

I just don’t want you to close the door on your future before you consider all of your options.

Angie

Fine. I’ll hear them out.

At 9 am the next morning, Angie receives a text.

Unknown

Good morning, Angie.

Unknown

We trust Mr. Whitfield let you know we would be contacting you.

Angie

Is this the Source?

Unknown

I’m sure Mr. Whitfield explained that we ask all of our applicants to complete small tasks during the initiation process.

Unknown

Your willingness to accomplish these tasks allows us to judge your loyalty.

Unknown

Your ability to accomplish the tasks allows us to judge your ingenuity.

Unknown

Are you ready for your first task?

Angie hesitates, then remembers the promise she made to Kyle.

She won’t close the door on their future just yet.

Angie

Yes.

Unknown

We have been informed that Mr. Whitfield will be your date to the charity ball this evening.

Unknown

Senator Lynthrop is also going to be in attendance.

Unknown

We would like for you to flirt with the senator in a private, discreet location.

Unknown

Mr. Whitfield already has an area in mind.

Unknown

Mr. Whitfield will take photographic evidence of the encounter. He will then send it to us.

Angie

But Senator Lynthrop is in his 50’s.

Angie

And he’s married.

Angie

Why would you want to get pictures of him flirting with me?

Unknown

Recruitment.
