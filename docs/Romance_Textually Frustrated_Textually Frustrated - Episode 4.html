Textually Frustrated - Episode 4

Ember sits straight up in the bed.

Ember

What…what are you doing here?

Wren

I’m off work and I thought I’d keep you company. Is that okay?

Ember

Umm.

Wren frowns, motioning to the door.

Wren

I can go if you want.

Ember

No. Sorry. I’m just a little surprised. Come in.

Wren

How are you feeling?

Ember

I’m fine. I can’t believe you were here…thank you for the flowers.

Wren winces.

Wren

Was that creepy? I debated but came anyway because I want to make sure you were okay.

Ember

No, it’s not creepy. It’s really sweet.

Ember

I didn’t exactly give you the first impression I wanted to make though.

She chuckles, trying to ease the tension.

Wren

Don’t worry about that.

Wren

Are you hungry?

Wren moves toward Ember’s bedside, and Ember catches a glimpse of the box Wren’s holding in her other hand.

She opens the red-colored box.

Inside, there are a dozen chocolate-covered strawberries.

She holds one out to Ember and Ember accepts it.

Ember

Sure.

They spend a few minutes eating the dessert and tossing each other shy looks.

Ember

Those were good. Thanks for bringing them.

Wren

Anytime. I’m sure the food here gets boring.

Ember

Yup.

Wren smiles and Ember’s heart flutters. A sudden realization hits her.

Ember

You’re so much prettier in person.

Wren immediately blushes.

Ember

I don’t mean you’re ugly in your photos. You’re pretty in those too. I just mean…

She covers her face with her hands.

Ember

I’m screwing this up.

Wren laughs.

Wren

No, you’re not. I get what you mean.

Wren

And thank you. I think you’re gorgeous too.

Wren

Of course, you only showed me half your face — but even that was gorgeous.

Ember can still feel herself blushing.

Ember

Thank you.

Wren

So tell me everything about yourself. What do you like to do?

Wren

What’s your favorite animal? Do you like to travel?

Ember

Umm. I would love to travel but I’ve never had the chance. I’ve been sick for so long.

Ember

And my favorite animal is a bioluminescent octopus because they’re really smart, and I mean, come on—

Ember

They make their own light.

Wren’s eyebrows go up and Ember continues nervously chattering.

Ember

As for what I like to do…

Ember

I mindlessly flip through the TV stations, stare at the ceiling, prank girls who accidentally text me…

Ember laughs at herself, but Wren looks at her more carefully.

Wren

I mean, what do you like to do when you’re not in the hospital?

Ember

Try not to go back to the hospital.

Wren

Oh come on. You must have a hobby. Were you in school for anything?

Ember shrugs.

Ember

I was planning to go for oceanography before I got sick.

Wren

Oceanography. Cool. If you could travel anywhere, where would you go?

Ember

If I get better—

Wren

When you get better…

Ember

I’d like to go to Antarctica.

Wren screeches at the admission.

Wren

Seriously?

Ember

Well, yeah. It’s got the most interesting sea life.

Wren chuckles.

Wren

Most people would choose somewhere tropical where they can lie on the beach and relax—

Wren

But you chose the coldest continent on the planet.

Ember

I’ve never really been normal.

Wren

You’re quirky. I like it.

A comfortable silence settles between them.

Ember

So what about you? Do you travel a lot?

Wren

I have to pay off my school loans first. But I have a list.

Ember

A list? What’s on it?

Wren

Not Antarctica!

They both laugh.

Wren

I really like history, so I want to visit places with historical significance.

Wren

I’d like to imagine the story taking place while I stand on the ground where it happened.

Ember smiles at Wren, completely in awe.

A warm and fuzzy feeling fills her chest.

Is this really happening to her?

Is she really meeting the woman of her dreams?

Ember

That’s really neat.

They both awkwardly glance at each other and then look away again.

Ember feels her heart swell but then reality hits—

Her smile fades.

Ember

I’m not sure you should be here.

Wren

It’s still visiting hours.

Ember

No, I mean…for your sake.

Ember

You know how sick I am. I might not…

A lump forms in her throat.

Ember

I might not get better.

Wren

Are you warning me away because of your health condition?

Ember

Basically.

Wren

Stop that! I know what I’m getting into.

Wren

And I know the risks involved with your condition. I wouldn’t be here if I was worried about that.

Ember

Yeah but—

Wren reaches out and pushes a lock of Ember’s hair behind her ear. Goosebumps erupt on Ember’s arms.

Wren

You just worry about yourself.

She gazes into Ember’s eyes.

Wren

Let someone like you. Let someone take care of you.

Ember is hypnotized by Wren’s warm brown eyes. She whispers in response.

Ember

Okay.

And for the first time in a long time, Ember feels a flutter of hope.
