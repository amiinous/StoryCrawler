Banging on the Door

Angie

Was that you?

Joey

Was what me?

Angie

There was a banging on the door.

Joey

I’m getting out of work now.

Angie

Why now?

Joey

Some weird woman came in asking for a book.

Angie

You kept the bookstore open for one customer?

Joey

Let’s not do this.

Angie

You rather stay at that stupid place then spend time with me.

Joey

It’s my job. If I get one more complaint, I’m fired.

Angie

So I guess I have to sit here in the dark with the creepy sounds.

Joey

What sounds?

Angie

There was a banging on the front door.

Joey

Did you see who it was?

Angie

It didn’t sound like a knock. It was loud.

Joey

So you didn’t check?

Angie

Definitely not.

Joey

Expecting a package?

Angie

Ten o’clock at night?

Joey

True. I prefer creepy sounds over weird ladies any day.

Angie

The woman at the store?

Joey

Yeah.

Angie

What did she do?

Joey

She was asking for some book.

Angie

What’s weird about that?

Joey

She was mumbling to herself. And she kept looking behind her.

Angie

She sounds harmless.

Joey

I thought so. Until I found the book she wanted.

Joey

It was some witchcraft book filled with spells.

Angie

Neat.

Joey

It actually wasn’t.

Angie

Did she ask if you had any small animals for sale?

Joey

When I was checking the book out, I read the inside. The title read WIND SPIRIT.

Angie

What does that mean?

Joey

How would I know?

Angie

Where are you?

Joey

I’m getting on my bike now.

Angie

I don’t want to be that kind of girl but lately with what’s going on between us, I feel like I don’t have any choice.

Angie

I know that you work constantly because you’re paying off for school. I get it.

Angie

But you’re taking a week off at the end of the month and you decided that five out of the seven days, you’re going to go camping with your buddies.

Joey

I need the time to relax.

Angie

Relax?

Joey

YES! No responsibilities, no stress, no nagging people around me.

Angie

I see. So I get two days.

Joey

Angie, I see you after work.

Angie

After dinner, you pass out on the couch. My parents only let you crash here because they know how tired you are.

Joey

This camping trip is really going to give me a second wind for the year.

Joey

It will be low key.

Angie

Oh of course. Becausethe beer drinking, girl gossiping and dirt biking are really low key activities.

Joey

I’ll make it up to you.

Angie

No, you won’t.

Joey

I promise.

Joey

When I get back, we’ll go to dinner.

Joey

No?

Joey

Fine. How about we take a trip ourselves?

Angie

If that’s you it’s not funny!

Joey

What are you talking about?

Angie

The banging!

Joey

I’m like five minutes away.

Angie

Are you lying?

Joey

No.

Joey

Where are your parents?

Angie

They went out for dinner.

Joey

Hang tight, okay?

Angie

Please hurry.

Joey

I’m going as fast as I can.

Angie

Should I check the door?

Joey

No!

Angie

I’m going to the kitchen.

Joey

Is the banging still going on?

Angie

It stopped.

Joey

It’s windy out here. Perhaps it’s nothing.

Angie

It sounded like someone was trying to break in.

Joey

It’s hard to bike and type at the same time. I’m going to call you.

Joey

Angie, pick up.

Joey

Angie?

Joey

HELLO?

Angie

They broke into the house!

Joey

What?

Angie

I can’t pick up. They’ll hear the ringing.

Joey

Where are you?

Angie

I’m underneath the table.

Joey

What happened?

Angie

The door swung open and I heard a rush coming in.

Angie

The closest hiding spot was the table.

Joey

I’m two blocks away.

Angie

Joey, the furniture is moving.

Joey

What?

Angie

Whoever it is, they are pushing the furniture around.

Joey

I’m almost there.

Angie

But I don’t see them.

Joey

What do you mean?

Angie

I only see the furniture moving.

Joey

Don’t make a sound.

Angie

I’m trying not to.

Angie

Joey, I think they are moving closer.

Angie

I feel them around the table.

Angie

The table is moving!

Angie

Hurry Joey!

Angie

They left and went upstairs.

Angie

Joey?

Joey

Angie, are you okay?

Angie

I can hear them moving things around upstairs.

Joey

I sprained my ankle.

Angie

How?

Joey

I fell off my bike. This gust of wind pushed me over.

Angie

Are you okay?

Joey

I can barely ride. I’m a block away.

Angie

The door is wide open.

Joey

Can you still hear them upstairs?

Angie

No.

Joey

Stay put.

Angie

I’m scared.

Joey

I’m almost there.

Angie

I heard something upstairs.

Joey

I see your house.

Angie

I think I can make a run for it.

Joey

Don’t risk it.

Angie

I can make it.

Joey

I’m coming up on the driveway.

Angie

I’m running!

Joey

Wait!

Joey

Angie, did you get out?

Joey

I’m standing in front of the door.

Joey

Angie, please respond.

Joey

I’m walking in.

Joey

I don’t see you. Everything is a mess.

Joey

I’m in the living area.

Joey

I’m in the kitchen.

Joey

I’m back in the living area.

Joey

Are you upstairs?

Joey

I heard a buzzing.

Joey

Is that you?

Joey

Is that your phone?

Joey

I’m going to follow the sound upstairs.

Joey

Where are you?

Joey

Is that you in the bedroom making the noises?

Joey

Did you hear me call for you?

Joey

I’m going inside.

Joey

Angie, the bedroom is empty. The whole house is empty.

Joey

Where are you?

Angie

I’m downstairs.

Joey

Angie, I was so worried. Where are you?

Angie

Come down.

Joey

I’m on my way.

Joey

I don’t see you.

Angie

I’m here.

Joey

Where?

Angie

Can you not hear my voice?

Joey

I don’t hear anything.

Angie

I’m standing right in front of you!

Joey

I don’t see you.

Angie

Oh my.

Joey

What is it?

Angie

I can’t see myself either.

Joey

What do you mean?

Angie

I’m floating.

Joey

We have to find help.

Angie

I’m moving away.

Joey

Where? Don’t leave.

Angie

I can’t control it.

Joey

Tell me exactly where you are?

Joey

Angie, we’ll fix this. And when you’re better, we’ll go on that trip.

Joey

Was that gust of wind you?

Joey

Angie, come back.
