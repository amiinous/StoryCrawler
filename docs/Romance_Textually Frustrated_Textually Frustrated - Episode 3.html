Textually Frustrated - Episode 3

With tears in her eyes, Ember sends another text.

Ember

It’s okay. We barely know each other, Wren.

Ember

You don’t owe me anything.

There’s another long pause.

Trying to hold back her tears, Ember puts her phone down on the table.

The aide from lunch the other day walks in. She takes one look at Ember then rushes to her side.

Aide

What’s wrong, honey?

Ember notices her nametag.

Ember

Your name is Amber?

Amber

Yes.

Ember

Huh, that’s almost my name. Weird.

Amber shrugs.

Amber

Or it's a sign. Now why did you look so upset?

Ember

It’s nothing. Just worried.

Amber reads her chart and then sighs.

Amber

Rough night, huh?

Ember nods.

Amber

What do you say I get a wheelchair and we take a little walk?

Amber

For a change of scenery.

Ember

That sounds perfect.

Amber leaves, and then returns with a wheelchair. She helps Ember into it.

Once situated, Ember grasps the rolling IV stand beside her as Amber unlocks the wheels.

Ember

Have you worked here long?

Amber pushes her out into the hallway.

Amber

About a year.

Ember

It must be hard.

Amber

Oh please. The patients are the ones having a hard time. I’m just here to try to make them more comfortable.

Ember

You must see a lot of people come and go.

Ember swallows hard, her mind turning over the idea of people going — of death.

Amber

I see a lot of people leave healthier than when they came in.

Amber

A lot of people who almost die here, but then they get up and walk out the door a week or two later.

Amber

To me, that’s inspiring — not hard.

She gently places a hand on Ember’s shoulder. Ember can’t speak.

They continue to stroll through the hallways. Ember spots the sign for the Emergency Room.

Ember

Hey, do you think we can go there?

Amber

To the ER?

Ember

Just to the lobby. I don’t want to get in anyone’s way.

Amber is quiet for a moment. Ember cranes her neck to look her in the eye.

Amber

Does this have something to do with your secret crush from the other day?

Ember is at a loss for words.

Ember

Maybe?

Amber smiles.

Amber

I know a back entrance.

Ember

But I don’t…I don’t want to be seen.

Amber

Don’t worry. I’ve got you covered.

She pushes the wheelchair toward an “Employees Only” door and places a face mask on Ember’s lap.

Flushed with excitement and nerves, Ember picks it up and puts it over her mouth and nose.

Amber

I’ll get you in if you tell me who we’re stalking — deal?

Ember

Deal.

As they wheel through a series of hallways, Ember takes a deep breath and summons all her courage.

She lifts the face mask away from her mouth and tells her story.

Ember

It started off as a wrong number text that I responded to because I was awake and bored — but I ended up really liking her.

Ember

And she works here. In the ER. It’s just…

Amber

What?

Ember

I’m just…scared. What if I’m leading her on?

Ember

It seems cruel.

Amber

Wait, why would you be leading her on?

Ember

Come on, Amber, look at me.

Ember gestures to her wheelchair and IV.

Amber

Because you’re sick?

Ember

You know I’m not just sick. I might not make it.

Ember

This has been my life the past few years — this wheelchair, these walls.

Ember

I’m not going to subject someone to this. I’ve already seen what it’s done to my mom.

Amber is silent for a moment. She clears her throat, looking directly in Ember’s eyes.

Amber

I want to share something with you.

Amber

Five years ago, I lost my dad to cancer.

Amber

And if I could only remember one thing he taught me, it’d be how to be brave.

Ember

What do you mean?

Amber

He lived each moment to its fullest potential.

Amber

He never gave up or acted like he was dying.

Amber

And when he did die, it was after a full life with no regrets.

As they enter the Emergency Department from the employee entrance, Ember lets the words turn through her mind.

She’s been acting as if she were dying.

Would she regret that some day?

Amber

So who are we spying on?

Ember

Her name is Wren. She’s a nurse. I think she’s working today.

Amber

Hmm. What does she look like?

Ember scrolls through her phone for one of the pics Wren sent her. She holds it up for Amber.

Amber

Pretty.

She creeps the wheelchair forward, staying slightly on one side of the hallway and behind a half-closed door.

They aren’t exactly hiding…but they’re not out in the open either.

Ember puts her face mask back on.

Ember

I don’t see her.

Amber

Three o’clock. Behind the desk. Is that her?

Ember looks. She spots a head full of short blonde hair.

The woman is laughing with co-workers. Her eyes are bright, and her smile wide and carefree.

Ember

Yes. I think so.

She grins under her mask.

Amber

She seems fun. It looks like her co-workers like her.

Ember

Yeah.

Ember starts to wonder if SHE’LL be likeable enough for Wren.

She’s overwhelmed with doubt.

Ember

Maybe I shouldn’t be here.

Ember

I don’t have much experience dating.

Amber

You don’t need experience.

Ember

What?

Amber

You don’t — you already have the basics.

Amber

Are you a good person? Yes.

Amber

Kind and funny? Yes.

Amber

So don’t worry about experience—

Amber

It just creates baggage anyway. You’re a pure soul. She’s gonna love you.

Ember feels heat creep to her cheeks.

Amber

But we better go before we get caught.

Amber starts to pull away Ember’s wheelchair.

Ember gives one last look at Wren, wishing she were braver.

After helping her get situated in bed, Amber leaves Ember in her room to check on other patients.

For the rest of the afternoon, staff come and go while Ember waits to hear from Wren.

Three hours later, Ember wakes up from a light doze when her phone pings.

Wren

What’s your favorite kind of dessert?

Ember

Umm…chocolate-covered strawberries?

She waits for more but nothing happens.

Ember

Why?

There’s still no text in return.

Frustrated, Ember turns on the TV and vegs out, wondering…

Is Wren is purposefully ignoring her or just busy?

She’s tempted to text her again when there’s a knock on the door.

Ember

Come in.

The door opens and a blonde-haired girl with a sunny smile walks in.

The breath catches in Ember’s throat.

Wren awkwardly waves with one hand.

Wren

Hi.

Ember

Hi.

And then Wren walks closer…
