V-Card - Part 7

A knock sounded at the door.

I reached for my little silver, beaded clutch and slipped my cellphone and wallet inside.

Chloe nodded in approval, so I guess it was a good choice with the dress.

Kinsley swung the door open to reveal Luke on the other side.

Luke

Christian went to get the car.

Luke

He’ll be pulling up out front any second.

Luke

Oh, Kinsley, Mr. Wonderful is here.

Kinsley jumped up at that and raced past Luke and down the stairs.

I could hear her squeal and jump into her boyfriend, Aaron’s, arms.

Chloe

Ugh, gag.

Chloe shoved the clothes littering her bed aside and lay across the mattress.

Reaching for the container of assorted nail polish on her nightstand, she went about choosing a color and forgot all about me and Luke.

His eyebrows shot up as he noticed my getup.

Luke

Wow.

Luke

You look … stunning.

I ran a hand through my hair, which was now falling in soft waves thanks to the curlers.

Jennifer

You don’t think it’s too much?

Luke flashed that boyish grin.

Luke

I think it’s just right.

Luke

Too bad you’re wasting it on Christian.

Jennifer

That’s what Chloe said.

Luke snorted.

Luke

Yeah, we’ve all been living together too long.

A horn beeped outside—Christian pulling up out front.

Jennifer

Geesh, whatever happened to coming to the door for your date?

I grumbled as I passed through the doorway and into the hall.

Luke followed, his hand resting on the small of my back.

Kinsley and Aaron were snuggling on the couch and oblivious to everything else, so I didn’t bother to say good-bye.

Luke

Fake date, no chivalry.

Jennifer

Still, I’m doing him a favor.

I reached for the door, but his arm brushed mine as he stretched it out to grasp the knob before me.

He swung it open and offered me the crook of his arm.

Luke

Milady.

He said it in a terrible British accent.

Luke

Thy chariot awaiteth.

Taking his arm, I giggled and let him lead me out into the front yard.

Jennifer

Awaiteth is not a word.

Luke

It is tonight.

We walked down the front steps, steadying me as I wobbled on Chloe’s heels.

Luke

Are you going to make it through the party on those stilts?

I gritted my teeth and forced myself to walk with grace and a slight swing of the hips.

I was not going to fall in these heels, I wasn’t!

Jennifer

How hard can it be?

Considering that the only heels I’m comfortable in are wedges—and even then only moderately so—it was going to be a taller order than I let on.

Christian hung his head out the window of his black Audi as we approached the curb and whistled.

Luke

Right?

Luke

She almost looks hot.

I elbowed him in the ribs and he doubled over.

Jennifer

Jerk.

Luke

Have fun, kids.

He clutched his middle as Christian got out to open the passenger door for me.

Luke

Chris, have her back by midnight or you’ll turn into a pumpkin.

We both waved good-bye before Christian peeled off from the curb.

Fallout Boy blasted through the speakers as he merged onto I-35.

The night was coming alive, and soon downtown would be clogged with students headed to 6th Street to blow off a little steam.

We bypassed downtown and headed west, putting the bustling city behind us.

Half an hour later, we arrived in what I think is the most beautiful part of Austin.

If you live downtown, you could never know Austin could be so green or scenic.

With the sun setting, the rocky ledges on either side of us were glowing red and orange, kissed by a pink glow.

Then we came out into the neighborhood Christian had grown up in.

The houses sprawled on large plots of land, with the lake running behind them.

Boats floated in boat garages and docks jutted out from people’s backyards.

As we drove down the winding road and descended into Rich-ville, Christian turned to me.

Christian

Thanks for doing this.

Christian

I’d have asked Chloe, but …

He trailed off and I nodded in understanding.

Chloe didn’t have the tact to deal with Christian’s mother; she was too outspoken.

I was perfect for Christian’s fake girlfriend: pretty, but not too pretty; outgoing, but not outspoken; smart, but not a know-it-all.

Jennifer

It’s cool.
