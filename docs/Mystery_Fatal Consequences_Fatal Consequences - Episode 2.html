Fatal Consequences - Episode 2

48 hours earlier.

Client

Where are you?

Forrester

Almost there. Don’t worry—

Forrester

Once I’ve got the recording, I’ll tie up the loose ends.

Client

You’re certain you can take care of this girl?

Forrester

Let’s just say the senator’s favorite dominatrix won’t be flogging any more bottoms.

Client

Pity. It put him in such a good mood.

Forrester

Guess a happy senator is a submissive senator?

Client

And vice-versa.

Forrester

Cute.

Client

Of course, once he sees the instant replay he’ll never be happy again.

Client

There won’t be any trace of her?

Forrester

None. I’ll sanitize everything. It’ll be like she never existed.

Client

Perfect.

A few miles away, at Arlo’s Goodnight Diner, Jo-Jo has been laying low and working as a waitress.

Arlo

Jo-Jo, Lizzy’s running late again. Can you handle one more before you split?

Jo-Jo

Sure thing, Boss.

She turns to the tattooed man who just walked in.

Jo-Jo

Have a seat anywhere, mister.

Forrester

Thanks.

Jo-Jo

Can I get you a cup of coffee?

Forrester

That’d be great.

Jo-Jo

I recommend tonight’s special. Arlo’s meatloaf is a party in your mouth.

Forrester

Hmm. Comes with mashed potatoes. Can I have fries instead?

Jo-Jo

Of course.

Forrester

You know in France, French fries are called pommes frites? “Fried apples.”

Jo-Jo instantly recognizes the signal.

She glances up as the bell over the door chimes as Lizzy walks in.

Then she looks back down, studying the tattooed man in front of her.

Jo-Jo

Forrester?

Forrester

One and the same.

Jo-Jo

Nice to finally put a face to the name.

Jo-Jo

We’ve done quite a few of these gigs together, haven’t we?

Forrester

That we have. You got the recording?

Jo-Jo

Not here.

Forrester

Close by?

Jo-Jo

Close enough. You have the funds?

Forrester

I could have wired them to your offshore account.

Jo-Jo

Wires have a way of getting crossed.

Forrester

Not taking any chances, huh?

Jo-Jo

Not if I can help it. Make an excuse to leave. I’ll meet you in the parking lot.

Forrester spits his coffee back into his cup.

Forrester

Is this from FUCKING YESTERDAY?!

Jo-Jo

I’m sorry, sir, let me get you another cup.

Arlo

Is there a problem here?

Forrester

Yeah, this dumb-ass bitch just served me lukewarm turd water.

Arlo

Sir, I think you need to leave.

Forrester

I’m not going anywhere. Haven’t you ever heard that the customer is always right?!

Arlo

Hell with that! This is my place and I reserve the right to refuse anyone who pisses me off.

Arlo

Consider yourself refused, pal. Out!

Forrester dumps his coffee on the floor, splattering the liquid onto Jo-Jo’s shoes.

Forrester

Guess your shift just got a little longer with that cleanup duty.

Arlo

I asked you to leave. If you’re not out the door in 10 seconds I’ll mop the floor with your head.

Forrester exits. Arlo makes a step to follow, but Jo-Jo pulls him back.

Jo-Jo

Leave him alone, Arlo. It’s not worth it.

Arlo

Yeah, I guess.

As Jo-Jo wipes up the mess, a chill runs through her.

Something definitely feels off. Forrester’s reaction felt over the top.

Why did he have to spill coffee on her?

Jo-Jo

Hey Boss, I’m gonna head out.

Arlo

You want me to walk you to your car?

Jo-Jo

Nah, that jerk-off’s halfway to Wyoming by now. See you tomorrow.

Arlo

Mañana.

In the kitchen, Jo-Jo makes a split-second decision.

She unhooks her pendant — a gold cross camouflaging the USB with the recording—

And she buries it in the freezer. Then she grabs her leftovers, and heads out to the parking lot.

Forrester

Is that it?

Jo-Jo

This? No, it’s meatloaf.

Forrester

You’re shitting me.

Jo-Jo

Nope. Really is a party in your mouth.

Forrester

Let’s get going.

Jo-Jo

Everything okay?

Forrester

Why?

Jo-Jo

That scene was a little intense.

Jo-Jo

You didn’t have to dump your coffee on my shoes.

Forrester

Sorry. I was trying to sell it.

Jo-Jo

Forget it. You have the payment or what?

Forrester

It’s in my bag.

Jo-Jo

Show me.

Forrester

Not here. In the car. Which one’s yours?

Jo-Jo

That old Volvo.

Forrester

Get in.

Jo-Jo

The money?

Forrester opens his bag, pulls out a gun, and points it at Jo-Jo.

Forrester

I’ve got a better idea. You give me the recording or I blow your brains out.

Jo-Jo

You’ll never find it if you do.

Forrester

Maybe not, but I think you’ll give it to me. I can be very persuasive.

Jo-Jo

It’s not on me. But I can take you to where it’s hidden…

Forrester

Then what are you waiting for? Drive!

Jo-Jo reaches toward the seat.

Forrester

Hey, what’re you doing?

Jo-Jo

Buckling up. It’s the law.

Forrester smirks. He buckles up as well.

Forrester

What? You can do it and I can’t?

15 minutes later, Jo-Jo feels exhausted. Her mind’s been working overtime as she drives up a winding mountain road.

Not knowing where else to go, she leads them toward the cut-off for Grange Ravine.

Forrester

How much further?

Jo-Jo

Half a mile.

Forrester

You could just tell me where it is.

Jo-Jo

So you can shoot me sooner rather than later?

Forrester

Quick and painless or slow and agonizing — your call.

Jo-Jo

It’s in an abandoned deer stand. I doubt you’d find it, much less be able to haul your fat ass up there.

Forrester

I should just off you right now.

Jo-Jo

You know, I thought you might pull something like this.

Jo-Jo

I made a copy for insurance. If anything happens to me…

Forrester

Aw, c’mon, Jo. Nice try, but that’s not your M.O.

Jo-Jo

Maybe I’ve changed.

Forrester

Doubtful. Granted, your rules are your own, but if I learned anything from working with you, it’s that you’re strictly by the book.

Jo-Jo

You were testing me?

Forrester

Unfortunately, you passed. You signed your own death warrant, kiddo.

Jo-Jo

Killing me was part of the deal?

Forrester

No loose ends. Ironically, our head honcho still thinks I’m delivering the goods as promised.

Jo-Jo

What?

Forrester

Much as the Client wants your little BDSM scene, I’m guessing the senator will want it more — and he’ll be willing to pay for it.

Jo-Jo

A bidding war?

Forrester

And may the one with the deepest pockets win.

Jo-Jo

You’re double-crossing both of us?

Forrester

Sad but true.

Forrester

We there yet or what? There doesn’t seem to be much more to this road.

Jo-Jo

Nope. This is the end.

Jo-Jo

And if I’m gonna die, I’m taking you with me.

Jo-Jo steps on the gas and grabs for the gun.

In the struggle, she manages to unclip Forrester’s seat belt as the car plows through the guardrail.

Jo-Jo

See you in hell, Forrester!
